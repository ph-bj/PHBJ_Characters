Precious Mirror for Grading Flowers
Chapter 28
**At parting, hidden words are sent through the Morning Glory;
blinded by greed, one learns petty and crooked tricks.**
Word came that after his beating,
Changqing was beside himself.
He had no choice
but to beseech Ye Maolin once more,
and together they made their way
to Secretary Wei’s study in the Young Master Hua mansion.
There they offered thorns on their bare backs to beg pardon,
pledging to hand the boy over immediately without taking a single string of cash.
Seeing him so meek and compliant,
and refusing all purchase money, Pincai swelled with triumph.
He thought his earlier fit of anger had successfully shamed them into delivering the goods directly to his door, exactly as he had predicted.
He then sought out Lin Shanzhi, had the Young Master called,
and made his report.
Young Master Hua asked, 'Why ask no price?'
Pincai replied, 'First, he fears the child may not know the proper rules;
second, if there is any fault
and your lordship wearies of him,
he can still quietly take him back.
Thus, he dares not accept the money.'
The Young Master nodded.
'That will do.
He may enter tomorrow.
But once he is part of my household, whether a price was paid or not,
he shall not be permitted
to drink wine or sing in the outside world.'
Pincai readily agreed, 'Of course.
How many heads could Changqing possibly have
to dare such a thing?'
Young Master Hua then instructed Shanzhi, 'Tell the accounts office
to allocate two hundred taels of silver a month to Changqing,
and have him draw it at the mansion monthly.'
Shanzhi assented, departed with Pincai,
and upon meeting Changqing, explained everything.
Pincai piled on the supposed favors he had done, leaving Changqing overjoyed.
He kowtowed his profound thanks
to Pincai and took his leave.
Returning home, he informed Qinyan of the arrangement.
At this juncture, Qinyan knew there was no escape.
This disaster had arisen within his own walls,
yet Ziyu remained completely unaware.
Tomorrow, he would enter the Hua mansion,
and who could say
when they might ever see each other again?
He longed to bid Ziyu a proper farewell,
yet he feared a meeting would only shatter them both;
moreover, there were too many prying eyes and insurmountable obstacles.
He wanted to write a letter,
but his mind was a tangled knot of sorrow.
Ten thousand words could not find their way to the brush,
so he could only entreat Sulan
to convey his innermost feelings.
After much thought, he took the silk handkerchief he always used
to dry his tears, selected four medicinal herbs and wrapped them separately, enclosed them in the handkerchief, sealed the entire bundle in an outer paper,
and entrusted it to Sulan,
imploring her to place it directly into Ziyu's hands when she saw him.
The next morning, Changqing delivered Qinyan to the Hua mansion.
The Young Master scrutinized him again in fine detail, found himself exceedingly pleased,
and assigned him to attend
to them at the Quiet-Green Retreat.
Qinyan was also led in to kowtow before Lady Hua.
Observing his frail, graceful form
and noting the complete absence of any vulgar actor's air,
she uttered a single word of approval:
'Good.'
As for the Young Master's satisfaction,
it goes without saying.
Yet Qinyan's heart remained anchored to Ziyu;
he could only weep in secret
and sigh softly when no one was looking.
After a few days,
observing that the Young Master’s temper was quite proper
and devoid of any indecent pestering, his mind eased slightly.
However, toward Wei Pincai,
he maintained the stony silence of Lady Xi of old.
Pincai found himself utterly powerless;
any malicious design to 'settle' the boy was,
for the moment, entirely out of the question.
As for the items Qinyan had left behind at parting,
which were entrusted to Sulan
to give to Ziyu, Sulan planned to invite Ziyu over in a few days to speak with him heart to heart.
Now concerning Ziyu:
ever since that single,
fleeting conversation with Qinyan in the fifth month,
it was not that he hadn't tried to visit him,
but countless misunderstandings had intervened.
One cool, pleasant day, after his morning meal, Ziyu made his way to Sulan’s place.
He first had Yun inquire if she was home;
upon hearing this, Sulan was delighted
and hurried out to welcome him in.
However, two others emerged from the room:
Ziyu recognized one as Wang Lanbao,
and the other as Qiguan.
Not having seen Qiguan for some time, Ziyu took a closer look.
His face was as smooth as almond paste, his willow brows arched like verdant peaks.
Radiant and gracefully slender,
he appeared about the same age as Sulan,
though slightly smaller in stature.
Ziyu stepped forward and greeted them.
Ziyu said, 'I truly did not expect Xiangwan
to be hosting such distinguished guests today.'
Lanbao teased, 'This is your little sister-in-law.
Have you two paid your familial respects yet?'
Ziyu replied, 'What kind of talk is this?
Where does such a title come from?'
Sulan chimed in, 'Actually, the title makes some sense.'
Qiguan felt embarrassed and protested, 'Jingfang, please don't joke.'
Lanbao countered, 'It hardly counts as a joke.
You are Yunong's junior apprentice-brother, does
that not make you his little sister-in-law?'
Ziyu laughed, 'That is utterly absurd.'
With that, they all took their seats.
Noticing the table was strewn with cups
and plates from a recent meal, Sulan had the servants clear it away.
She then personally brought Ziyu a bowl of tea
and asked, 'Your visit today is quite a coincidence;
you must have already heard the news.'
Ziyu listened with bewilderment
and asked, 'What news should I have heard?
I genuinely do not know.'
Lanbao stared at Ziyu and said, 'You really don't know?
It has already been five or six days.
Even if you haven't left the house, has truly no one told you?'
Ziyu grew even more baffled, the thought never crossing his mind
that this might concern Qinyan.
He said, 'I honestly have no idea what you are talking about.
I haven't stepped past my front gates,
and no one has visited me these past two days.
How could I know of matters in the outside world?'
Qiguan offered a small smile,
while Sulan said, 'Since you truly don't know,
I have no choice but to tell you.
But brace yourself.
Jingfang, Yuyan, you two support him while I explain.'
Ziyu replied, 'Xiangwan, you have always been straightforward.
Why adopt these dramatic airs today?
Surely it cannot be anything so astonishing;
you are merely trying to startle me.'
Sulan looked at Ziyu again and again,
which provoked laughter from Lanbao and Qiguan.
Observing their behavior, Ziyu's suspicions grew.
'Xiangwan, please, just speak,' he urged.
Sulan hesitated, 'It is somewhat difficult to say.
But showing you a certain item will make it clear.'
Ziyu still had no inkling of what was happening.
He watched as Sulan retrieved a paper bundle from a small visiting-card box.
It looked like a sealed letter,
but the label bore no writing,
and the shape was square.
Taking it in his hands,
it felt practically weightless;
pressing it gently, it yielded softly,
as though something was folded inside.
Tearing away the paper seal,
he found a piece of white silk, resembling a handkerchief.
His doubts intensifying, he gave it a shake,
and four smaller paper packets tumbled out.
Lanbao and the others stepped closer to look.
Ziyu opened the packets and spread them on the table;
they contained four distinct medicinal herbs, none of which he recognized.
Sulan asked, 'What medicines are these?'
Ziyu replied, 'I do not know.
Let me ask you:
what is the meaning of showing this to me?
And how is it that you yourself don't know?'
The three remained silent, their eyes fixed on the packets of herbs.
Seeing their apparent confusion, Ziyu's bewilderment deepened.
He asked Sulan, 'Whose belongings are these, really?
You are all speaking in riddles.'
Sulan answered, 'I am not the one giving this to you.
The one you dream of day
and night left this for you upon his departure,
instructing me to pass it on.
I assumed it was something of great importance, little knowing he had simply wrapped up the medicine he takes daily.
He believed you would recognize this handkerchief,
hoping that seeing the object would stir your memory of him.'
Hearing this, Ziyu's heart gave a violent lurch.
He stared at the silk handkerchief, a dreadful thought forming:
'From the way she speaks, has something terrible happened to Yunong?
It sounds like an ill omen.'
With this grim realization, a surge of bitter sorrow rose from his heart, flooded his senses,
and gathered at his eyes
and nose until it became an unstoppable torrent of tears.
The three onlookers sighed in unison.
Witnessing their reaction, Ziyu dared not ask further, feeling as
though the tragic truth was already clear.
He wiped his face with the handkerchief,
thinking, 'These must be the medicines he took on his deathbed, sent for me to see.'
A sensation of ten thousand arrows piercing his heart overwhelmed him.
At a loss for what to do,
he stood up and moved to the outer room, desperate to wail aloud.
Yet, finding it inappropriate to do so in Sulan's home,
he merely covered his face,
sobbing in a daze.
Sulan, seeing his extreme distress, regretted her rashness.
Realizing her words had made it sound as though Yunong were dead,
she hurried out to Ziyu
and explained, 'Please, don't be so anxious.
Let me finish.
Yunong is fine.
Come sit inside, and I will explain everything in detail.'
Ziyu, hearing this, grew frantic.
'Xiangwan, if you have something to say, say it directly!
Stop speaking in half-truths and terrifying people.
What exactly has happened to Yunong?'
He stepped back into the inner room.
Lanbao and Qiguan looked at him with deep sympathy.
Sulan began, 'Listen carefully to what transpired this fifth month.'
She then recounted the entire ordeal:
how Wei Pincai had schemed, how Xi Eleven had caused a chaotic scene, how she
and Lanbao had tried to intervene and devise a plan, how Qinyan had hidden from Xi Eleven,
and finally, how he was sent into the Hua mansion.
She detailed his tearful parting instructions, his refusal of the purchase price,
and the possibility of him requesting leave to come out.
She went over every detail meticulously,
offering comforting words throughout.
Hearing that Qinyan still remained in the realm of the living, Ziyu's heart eased a fraction.
He paused, then said, 'With Yunong gone there,
it is as though he has vanished from the mortal dust.'
Fresh tears cascaded down his cheeks.
After a long moment of stunned silence,
he examined the medicinal herbs once more.
Recognizing only the peony root,
he turned to Sulan, 'Yunong sending these herbs must carry a profound, hidden meaning.
But I do not know what they are.
Could you have someone take them to the apothecary to inquire,
and write their names on the packets?'
Sulan agreed, 'A sensible idea,'
and was about to call for a servant.
Qiguan interrupted, 'No need.
The servant who accompanied me will recognize them;
he used to work in an apothecary.'
Qiguan summoned the man and showed him the four herbs.
The man inspected them
and stated, 'This is morning-glory (Qianniu), this is angelica (Duhuo), this is peony (Shaoyao),
and this is stephania (Fangji).'
Qiguan took a brush and wrote the names down,
but could not decipher the riddle.
Sulan offered, 'He has been separated from you,
so he is now "living alone" (Duhuo).
I understand that one.'
Lanbao added, 'Fangji means
to "guard oneself," telling you to rest assured.
But I truly cannot fathom the other two.'
Ziyu, tears shimmering in his eyes, said softly, 'Yunong's entire heart is revealed here.
This peony, Shaoyao, is also known as Jiangli,
meaning "about to part."
It signifies that by entering the Hua mansion,
he is already parted from me.
Since we are parted,
he is naturally "living alone" (Duhuo).
Living alone in the Hua mansion, how can he simply drift along with the crowd?
Naturally, he must be extremely cautious, constantly on guard,
protecting himself (Fangji), keeping his body as pure as jade.
As for the morning-glory, Qianniu, there can be no other interpretation.
It must mean he will return on the Seventh day of the Seventh Month to meet me.
It represents the reunion of the Weaver Girl and the Cowherd (Qianniu).'
Sulan exclaimed, 'Exactly!
Brilliant!
You have guessed it flawlessly;
that is precisely the meaning.
Yunong's mind works differently than others.
If he were to write you a letter,
he would fear it falling into the wrong hands.
Moreover, such endless sorrow is difficult to put to paper;
this hidden message is far better.
When the Seventh Eve arrives,
you must spend the day here with us.
We will go in and recount your deep devotion today;
your bitter longing will not have been in vain.'
With these earnest reassurances, the three managed to comfort Ziyu,
and he forced himself to endure.
This was Qiguan's first time spending a prolonged period with Ziyu.
Observing his elegant bearing, Qiguan was filled with profound admiration;
it was no wonder Yunong held only him in his heart.
Seeing such profound devotion, Qiguan thought it was like a beautiful bride perfectly matched with her general.
It was only a pity their destiny was so thin, doomed
to brief meetings and long partings.
He truly believed the Heavens delighted in tormenting mortals.
To Ziyu they also recounted the vulgar
and despicable behavior Wei Pincai had exhibited on the boat when they previously shared passage.
The mere mention of Xi Eleven caused them
to grit their teeth and curse his name.
Sulan urged Ziyu to eat,
but his spirits were too low.
Desiring an early departure,
he took his leave and headed straight home.
Already in the carriage,
he began to feel an unnatural unease creeping into his limbs.
Upon returning home, he paid his respects
to Lady Yan and then retired to his study.
He lay down, muttering to himself,
alternating between heavy sighs and sudden tears, as though intoxicated.
By the following day, a severe illness had taken hold.
His mind was in turmoil, his speech disjointed.
Throughout the day, he broke down weeping multiple times.
Initially, he could suppress his tears when others were present,
but gradually, he lost all control.
Even in the presence of his mother, tears streamed down his face, his expression vacant and his spirit crushed.
Lady Yan, believing he had been afflicted by some evil spirit, summoned physicians to treat him.
She even resorted to drawing lots,
consulting diviners, making vows,
and praying to the gods.
For over ten days, there was not a sliver of improvement.
During a single day,
he would alternate between stupor and clarity.
In his stupor, he was entirely oblivious, seemingly deaf
and blind to his surroundings.
When clear-headed, he appeared as healthy as anyone else.
In his sleep, he muttered incessantly—
sometimes calling for 'Yunong,' sometimes 'Xiangwan,'
and at times, vehemently cursing Xi Eleven and Wei Pincai.
Lady Yan was frantic with worry.
Yan Zhongqing and Wang Xun visited every few days;
they understood the root of the malady
but were powerless to cure it.
After discussing the matter,
they felt they could not reveal the truth to Lady Yan,
offering only gentle, evasive comforts.
Hearing Ziyu constantly cry out 'Yunong' in his sleep, Lady Yan grew suspicious
that he had become entangled in some illicit affair outside.
Assuming 'Yunong' was a woman's name,
she suspected a scandalous secret.
Knowing Ziyu rarely ventured out,
and never without Yun,
she summoned the servant one day for a rigorous interrogation.
'Where exactly have you been taking the young master?'
she demanded.
'Who is this Yunong?
A courtesan, or someone else?'
Yun initially denied everything,
insisting, 'The young master only goes to the Garden,
or to Young Master Wang’s and Young Master Shi’s residences.
He has never seen a woman.
If this lowly one lies, may I not live to see tomorrow.'
Lady Yan thought, 'Gentle questions will yield nothing;
he won't confess.'
She ordered the servants to bring the heavy bamboo rods.
'Beat the truth out of him!'
she commanded.
Seeing the rods, Yun fell to his knees and kowtowed frantically.
'There truly is a young dan named Qinyan!
The young master often seeks him out.
When they meet, they cry more than they laugh.
During the fifth month, there was a day they claimed
to be visiting Lord Xu at the Yiyuan Garden,
but it was a lie.
The young master went with
that young dan and another young dan to boat on the canal east of the gate for half a day,
and they cried for half a day.
This lowly one was at the bow of the boat and heard nothing else.
The other day, the young master visited that young dan’s home.
The other young dan told him Qinyan had entered Young Master Hua's mansion,
and handed the young master a paper bundle—
this lowly one does not know what was inside.
The young master wept right there.
They tried to comfort him,
but ever since returning,
he has been like this.
Not a single word is false,
and nothing else has happened!'
Hearing this, Lady Yan burned with anger
and scolded Yun fiercely, 'You deserve a severe thrashing!
Why didn't you tell me sooner,
waiting until the rods were brought out to speak?
If not for your honesty today,
I would have had you beaten to death!'
She dismissed Yun, her heart filling with resentment toward her son.
He was young and reckless;
to squander his health
and fall so desperately ill over a mere young dan!
She wanted to lecture him harshly,
but seeing him so profoundly sick,
and realizing she was past forty with only this one son, she held back.
If she lectured him
and worsened his condition, what good would it do?
It was better to wait until he recovered.
Pondering the situation from every angle,
she summoned Li Yuanmao to uncover the full truth.
Yuanmao said, 'This student has never gone out with him.
Whether there is a Qinyan or not,
I truly do not know.
Last year, I heard old Wei Pincai often praising that Qinyan,
and my uncle took notice.
On the sixth day of the New Year, at the guild gathering, my uncle saw his performance and heard the praises.
After that, this student has witnessed nothing.
You must ask Wei Pincai for the detailed truth.'
Lady Yan immediately ordered the gatekeeper, Xu Shun,
to go to the Hua mansion and invite Secretary Wei over,
stating there was urgent business to discuss.
Pincai had no inkling it concerned this matter.
He had grown distant from Ziyu recently
and hadn't visited for over a month.
Hearing Lady Yan's summons,
he assumed some favorable opportunity awaited him.
The next day, dressed in splendid attire,
he rode out and arrived at the Mei residence.
Upon meeting Lady Yan,
he noticed the careworn expression on her face.
He first offered standard pleasantries,
asking for news from Jiangxi
and if any letters had arrived, then inquired about Ziyu, confidently predicting he would secure top rank in the upcoming exams.
After exchanging pleasantries, Lady Yan cut to the chase.
'Ziyu has contracted a strange illness,' she said,
describing the symptoms and repeating the testimonies of Yun and Yuanmao.
She pressed him, 'What exactly is the bond between my son and this Qinyan?'
Pincai felt a sudden wave of unease;
his conscience pricked, and a guilty flush colored his face.
He hesitated before answering, 'Last year,
when this nephew entered the capital,
I shared a boat with a theatrical troupe.
Among them was a young dan named Qinyan.
On the day of the guild gathering this year,
I happened to see his performance.
As for how elder brother came to know him later,
I heard they met during the lantern riddles at the Garden,
and elder brother gifted him a qin.
This nephew is a coarse man;
I cannot mingle with such refined literati.
How close they became,
or what bond they share, this nephew truly does not know.
Elder brother’s usual circle of young lords—
madame is acquainted with them—
they must know the depth of this feeling.
Why not ask them?'
Lady Yan demanded, 'Where is Qinyan now?'
Pincai replied, 'Qinyan was studying new plays at the Garden,
and Young Master Hua favored him—
' He half-rose from his seat.
'This nephew owes you and the lord deep kindness.
Knowing elder brother's illness stemmed from that actor—
wasting his studies and ruining his health—
I urged Young Master Hua to purchase him.
Unexpectedly, a Mr. Xi caused trouble,
wanting to ruin them.
Qinyan’s teacher was terrified and at a loss.
I persuaded him, and a few days ago, Qinyan was sent into the Hua mansion.
Once inside, he will not easily come out.
This nephew rejoiced inwardly,
thinking elder brother might finally close off
that part of his heart and turn to proper pursuits.
Otherwise, that actor would drag him down forever.'
Hearing this, Lady Yan’s anger surged.
She hated her son for his foolishness,
but she felt oddly grateful to Pincai.
She thanked him and said, 'Your brother is now gravely ill.
In his dreams, he raves nonsense,
crying and laughing, his mouth constantly calling for Yunong.
It is surely because that actor entered the Hua mansion.
Your brother may be worthless,
but he is my only son.
If any harm comes
to him, how will I face his father when he returns?
You are a capable man.
You must think of a way to cure him.'
Pincai shook his head, 'It is very difficult.
The proverb says, "Heart's disease requires heart's medicine."
This nephew knows your strict household rules.
Would the lord father ever allow him
to roam outside causing such scandals?'
Lady Yan retorted, 'I am not suggesting we indulge him in chasing young dans.
Even if I dote on him,
I would never go that far.
I heard Yun say
that when he met the young dan, they only wept.
Children's whims are hard to decipher;
perhaps there is some fate between them.
Now he dreams only of that actor.
Since you reside in the Hua mansion, think of a way
to bring that actor out to comfort him.
Perhaps he will heal faster.'
As she spoke, tears spilled from her eyes.
Pincai knitted his brow
and sighed, 'But this man is utterly unreasonable and devoid of human feeling.
Moreover, his wage is two hundred taels a month.
How can I possibly summon him out?'
Lady Yan asked, 'Why two hundred taels a month?
Is this person considered some sort of living treasure?
And since they demand so much money,
and your brother has none, how did he even come to know him?'
Pincai replied, 'Qinyan originally never wanted money,
but his teacher absolutely refuses to budge without silver.
This nephew has thought long and hard,
but there is no easy path.
We must negotiate with the teacher,
and the teacher will not accept empty talk.'
Lady Yan pressed, 'What kind of man is his teacher?'
Pincai explained, 'He is exceedingly difficult, a man who lives inside the eye of a coin.
To negotiate with him, unless you promise a substantial sum,
it is uncertain if he will agree.
He fears offending the Hua mansion;
if he loses his two thousand four hundred taels a year,
it would be disastrous.
In my view, relying on mere words with this creature is utterly hopeless.'
Hearing that money must be spent, Lady Yan declared, 'Just bring him here.
I do not mind spending the money,
but how much will it require?'
Pincai replied, 'This nephew will go and negotiate.
I will spare no effort.
Please set your mind at ease, Madame.'
With that, he rose to take his leave.
Through her tears, Lady Yan said, 'I am causing you much trouble, nephew.
I will say no more for now.
Since you are willing, please go today.
If possible, a reply today would be best.'
Pincai agreed.
Before leaving, he looked in on Ziyu.
Even in his severe illness, Ziyu had not forgotten past grievances;
upon seeing Pincai, he shut his eyes,
feigning sleep, and completely ignored him.
Pincai exchanged a few brief words with Yuanmao before departing.
He went straight back to the Hua mansion, sat in his room,
and pondered deeply, yet no plan came to mind.
He sought out Shanzhi, repeated Lady Yan's desperate plea, added his own embellishments,
and pleaded, 'This brother
and I are bound by three generations of friendship.
Madame Mei has only this one son, a clever and handsome youth.
Please, do this good deed and help cure him.
I will owe you an endless debt of gratitude.'
Shanzhi replied, 'What can I do?
I can only report to the Young Master, say that you asked,
and see if he will allow a visit.'
Pincai waved his hands frantically.
'You cannot!
We both know the Young Master's temper.
If you ask like that,
he will not only refuse,
but it will look ugly for everyone.
We need another plan.'
Shanzhi shrugged, 'You make the plan, then.
I don't meddle in such affairs.'
Pincai bowed deeply.
'Good third brother, good friend.
If you secure this, my brother will send you two bolts of fine, patterned treasury gauze.'
Pressed until he could no longer resist,
and recalling his own jealousy, Shanzhi finally said, 'Fine, let him go tomorrow.
If anyone asks, I will handle it
and keep your name out of it.'
Pincai beamed, bowed in gratitude,
and early the next day, took a carriage to see Lady Yan.
He played the part of the beleaguered negotiator,
claiming, 'His teacher finally agreed,
but demands two hundred taels of silver just to call him out.
He also fears that
if the boy is barred from re-entering the mansion,
it will be a disaster,
and he might demand the full two thousand four hundred taels.
From what this nephew sees, the man is incredibly slippery and detestable.
I scolded him harshly,
which frightened him a bit.
He said whether the boy can go back in or not is besides the point,
but the two hundred taels now is non-negotiable, otherwise he won't take the risk.'
Lady Yan, pained for her son, had no choice but to agree.
'Let him come tomorrow, then.
We will give him the two hundred taels,
and deal with the rest later.'
Pincai, seeing his trap sprung, was exceedingly pleased.
He took his leave, went straight to the silk shop, purchased two bolts of fine gauze,
and delivered them to Shanzhi the following day.
Why did Shanzhi dare to orchestrate such a scheme?
Qinyan had only been in the mansion for half a month,
yet even Lady Hua doted on him, frequently bestowing gifts and praising him:
'This child is honest;
he doesn't act like a theatrical performer at all.'
This favoritism stoked the fires of Shanzhi's jealousy.
He reasoned, 'I have been here for over three years,
holding the first rank.
He's been here a matter of days
and already shows such a clever head.
Won't he eventually crush me?'
He fully intended to squeeze Qinyan out.
Hearing Pincai's request presented the perfect opportunity.
He went to the Quiet-Green Retreat
and informed Qinyan of the plan.
Upon hearing the news, Qinyan’s eyes filled with tears.
'How could Gengxiang fall so gravely ill?
Brother Lin, can you truly send me out?
Does his family really want me to visit him?'
Shanzhi replied, 'Why would I lie
to you for no reason?
Rest easy.
The Young Master won't descend from his quarters for half the day.
Even if he calls for you,
I will simply say you took leave
to visit your sick teacher and will return shortly.
If there's any trouble,
I'll answer for it.
Just make sure you go and return quickly.
Furthermore, you must cure him this time.
A second visit will be incredibly difficult to arrange.'
Qinyan flushed and remained silent,
but his heart swelled with profound gratitude toward Shanzhi.
He felt he relied entirely on Shanzhi's care in this mansion,
and to be granted the chance
to see Gengxiang was a debt he must never forget.
In the glow of this favor, even his deep-seated hatred for Pincai faded,
acknowledging that this opportunity was also owed to him.
The day passed without incident.
Finally, dawn broke, and heaven itself seemed
to smile upon their endeavor.
The Young Master felt unwell
and remained in Lady Hua’s quarters, not emerging.
Tremendously relieved, Qinyan quickly ate his meal and sought out Shanzhi.
'How do I get out?
I don't know the way.'
Shanzhi instructed, 'Go with Secretary Wei.
The gatekeepers won't ask questions,
and even if they do, the rumors won't pierce the inner walls.'
Qinyan swallowed his pride and found Pincai.
Pincai was inwardly delighted but maintained a cold exterior.
'Go on, but be careful.
If this is exposed, do not say I accompanied you.'
Qinyan agreed.
They passed through the gates, layer by layer.
The guards recognized Wei and dared not question them.
Once outside the gate, Qinyan was seated in the carriage, the curtains drawn tight.
Pincai stood on the footboard,
with the servant Four riding on the tail.
Before long, they arrived at the Mei residence.
Pincai did not wait for an announcement;
he led Qinyan straight toward the study.
The gatekeeper, Xu Shun, was startled
but could not block their path,
so he followed them in.
Lady Yan was waiting anxiously.
Seeing Xu Shun enter as if to make a report,
she asked, 'What is the matter?'
Xu Shun replied, 'Master Wei brought someone who looks like an actor.
This lowly one dared not fail to report it.'
Lady Yan said, 'I know.
Bring them in quickly.'
As Pincai entered with a youth of about fifteen or sixteen, Lady Yan took one look and was profoundly startled.
She felt she had seen him before, suddenly realizing he bore a striking resemblance to her un-wedded daughter-in-law, Qionggu.
She marveled inwardly, 'I have watched many operas
and seen countless actors painted like women,
but I never studied them closely offstage.
But this Qinyan—
jade bones and ice flesh,
radiating a noble and resplendent air.
If his hair were dressed,
he would possess the bearing of a grand lady.
Even bare-faced, he looks like a refined scholar from a noble house,
bearing a remarkable resemblance to my own son.'
Her initial anger melted away, the harsh words she had prepared vanishing, replaced by an involuntary, gentle smile.
As Qinyan stepped over the threshold, his mind was solely on Ziyu's grave illness.
Driven by a love he could scarcely endure,
he had ignored the risks of fortune and disaster,
steeling himself for Lady Yan's harsh scolding.
Pincai’s threatening words in the carriage had left him timid and trembling.
He approached, kowtowed deeply to the Lady, rose,
and stood with his head bowed.
Lady Yan called him closer, studied him intently once more,
and bid Pincai sit.
'Where are you from?
When did you arrive in the capital?
How did you come to know my son?
And how close is your bond?
Tell me the complete truth,
and I will not harm you.'
Seeing her gentle expression, Qinyan relaxed slightly,
though tears still shimmered in his eyes.
He mumbled a few indistinct words.
Sensing his fear, Lady Yan soothed him, 'Do not be afraid.
This is my son's failing;
he sought you out, not the other way around.
Put your mind at ease.
I swear I will not hurt you,
but you must not evade the truth.
Speak plainly.'
Qinyan paused, then said, 'This humble one is from Suzhou.
I arrived in the capital last winter with the Lianjin troupe.
Both my parents are dead;
my uncle's wife sold me.
On the sixth day of the New Year, at the Suzhou guild,
I saw the young master for the first time.
Oddly, it felt as
if we had known each other before.
During the Lantern Festival,
I went to Lord Xu's Yiyuan Garden
to see the lanterns and the riddles they were making.
I loved the riddle, "Fallen flowers, a man stands alone;
fine rain, a pair of swallows fly."
Second Master Xu offered a jade qin as the prize,
saying he would introduce the winner to me.
That day, it was the young master who guessed it.
Two days later, there was a feast,
and I was called to serve.
That was our first true meeting.
The second time was
when Sulan invited us boating on the canal,
where we spent half a day.
It was only those two times.
This is the absolute truth.
If Madame does not believe me, please ask Secretary Wei.
Besides, Madame surely knows of the young master’s outings.'
Before he could finish, tears spilled uncontrollably down his cheeks.
Pincai interjected, 'This is all true.
They truly haven't even met three times.'
Lady Yan listened, puzzled,
yet moved to deep pity by Qinyan's demeanor.
She thought, 'How could they have only met twice in half a year?'
She asked, 'Your words are naturally true,
but my son now dreams of you constantly.
Surely you understand why.'
Qinyan replied, 'With Madame's grace, how would this humble one dare to lie?
It is truly strange.
Not only did I feel I had seen the young master before,
but upon seeing me,
he also said it was as
if we were already acquainted.
The moment we met,
it felt as though we were family,
yet neither of us could explain the cause.'
Lady Yan smiled, 'Hearing your words,
it is indeed strange,
and I cannot fathom it.
But my son is now this ill
because you entered the Hua mansion.
I called you here to comfort and heal him.
If you succeed, I will not blame you;
instead, I will reward you.'
Qinyan felt a deeper surge of grief,
stifling a sob and murmuring an indistinct reply.
Pitying him further, Lady Yan invited Pincai
and Qinyan into Ziyu's room,
while she and Pincai remained in the outer room
to listen to what they would say and observe the scene.
Xu Shun, having stood by
and finally understood the source of the young master's illness, followed them into the bedroom to listen closely.
