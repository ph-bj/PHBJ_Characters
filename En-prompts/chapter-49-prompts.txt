Precious Mirror for Grading Flowers
Chapter 49
**Moved by love, Scholar Tian seeks marriage as top laureate;
by chance affection, Third Sister Xu recognizes her lost brother.**
After sending Qinxian away, Ziyu suffered such acute shock
and grief that his mind drifted into a stupor;
his old illness relapsed,
and a full month passed before he recovered.
Later, when Lady Yan learned
that Qinxian had left the capital
and had been taken by Qu Daosheng as an adopted son,
she, too, felt a measure of relief and joy for him.
Meanwhile, after Chunhang lost his wife, Madam Tian, feeling her age
and unaccustomed to running a household without a daughter-in-law,
urged Chunhang to seek a new match from a respectable family.
Chunhang hesitated, initially inclined to take a concubine first.
Yet, finding the local northern women too blunt
and lacking in tender grace,
he sought to hire a refined older servant
to wait upon the old madam.
To his dismay, the older maidservants of the capital were a hopeless lot;
scarcely one in ten thousand proved suitable.
Once, he hired two women from east of the city, both around forty.
One had a face as pockmarked as a honeycomb, wore a horse-tail hairnet over her bun,
and bound her trouser legs loosely like two cloth sacks.
With paper flowers stuck haphazardly in her hair,
she waddled clumsily, her waist twisting
and hips swaying, a truly unsightly figure.
Worse, she was a chronic gossip,
stirring up discord in every corner—
accusing one of thievery and another of illicit affairs—
merely to cover her own flaws.
It is exactly this sort of woman who so often severs the bonds of family harmony.
The other was even more peculiar:
she had a face broad as a sand basin
and a belly round as a water vat.
After serving the old madam a single meal,
she refused to lift a finger further.
Whenever called upon, she would feign deafness, thrust out her great belly,
and stand with her feet splayed in thin-soled shoes,
puffing on Guandong tobacco.
She spent her time fraternizing with the kitchen stokers and odd-job men,
calling them 'Big Brother'
and 'Master,' laughing uproariously as she sat on the earthen kitchen kang,
squeezing into the crowd
and demanding they entertain her with jokes.
Every other day, she would wander out for half a day
to flirt and relieve her boredom with the solitary carters, rice-pounders, and coal-porters.
Even when she caught sight of Chunhang,
she would cast him sly, sidelong glances.
How could Chunhang endure such creatures?
In less than half a month,
he drove them all out.
He then bought two young maids of twelve or thirteen,
but they too were of mediocre stock.
One day, the carriage driver Zhou Xiaosan mentioned his Third Sister to Huifang,
saying she was willing
to come in and serve the old madam.
He boasted that she was highly capable in all tasks, both rough
and delicate, could sew, read, and calculate—
and her needlework was beyond compare.
Huifang, who had heard of Third Sister's formidable reputation
from the time she taught Pan San a lesson,
figured she must be a sharp and clever woman.
Curious to meet her
and learn exactly how she had dealt with Pan San,
he recommended her to Chunhang, who readily agreed.
Ever since the Pan San affair, Third Sister had harbored a lingering fear of retaliation;
thus, she had remained idle at home for two or three months until this opportunity arose.
Seeing that Chunhang had recently been named top scholar
and that the old madam had arrived without a satisfactory attendant,
Xiaosan thought it best to bring his sister in,
both for convenience and to spare a young widow the vulnerability of living alone outside.
On the day she arrived,
she dressed with a style neither overly rustic nor overly ornate:
a light dusting of fragrant powder, faintly drawn moth-eyebrows,
and a single pomegranate blossom pinned at her temple.
She wore a moon-white cotton blouse over a lined vest, pale water-green silk trousers,
and arched, three-inch lotus shoes, her waist as slender as a pestle.
She presented herself to Chunhang and bowed.
Chunhang was taken aback;
he had assumed Zhou Xiaosan's sister would be coarse
and heavy-set, never expecting a woman as delicate as a flower branch.
Treating her with gentle courtesy,
he sent her in to greet the old madam.
Madam Tian took to her immediately, especially since Third Sister proved
to be exceptionally clever and attentive to every detail.
She served not only the old madam,
but also poured her care into attending to Chunhang and Huifang.
The dishes she prepared were a dozen times finer than those of the capital's chefs.
When the old madam had gifts for Huifang,
she would send Third Sister out to deliver them.
Before seeing Chunhang, Xiaosan had never described him to her,
leaving her to assume he possessed an ordinary countenance.
But upon beholding his graceful, unconstrained elegance and his jade-like brilliance,
that universal human admiration for beauty arose within her,
and she served him with extra diligence.
Then, observing Huifang's outstanding talent and looks,
she felt herself vastly inferior by comparison
and could not help but feel a touch of self-conscious inadequacy.
Yet, exchanging even a passing word with him brought her exceptional joy;
consequently, she treated Huifang with a warmth
and attentiveness quite unlike the rest.
After several encounters, they grew quite familiar.
One day, Chunhang was away from home,
and Huifang sat alone in the study.
Knowing Huifang was there, the old madam sent Third Sister out with some pastries.
Carrying the small dish, Third Sister paused at the study door
and gave a soft cough before entering,
her face wreathed in a bright smile as she called out, 'Master Su!'
Huifang returned the smile and called back, 'Third Sister!'
'This is from the old madam,' she said,
placing the dish near Huifang's hand.
Huifang noticed her slender, pointed fingers adorned with silver nail-guards.
He accepted the dish and set it down,
saying, 'Please, Third Sister, just call me by my name,
and pass my thanks to the old madam for her reward.'
Third Sister agreed and then stood looking Huifang over carefully.
Her presence reminded Huifang of the Pan San affair;
he wanted to ask her about it
but felt a bit timid.
With her perceptive eyes, Third Sister could already
tell he had a question lingering on his lips,
so she simply gazed at him blankly,
waiting for him to speak.
Being stared at so unblinkingly made Huifang somewhat bashful.
He thought to himself, 'Looking at her demeanor, even if I ask,
she probably won't hold it against me.'
Taking a step closer with a charming smile,
he asked, 'Third Sister!
There's a question I'd like to ask,
but I'm afraid it might upset you.
I wonder if I should ask it or not?'
Third Sister gave a faint smile.
'What could possibly be wrong to ask?'
Huifang, keeping his coaxing smile, continued, 'I know you are a true heroine among women,
and that you gave Pan San a thorough, satisfying thrashing.
Is that really true?'
Hearing this, Third Sister's face flushed red.
She gave a low, playful 'tut,' turned on her heel with a smile,
and retorted as she walked away, 'I wondered what you were going to ask!
Who even knows Pan San?
Where did you hear such talk?'
As she reached the door curtain,
the long silver ear-pick pinned in her hair snagged the fabric
and fell to the floor.
She turned her face back, flashed another smile, picked it up,
tucked it hastily into her hair, and hurried inside.
Though Huifang had hit a soft wall,
seeing that she showed no real anger
and had even smiled twice, he felt relieved.
Yet, he ultimately regretted his slip of the tongue,
realizing he should not have brought the matter up.
After that, Huifang visited twice more without catching sight of her.
One day, he came again while Chunhang was out.
Sitting idly in the study,
he heard Third Sister's footsteps passing his door.
He hurried out to look
and saw her at the second gate,
calling Xiaosan over for a word.
After she finished speaking
and turned back inside, Huifang intended to greet her and chat a little,
but she lowered her head as
if she hadn't seen him.
Yet, just as she passed the study door,
she turned her face back, her eyes meeting Huifang's directly.
With a downward glance
and a slight smile, she walked away.
From then on, Huifang knew she bore him no ill will.
Meanwhile, rumors that Chunhang sought a new match from a pure,
respectable family reached the ears of Su the Marquis,
fitting his own desires perfectly.
He dropped a few subtle hints before Yang Fangyou,
a director in the Bureau of Military Appointments,
suggesting he convey to Chunhang
that he should send an intermediary to propose.
Yang Fangyou was Chunhang's examiner-teacher;
delighted by the prospect,
he immediately relayed this to Chunhang,
urging him to send a matchmaker.
Chunhang felt somewhat hesitant.
The Su family was a house of hereditary nobility with a dazzling reputation,
and while he himself had achieved fame, his origins remained humble and plain.
Thus, he was somewhat reluctant.
Furthermore, knowing nothing of the young lady's character or appearance,
he wanted to make discreet inquiries first.
But since his patron teacher desired the alliance,
and his examiner-teacher had brought the word, how could he easily decline?
He had no choice but to verbally assent,
adding that he must first report to his mother before giving a final answer.
After Lord Yang departed, Chunhang,
knowing that Ziyun was closest
to Su the Marquis, decided to delay speaking to his mother
and first sought out Ziyun to investigate.
Arriving at the gates of Delightful Garden,
he saw a green-canopied carriage
and eight horses crowded to one side,
realizing there was a guest.
Inquiring with the attendants,
he learned that Huagongzi was in the garden.
Chunhang therefore decided to head first to the Cool Tranquil Poetic Realm to find Nanxiang.
But why had Huagongzi returned
to Delightful Garden after his rift with Ziyun over Qinyan?
Huagongzi had written that harsh,
severing letter in a fit of sudden temper.
After two days had passed,
he began to regret it.
Yet Ziyun carried on as if nothing had happened,
making no move to invite him over or entertain him,
leaving Huagongzi feeling extremely awkward and bored.
Later, when bidding farewell
to Qu Daosheng, Daosheng had spoken at length about Ziyun's many virtues.
Daosheng also recounted the spirit-writing session,
revealing that Qinyan and Huagongzi had been father
and daughter in a former life,
and he recited the entire poem to him.
Hearing this, Huagongzi was deeply shaken.
Daosheng then went on to praise Qinyan's many fine qualities,
mentioning that he had now taken him as an adopted son
and would be bringing him along to his new post.
Huagongzi's anger dissolved like ice melting after a rain,
leaving him feeling more than a little apologetic.
Thinking over Qinyan again,
he realized the boy truly had no faults;
he had wronged him,
and he voiced his praise.
After Daosheng left, Cixian arrived
and clarified the entire matter thoroughly.
Only then did Huagongzi fully repent his rashness,
mindful of the generations of deep friendship between their families.
To break ties over such a trivial matter would surely invite public ridicule.
Recognizing his duty as an elder sworn brother,
he swallowed his pride and went first to seek reconciliation.
Daosheng and Cixian had already conveyed Huagongzi's remorse to Ziyun.
Ziyun, ever magnanimous and accommodating, harbored no lingering grudges since Huagongzi had made the first move.
He treated him exactly as before, never mentioning the incident.
Unable to hold back, Huagongzi admitted he had mistakenly believed idle rumors and confessed his fault.
Ziyun comforted him with many reassuring words
and kept him for a small drink at the 'Spring Breeze Intoxication' pavilion before they parted.
Neither Cixian nor Nanxiang were present.
The previous night, after Ziyun left, Nanxiang had been struck by a wild drinking mood.
He dragged Cixian onto a boat
and they polished off an entire jar,
leaving Cixian dead drunk.
Nanxiang himself fell into the water
and swallowed several gulps before the servants pulled him out.
Today, his stomach was bloated and his back ached,
making him unable to rise,
while Cixian slept in a heavy, groggy stupor.
Chunhang visited their rooms to chat briefly,
and upon learning Huagongzi had departed,
he finally went to see Ziyun.
Ziyun was in the Hall of Treasured Fragrance.
Seeing Chunhang enter, he hurried to welcome him,
and they exchanged pleasantries.
Chunhang asked whether Ziyun,
being a student of Su the Marquis, knew the intimate details of his household.
'Why do you ask?'
Ziyun inquired.
Chunhang recounted Yang Fangyou's words.
Ziyun immediately offered his congratulations.
'Congratulations, congratulations!
This joy is even greater than your taking first place in the imperial examinations!'
Chunhang laughed, 'It's nothing but a connection to high office,
and you start congratulating me before we even know if it will succeed.'
Ziyun replied, 'High office is of no importance;
we don't need to borrow his prestige.
But Su the Marquis is the patron who passed me for my provincial degree,
the examiner who passed my elder brother,
and a sworn brother to my father.
With these double layers of academic ties and deep family friendship,
he is an exceedingly good man.
But leave that aside.
Why do I say this joy is greater than winning top scholar?
My two young 'sisters' of the Su family are truly unparalleled in this world,
famous as the Two Qiaos of the Su clan.
The elder sister is Huagongzi's wife, now twenty-one, named Huanxiang.
The younger sister you just mentioned is Huanlan, born of the same mother.
If you secure this match,
you will be so overjoyed you won't be able to speak;
only then will you believe my words.'
Hearing Ziyun praise her so highly, Chunhang half-believed him
and asked, 'What is so wonderful about her that you praise her so?
Tell me a bit about her.
Have you actually seen her?'
Ziyun replied, 'How could I not have seen her?
The two sisters used
to come frequently with their mother to visit mine,
and they are sworn sisters with my wife;
they never avoided me when I was around.
Since the elder sister married, the younger hasn't cared
to come alone as much, usually only visiting when her sister returns home.
It's a strange thing,
and you surely won't believe me when I tell you.
If the match succeeds and you see her,
you will realize that the two sisters look exactly like Su Meixiang!
The elder sister resembles him perhaps seven parts out of ten,
but the younger sister is a nine-part likeness,
though even more tender and radiant than Meixiang.
After all, Meixiang is a man,
and naturally cannot match the delicate charm of a woman.'
Before he even finished, Chunhang burst into delighted laughter.
'Is this really true?
I can hardly believe it.
How could they share the same surname and the same face?'
He laughed uproariously.
Ziyun found it amusing too,
saying, 'Believe it or not,
it's up to you;
just assume I'm lying.'
Chunhang made a deep bow
and said, 'I have been rash, please do not take offense, my brother.
But given your profound friendship with Teacher Su,
if I am to ask for a matchmaker now,
it absolutely must be you.'
Ziyun demurred, 'I don't know how to play matchmaker,
I dare not take on the task.
Since Fourth Master Yang already brought it up, just ask him to be the matchmaker;
why do you need me to go?'
Chunhang bowed again;
Ziyun pretended not to see
and did not return the courtesy.
Chunhang smiled, 'Teacher Yang is his subordinate;
he feels constrained in his presence
and finds it hard to speak freely.
He wants me to find someone else to propose,
and you have always loved
to help others achieve beautiful things.
Besides, if someone else goes, Teacher Su might not believe them.
Words carry the weight of the speaker;
it simply must be you.'
Ziyun paused for a moment before saying, 'What
if I am tricking you?
Don't blame me later.'
Chunhang bowed continuously, and Ziyun finally had no choice but to agree.
Chunhang then took his leave.
Two days later, Ziyun returned Huagongzi's visit,
and on his way back through the city,
he stopped at the Su residence.
Su the Marquis had just returned from his office and invited him in.
Ziyun paid his respects
and then went inside to greet the Madam,
conveying his wife's regards, to which Madam Su warmly replied.
Su the Marquis then invited him into the inner study to sit.
After chatting for a while, Ziyun relayed Chunhang's situation:
that he had lost his wife in the spring,
had heard of the second young lady's virtuous reputation,
and was acting on his mother's command
to seek a marriage alliance.
Su the Marquis feigned thoughtful deliberation.
'Looking at Compiler Tian's literary talent and appearance,
he is truly excellent,
and he comes from an old, established family.
But I do not know of his moral character;
what I fear most is a frivolous youth.
Since you are his close friend,
you must know him deeply.'
Ziyun replied, 'Compiler Tian's moral character is known to all;
there is no need for this student to elaborate,
for Teacher can easily inquire and find out.
He truly sharpens his integrity
and is complete in both filial piety and loyalty to friends.'
Su the Marquis let out a hearty laugh.
'This shows that your choice in friends is upright;
naturally, nothing more needs to be said.'
Ziyun responded, 'Under Teacher's transforming spring breeze
and nurturing rain, how could any weeds grow?'
Greatly pleased, Su the Marquis kept Ziyun for a small drink.
He asked if Ziyun had seen Huagongzi recently,
and Ziyun replied that he had just come from there.
Su the Marquis then asked, 'Your garden must be even more beautifully arranged now;
I haven't been there for a stroll in a year or two.'
Ziyun said, 'It is somewhat better than when first completed;
the flowers and trees are more lush,
and the waterways have been fully opened.'
Su the Marquis sighed, 'I have been truly busy these past few years,
without a single day of leisure.
Your Madam Teacher, however, has been wanting
to go for a stroll,
though the weather has grown hot now.'
Ziyun immediately offered, 'When I return,
I will have my wife choose an auspicious day
and invite the Madam and the young lady to visit the garden.'
Su the Marquis replied, 'Let us wait until the autumn weather turns cool.'
Ziyun then steered the conversation back to Chunhang's proposal.
Su the Marquis said, 'Since you have come for this purpose, how could I refuse?
Please convey my assent to Compiler Tian.'
Ziyun made a deep bow of gratitude.
Su the Marquis then inquired about the well-being of Ziyun's parents at their posts,
assuming regular family bulletins arrived,
and asked after his elder brother in Huaiyang.
Ziyun answered, 'My father sent servants to the capital last month;
thanks to your blessing, all is well.
The officials, the military, the common people,
and the foreign merchants are all peaceful, goods are abundant,
and the harvest is good;
it is quite comfortable.
My elder brother recently took up the post of Salt Controller,
and he sent a letter of greeting to you last month.'
Su the Marquis nodded, 'Yes, yes,
I just wrote a reply;
I forgot after only a few days.
He also brought some gifts,
and I haven't yet thanked him.'
Ziyun half-rose from his seat
and murmured, 'You are too kind.'
He then added, 'My elder brother also had another nephew born this year.'
Su the Marquis smiled, 'A double congratulations then.'
He continued, 'Your father-in-law has now been promoted to Fujian;
that must be better than Yunnan?'
Ziyun replied, 'Previously, as Governor of Yunnan, there were fewer affairs to manage.
Now, overseeing both Zhejiang and Fujian provinces,
and handling foreign maritime matters as well,
it is much more complex.'
Su the Marquis reminisced, 'Your father-in-law
and I passed the same examination year and served in the same academy;
I suppose he has mentioned this to you.
The day we were retained at the academy,
he had a dream
that night of a grand procession leading to a place
with a ceremonial archway inscribed with the words 'Blessed Land' (Fudi).
He knew beforehand he would go to Fujian.
How old is his son this year?'
Ziyun said, 'He is only eight this year.'
Su the Marquis sighed, 'He is four years older than I am;
at fifty-five, he has an eight-year-old son.
I am fifty-one, and I have none at all.'
Ziyun consoled him, 'To have a son past fifty is not considered very late.
A family of such accumulated virtue
and noble lineage has no need to worry about this.'
Su the Marquis sighed, 'I no longer entertain such thoughts.
Is your father in his sixties this year?'
Ziyun replied, 'My father is sixty-three,
and my mother is sixty-two.'
Su the Marquis exclaimed, 'What tremendous fortune your father has!
When he was in the capital that year,
he was already fifty-nine,
yet his beard and hair were glossy black.
He looked nothing like a man of sixty;
he possessed the spirit of a dragon or horse,
something we cannot compare to.
And your father's fortune is truly complete in every worldly aspect;
even your father-in-law cannot match him.'
Ziyun said humbly, 'It is all due
to Heavenly grace and ancestral virtue.
My father's career has been quite smooth,
encountering no great storms.
As for my father-in-law,
he has met with several stumbles along the way.'
Su the Marquis commented, 'Mr.
Hailou is overly upright and unbending;
I imagine being his subordinate is no easy task.'
He then asked, 'This year, a new Hanlin bachelor named Shi Nanxiang was selected;
he is the son of Intendant Shi of Daming,
who passed the same year as I did.
This man has some literary reputation,
but I never see him out and about.'
Ziyun smiled, 'Shi Zhujun is a lofty, unconstrained character;
he is currently staying in my garden.
Do you have any instructions for him, Teacher?'
Su the Marquis replied, 'No particular instructions.
I just heard someone say
that his etiquette when meeting seniors is somewhat lacking.
Some criticize him as arrogant,
though perhaps, being newly entered into the Hanlin Academy,
he simply doesn't know these customs yet.
As for the strict rules between seniors
and juniors, even when I was in the academy, people debated them.
Just remind him to be a little more careful in the future.'
He continued, 'In autumn this year,
there will be the special 'Broad Learning and Grand Diction' examination;
this honor hasn't been tested for fifty years.
You have a wide circle of acquaintances;
among the talents of the realm
and the sons of noble families,
have you seen or heard of any with true, substantial learning?'
Ziyun replied carefully, 'Since Teacher asks,
I dare not fail to answer.
The talents of the realm are vast,
and I am isolated and ignorant, unable to know everyone.
But among the noble families, sons of high officials,
and the famous scholars from the provincial and metropolitan exams,
I do know quite a few intimately.
However, many merely echo the opinions of others.
There is one older senior who recently left for a post, named Qu Benli.
I assume that as an active official,
he cannot take the exam in the capital.'
Su the Marquis said, 'Ah, Qu Daosheng?
He was recommended for Filial Piety and Incorruptibility;
it is a pity he is relegated to a lower post.
Otherwise, it would be good to sponsor him.
There is also the famous Nanjing scholar Jin Su,
who is restricted by established regulations and cannot be recommended—
truly a regrettable thing.
Who else?'
Ziyun continued, 'There are a few others whose brilliant talents are yet unrevealed.
The son of the Hanlin Reader-in-Waiting Mr. Mei, named Ziyu,
is currently hailed among the youth as a resplendent star and a phoenix.'
Su the Marquis nodded.
Ziyun went on, 'The son of the late Hanlin Compiler Yan Zhuang, named Zhongqing;
the son of the current Minister of Rites Lord Liu, named Wenze;
and the son of the Grand Secretariat Lord Wang, named Xun.
In addition, there is the Suzhou specially recommended graduate Gao Pin,
and the Hunan honored graduate Xiao Cixian.
These gentlemen fully live up to their reputations.
They study day and night with Compiler Tian and Bachelor Shi,
and I associate with them closely.
As for the rest,
I do not know them well
and dare not recommend them indiscriminately.'
Hearing this, Su the Marquis stroked his beard and laughed heartily.
'How is it that the people you recommend are mostly the sons of my examination-year peers?
Do not play favorites just to please me.'
Ziyun smiled, 'How would I dare?
As for Teacher's old friends and classmates,
I do not know all of them.'
Su the Marquis laughed, 'It was just a joke;
I know you wouldn't show private bias.
The ones you just mentioned—
I didn't know the family backgrounds of those two provincial graduates.
But as for Mei Tie'an, Wang Zhifu, Liu Dingzhi, the late Yan Mutang,
as well as your father-in-law Yuan Hailou
and Shi's father Shi Jianhou,
we all passed in the same year.
Now, some serve as ministry directors, some as prefects and magistrates.
This is the uneven fate of human life.
But our cohort has done well;
quite a few have reached the first rank.'
They spoke of other matters for a while.
Seeing it was getting late after sitting for so long,
Ziyun took his leave and departed the city.
In his carriage, he pondered for a moment
and thought, 'Xiangfan got off too easily;
I'd better wait for him
to come beg me before I tell him.'
And with that, he headed straight home.
The next day, Chunhang indeed came looking for Ziyun,
but Ziyun merely claimed there was business in the inner household
and left Chunhang waiting in Nanxiang and Cixian's quarters all day.
The following day, Chunhang came again,
and again Ziyun avoided him.
Chunhang knew perfectly well
that Ziyun was deliberately making things difficult,
but fearing the match might fall through,
he swallowed his impatience.
On the third day,
he came yet again and finally saw Ziyun.
Ziyun laughed, 'What urgent business brings you seeking me day after day?'
Chunhang smiled wryly, 'I have already paid three visits.
I know I misspoke the other day,
and you are blaming me for it.'
Ziyun laughed, 'Preposterous!
Between sworn friends of the heart like us, even
if a wrong word was spoken, there is absolutely no reason to hold a grudge.'
He then chatted of idle matters, deliberately avoiding the subject of Su the Marquis.
Impatient, Chunhang was forced
to ask, 'Did you go into the city to meet Teacher Su the other day?'
Ziyun said, 'We talked for half a day,
and I had to rush
to get out of the city before the gates closed.'
Seeing Ziyun's unreadable expression, Chunhang grew anxious
and asked, 'And how did the matter I entrusted to you turn out?'
Ziyun replied, 'There is some hope.'
Hearing this, Chunhang became deeply suspicious,
thinking to himself, 'According to Teacher Yang,
he was entirely willing.
Why is there only 'some hope' now?
What does this mean?
Was Teacher Yang just imagining his willingness?'
He asked Ziyun, 'In your view, what is his intent?
Does it match what my examiner-teacher said?'
Ziyun replied, 'Teacher Su did praise your talent and learning,
saying you are truly worthy of the title of top scholar,
and that the marriage is possible in principle.
But I don't know where he heard a certain piece of idle gossip.
I defended you at length,
and only then did he remain half-doubting, half-believing,
saying he would consider it further.'
Unable to guess what this meant, Chunhang asked, 'What gossip did he hear?'
Ziyun teased, 'I'm afraid you'll get angry if I say it,
so I'd better not.'
Chunhang pressed, 'Why would I be angry?
Just tell me the truth.'
Ziyun laughed, 'The question he asked amused me too.
He said:
'I hear he already has a Madame Zhuangyuan at home, also surnamed Su,
who even showed him great grace—
so why does he need to remarry?''
Chunhang flushed crimson and retorted, 'That is outrageous!
How can you make such jokes?
It is no matter to mock me,
but you should leave some respect for Meixiang's memory.'
Ziyun smiled, 'Those were his words, what does it have to do with me?'
Chunhang forced a smile, 'You have toyed with me enough.
What is the real outcome?
Now it is not he who is asking me,
but I who am begging him.'
Ziyun asked, 'Are you willing to beg him?
If you devote yourself
to begging and follow him closely, after a month or two,
his heart might naturally soften and he will agree.'
Hearing Ziyun's every sentence layered with sharp teasing, Chunhang felt a mix of anger and embarrassment,
but knowing it was Ziyun,
he couldn't easily snap back.
He could only offer a placating smile
and say, 'It isn't that I am in such a hurry,
but it is my mother's command
that it is best accomplished early.
Today my mother repeatedly ordered me
to respectfully beg your help to finalize it swiftly;
in the future, I will offer you my deepest thanks.'
Ziyun laughed loudly, looking at Chunhang.
'You truly are a fine fellow—
able to swallow your pride and bow when needed.
Since it is the venerable mother's strict command,
how could your foolish brother not exert all his effort
to plan this for you?
Perhaps it might actually be achieved,
it is not impossible.'
Overjoyed, Chunhang thanked him again and again.
Just then, Cixian and Nanxiang entered,
and everyone took a seat.
Ziyun immediately relayed Su the Marquis's remarks about Nanxiang's etiquette.
Hearing this, Nanxiang's eyebrows shot up in indignation.
'What did I do wrong?
I follow the same etiquette as everyone else.
Besides, that day I went with a group;
I was not alone.
Why is it suddenly my fault,
and why am I singled out as arrogant?
This is difficult indeed.
This is truly difficult.'
Chunhang smiled and suggested, 'Your formal etiquette might not have been wrong,
but perhaps there was something amiss in your expression or demeanor.'
Nanxiang glared at Chunhang
and demanded, 'I'd like you to explain to me—
what exactly does 'something amiss in expression or demeanor' mean?'
The room fell silent.
Cixian broke the quiet by asking Ziyun, 'How did Xiangfan's matter go?'
Ziyun replied, 'It can be done.'
He then recounted how Su the Marquis had asked him
to seek out men of true talent and learning,
detailing the names he had proposed.
Turning to Nanxiang, he added, 'It turns out you all share examination-year ties.'
Nanxiang replied, 'He is indeed an elder of my father's examination year,
but we never had much interaction before.'
Ziyun noted, 'I hear the Grand Diction exam is set
for the first day of the eighth month,
which is only a little over two months away.
Why hasn't Gao Zhuoran arrived yet?'
Chunhang said, 'He hasn't even sent a single letter.
Who knows what he is doing at home—
it's utterly absurd.'
Cixian surmised, 'I imagine Zhuoran must be delayed somewhere;
he will probably arrive next month.
He needs the governor of his home province to formally recommend him.'
The four chatted a while longer before Chunhang took his leave and returned home.
He reported everything Ziyun had said about the marriage proposal to his mother,
and Madam Tian was delighted.
She immediately asked Ziyun
to finalize the agreement and choose an auspicious day to exchange the preliminary betrothal cards,
to be followed by the formal presentation of betrothal gifts on a set date.
Eventually, the date was set for the seventh day of the seventh month.
Chunhang explained the matter to Huifang,
who also rejoiced for him.
Chunhang then recounted Ziyun's words,
saying, 'He says this Miss Su resembles you nine parts out of ten.'
Huifang laughed, 'Isn't that just insulting her?
For a young lady of a noble house to look like me,
and for that to be called good—
what sort of people are we supposed to be?'
Chunhang said, 'I only fear she might not match you.
If she truly looks like you,
I will be completely satisfied,
and I will worship her like a Bodhisattva every day.'
Huifang smiled, 'You always say that,
but I've never seen you worship anyone.'
Chunhang laughed, 'If you want me to worship,
I'll worship right now!'
He promptly made a deep bow to Huifang.
Huifang laughed, hastily stepping aside,
and said, 'Don't shorten my life with such unearned honors!
Save your bows for your future Madame Zhuangyuan.'
Chunhang chuckled, 'Someone just made that very remark.'
'What remark?'
asked Huifang.
Chunhang thought for a moment and said, 'Oh, nothing.'
Huifang pressed, 'You said someone just made a remark;
why do you change your tune and say it's nothing?'
Chunhang replied, 'I meant the remark about the Madame Zhuangyuan;
she originally has the surname Su as well.'
Huifang's face flushed, and he shot Chunhang a pointed look.
Chunhang dared not say more,
and Huifang did not ask again.
Chunhang said, 'You really ought
to settle down and start a family yourself,
though there are few who are worthy of you.'
Huifang replied, 'That's true enough,
and I've thought the same.
For a match with us, good families won't agree to it,
and I wouldn't want a daughter from a petty, low-class home.
Furthermore, people like us have had our reputations so ruined by those shameless scoundrels
that no one would believe we are clean and upright.'
He continued, 'I think, rather than marrying a girl from a petty family,
it would be better
to marry a maid from a great house.
They are accustomed to proper manners and virtuous conduct,
without that narrow-minded pettiness.
There is only one issue:
I fear the master of the house may have already taken his pleasure with her—
that's true nine times out of ten.'
Chunhang laughed, 'That is an inevitable fact.
I imagine Duxiang's household has quite a few maids.'
Huifang said, 'Duxiang naturally has good ones,
and the inner household discipline is excellent;
I've never encountered the maids coming out into the garden.
Besides, we're separated by a street,
so it wouldn't be convenient.
I've only heard that Huagongzi has the most maids,
and they are all fine.
Once, when we were performing at his house,
I saw a large group of them behind the bead curtain—
some in men's clothes, some in women's, all powdered and painted,
though I couldn't see them clearly.'
Chunhang offered, 'In the future,
when Su the Marquis sends over the dowry maids, there are sure to be several.
If there is a good one among them,
I will give her to you.'
Huifang laughed, 'Many thanks, many thanks!
In that case, I will just have
to serve here for the rest of my life,
counting as a bonded servant
to both the Tian and Su families.'
Chunhang protested, 'Your words are too heavy!
Too heavy!
I have my own principles
and would never let you suffer the slightest wrong.
Besides, it was just a joke;
who knows if there will be good ones or not?
I think there truly are mismatched pairs in this world.
Take our own Zhou Xiaosan—
to have such a fine woman as his wife, isn't
that a terrible waste of her?'
Huifang asked, 'Do you like her?'
Chunhang laughed, 'Preposterous!
I was merely making a casual remark.'
Huifang countered, 'The word 'like' is of no great consequence;
a heart that appreciates beauty is naturally unavoidable for anyone.
This Third Sister is not only beautiful, but exceptionally intelligent—
and she is a chaste woman at that.'
Chunhang laughed, 'Intelligent, yes;
chaste, I'm not so sure.'
Huifang smiled, 'Haven't you heard how she dealt with Pan San?'
Chunhang chuckled, 'I've heard something of it,
but with a face like Pan San's,
he naturally deserved to be dealt with.
As you just said, a heart
that appreciates beauty is universal.
If you had been Pan San,
she wouldn't have had the heart to deal with you.'
Huifang shot back, 'Why don't you test her yourself?
She's right here in your house;
even if she wanted
to deal with you, she wouldn't dare.'
Chunhang laughed, 'Now you're just talking nonsense.'
Suddenly, a servant arrived with an urgent summons:
'Master Yang the examiner-teacher has an important matter
to discuss and requests the master's presence immediately.'
Chunhang ordered the carriage harnessed, changed his clothes, and departed.
Left with idle time, Huifang felt bored staying alone in his quarters
and decided his only recourse was to visit his various acquaintances.
Just as Chunhang left and Huifang stepped out,
he suddenly heard a soft, tinkling laugh.
Turning his head, he saw it was Third Sister.
Huifang greeted her with a smile,
and Third Sister, beaming, said, 'I haven't seen you come around for days.'
Huifang replied, 'I actually come every day,
I just never see you come out.'
Third Sister asked, 'Has the master gone out?'
She then glanced at the watch pouch hanging at Huifang's waist
and remarked, 'I know how to make those too.
I can also make gauze-embroidered sachets.'
Huifang smiled and said, 'Why not grace me with one, then?'
Third Sister giggled, 'I don't give my things to just anyone.'
Huifang countered, 'Giving away needlework is no big matter.'
She shot him a sidelong glance
and asked, 'What is your honorable age this year?'
Huifang replied, 'I am nineteen.'
Third Sister said, 'Then we are the same age.
But I fear your birth month must be smaller than mine.
Which month were you born?'
Huifang said, 'The third month.'
Third Sister replied, 'I am older than you;
I was born in the first month.'
Huifang said, 'Then you are my elder sister.
From now on, I will call you sister.'
Third Sister smiled, 'I am not worthy.'
Huifang said, 'I have spoken rashly again;
the truth is, I am not worthy
to be your younger brother.'
Third Sister retorted, 'I said I am not worthy;
what makes you unworthy?
If you are willing to call me sister,
I will call you brother.'
She immediately followed up by calling out, 'Brother!'
Huifang responded with, 'Sister!'
Third Sister then said, 'The other day,
I really did think you were a bit too forward.
Why did you ask about the Pan San affair?
What does that business have to do with me?
That was your brother-in-law's doing,
taking revenge for Third Brother.
I haven't even laid eyes on what Pan San looks like!
If you had asked anyone else that question,
it might have caused trouble.
Luckily it was me,
and because it was you asking,
I couldn't bear to get angry.
If it had been anyone else, do you think I would have let it go?
Brother, tomorrow I will give you a pair of sachets,
but you must not tell anyone I gave them to you.
If you do, it will just incite this one
and that one to come begging for them.'
Huifang thanked her.
They stood chatting a while longer before going their separate ways.
