Chapter 17 — At Zhu Fangnian's banquet, poets gather in splendor; in the floral register, supreme beauty crowns all fragrance.

When Ziyu returned from Sulan's and paid his respects to Lady Yan, he went to his study. After the evening meal, a round moon rose and cast its brilliance among the flowers; a mild breeze drifted in, and the sky and clouds shone bright all around. He rolled up the bamboo curtain and leaned against the railing to gaze out. Suddenly a page boy came in to report: "Masters Gao, Shi, Yan, and Wang have all come together." Ziyu had been brooding in his reverie; seeing them all arrive together, his delight was unbounded, and he invited them in to sit.

Ziyu recounted how he had called on each of them during the day without finding anyone at home. Wang Xun then spoke first: "Today we were all at Zhuoran's study together, where we met Tian Xiangfan and Meixiang, and then encountered Zhujun who came along as well. This Xiangfan is truly one of us — the way he and Meixiang conduct themselves together is truly enviable." Gao Pin said: "At this point Xiangfan has all six roots purified and all five skandhas emptied; he adheres strictly to the three-article covenant and is not allowed to wander and stray about freely. Now one truly believes that there are indeed many hen-pecked men in this world — no wonder Yongan and Zhujun guard their inner chambers so steadfastly, not daring to leave so much as a step without orders. Violations bring severe night-time consequences.

"And Xiangfan's is only a half-feminine, half-masculine partner — already he is brought to such a state. Is it any wonder about the rules in the Hall of the Four Fears?" The others were about to laugh, when Zhongqing said: "You too are from that household, and now that you've moved away, you boast." Nanxiang said: "I saw Zhuoran write to his wife — just like a subordinate submitting a report to a superior, with phrases such as 'deeply indebted to your grace, permeating to marrow and saturating every sinew.'" Everyone burst out laughing. Gao Pin said: "Preposterous! That lie of yours doesn't even ring true." Everyone laughed and joked for a while more. Then Gao Pin said: "Gengxiang, I have an excellent matter the day after tomorrow to discuss with you." Ziyu asked: "What matter?" Gao Pin said: "The fifteenth is Meixiang's birthday. Today we all discussed it and decided: Qianzhou and you will join forces as the two hosts, pooling a shared contribution, finding a spacious venue, inviting all those famous treasures to gather for a day of festivity, and hosting Xiangfan and Meixiang for a birthday celebration — how does that sound?" Ziyu said: "Wonderful, wonderful! But where should we gather?" Wang Xun said: "My home would work, but I have no garden; Qianzhou's garden would be better. We six as hosts, plus Xiangfan makes seven; Meixiang, Yaoqing, Xiangwan, Peijian, Jingfang, Ruixiang, Shouxiang, and Xiaomei together make eight — that would require three tables to seat everyone; the shared contribution cannot be fixed in advance, we'll reckon it up after." Everyone agreed. Ziyu sat blankly. Zhongqing laughed: "Yongan, you haven't done your duty in organizing this — you'll earn people's complaints." Wang Xun had not yet replied when Nanxiang said: "What makes you say so?" Zhongqing said: "Don't you see that Yongan, in his mustering of guests, has left out one most important person? Will that not invite reproach?" Nanxiang counted on his fingers and laughed: "Indeed, indeed."

Wang Xun said: "Are you not speaking of Xu Duxiang? I have not forgotten him — I only feared his affairs were too numerous and he might not be able to come." Ziyu said: "Duxiang has many social obligations, but given his character and manner, if we invite him, he will certainly come even if he has other things on. As for Xiao Jingyi, too — he absolutely cannot be left out." Everyone agreed: "Very well — add those two. That makes nine, together with the eight others, seventeen altogether — quite lively indeed." Nanxiang said: "No one else?" Wang Xun said: "Who else is there?" Nanxiang said: "What a memory you have — while convening this great gathering of flowers, you've forgotten the king of flowers. You have Gengxiang, but you have no Yunong — to leave him alone and excluded, what logic is there in that?"

Wang Xun said: "Quite right — I deserve a reprimand. I entirely forgot Qinyan for a moment; he must certainly come. And that Qin Qiguan, whose stage name is Yuyan — let's invite him too, to make it a round ten." Everyone agreed: "Even better." Ziyu said: "Now then, let us discuss how to invite the guests." Wang Xun said: "You write a short letter to Masters Xu and Xiao at Yiyuan, and to Qianzhou — the rest of us will go and inform the others ourselves tomorrow." So they chatted on until past the second watch before parting. After seeing the guests off, Ziyu sat alone in his own reflections for a while and thought: "The gathering the day after tomorrow will be a moment for the ages — I only wonder whether Qinyan's illness will have recovered in time. When that jade-forest gathering of ten takes place, naturally Du Ruopu (Qinyan) should be ranked foremost. I wonder how Master Hua's Huifang compares to my Yunong? Thinking of the manner in which Tian the gentleman was received — he is clearly a man of real talent and wisdom; there must be a deep feeling of another kind in him. Each person has their own strengths; there is no need to make comparisons between them.

So he gently ground the ink, slowly took up his brush, and wrote:

"The ninety days of spring are gone and past recall; two or three kindred spirits come unbidden to gather. Let us make a connoisseur's assembly of qin and cup, and try our mood amid birds and flowers. Though the site may lack the splendor of famous mountains, we rejoice that the courtyard is free of common weeds. We pity them — those of orchid essence — that they have fallen into the pear-garden world; we convene our bamboo-grove assembly, and count out the flower-tidings. The many work at refining the score; by chance they are alike to those who weave brocade. Their wisdom accumulated into heartfelt understanding — they would blush to be counted mere players of the money-counting trick. They have transported tender warmth into a cool temple; they have discovered elegance amid the dusty track. We celebrate the day when the pearl formed in its azure sea, and congratulate the moment when the jade issued from its Lantian hills. Famous gentlemen who have set the city aflame — how should they not cherish one another? The powder-red and the blue gown — they may well be placed on the same scale. Set out the thirty-six tiles of the mandarin-duck game, let the red beans be thrown together; prepare a thousand pairs of kingfisher-wing ornaments, let the purple clouds come and be welcomed freely. Respectfully, I address this note in advance of meeting. Set off your fine carriage early; together grace this humble lane. Mei Ziyu bows in respect. To the honored desks of Master Duxiang and the recluse Jingyi." When Ziyu had finished writing, he sealed the letter, applied his seal, handed it to a page boy to deliver to the gatehouse, instructing that it be sent first thing the next morning to Yiyuan, to invite Masters Xu and Xiao to come on the day after tomorrow to drink at Young Master Liu's residence — making the instructions clear and unmistakable. The page boy agreed, and Ziyu retired for the night. No more of that.

The next day, Wang Xun, Shi Nanxiang, and the others came to Liu Wenze's house and told him; Wenze was very pleased and said the arrangements would be made in the "Sword Reclining, Qin Sleeping" study (倚剑眠琴之室) tomorrow. As it happened, orchids and thoroughworts were fragrant, and peonies, crabapples, and the like were in full bloom. They then went off to inform the assembled beauties to arrive at the chen hour (7-9 a.m.) on the morrow. Wenze said: "Tomorrow among the flower retinue, I fear several may not be able to come. I know Qin Qiguan has an eye ailment; Du Qinyan is also ill and not yet recovered. He declined early this morning. Even if those two do not come, there will be eight — quite festive enough. Gengxiang and Jingyi will certainly be there." Nanxiang said: "For the drinking-game rounds at table, fresh ideas are few; overly difficult ones may put our guests at a loss in the moment. We need something that suits both the refined and the common taste — easy to understand and easy to execute — and yet avoids what is overly familiar. Guessing-the-object forfeits (shè fù) are also rather dull." Wang Xun said: "The linked-couplet game we did here before would work." Wenze said: "Doing that game again would feel stale as well. Let us wait until tomorrow and improvise as the mood takes us." Wang Xun and the others stayed for dinner at Wenze's that day, chatted for a while more, and then dispersed. Wenze also sent around word to each place asking everyone to come early tomorrow, to enjoy a full day's pleasure; all to come in informal dress, no formal caps or sashes required. The messengers returned with everyone's agreement.

Now let us speak of Tian Chunhang: since forming his bond with Huifang, he had not left the temple. Huifang came without fail once a day regardless of the hour — sometimes for leisurely conversation, sometimes for a small drink — and would often offer gentle words of counsel and encouragement. So Chunhang was already fully contented, concentrating only on his studies of the classics and on writing poetry and prose. By nature he was richly learned from long years of reading, able to produce ten thousand words as easily as a horse can cover ten thousand paces; his name had for a time made a stir throughout the capital, and those in power all wished to bring him under their patronage. But Chunhang, magnanimous and self-reliant, considered seeking powerful connections beneath his dignity; never once did he present a calling card to petition an important official. He preferred to dwell in the cool temple, with a beautiful person as companion and celebrated men as fellows. Through Huifang, he had also made the acquaintance of many fine friends — Xu Duxiang, Xiao Jingyi, Liu Wenze, Shi Nanxiang, Yan Zhongqing, Wang Xun, and others. Zhongqing had formerly been at odds with Chunhang — this had in fact been his way of spurring Chunhang on; after Gao Pin had explained the full circumstances, and mentioned too that Zhongqing had still sent the fifty taels for travel expenses, Chunhang naturally felt immense gratitude and respect. Zhongqing asked Huifang to serve as mediator; relations between them became even warmer than before. Only Ziyu he had not yet met in person. Knowing today that Wenze and the others were celebrating Huifang's birthday, his heart was immensely pleased; but then, their friendship having become common knowledge, he felt somewhat abashed about it, and was tempted not to attend. Yet he could not in good conscience disappoint the group, and had no choice but to keep his word.

Wenze had the "Sword Reclining, Qin Sleeping" study arranged from very early in the morning. Since the occasion was a birthday celebration, it required a degree of decoration: in the center hung a large painting of "The Immortals Gathered in Solemn Assembly." All the antique ornaments placed about were of the finest quality. Along the long gallery outside the blinds were ranged over ten pots of Cymbidium orchids; swaying gently in the mild breeze, their fragrance came in waves. Wenze was standing alone at the gallery's edge when he saw someone approaching from the front at a distance — he recognized it as Huifang, magnificently dressed, coming toward him. Huifang mounted the step-stone and at once began performing a full formal bow. Wenze hurried to raise him up and said: "Meixiang, why do you do this? It should be me who first offers birthday congratulations to you." Huifang said: "That the lowly anniversary of my birth should receive the attendance of all you honored persons is more than Huifang can possibly deserve. Yesterday I wanted to go around to everyone and decline with thanks, yet feared that would give offense — as if I were refusing to accept the kindness. And today is a great celestial assembly with all the immortals gathered: to allow those of our humble station to ascend with the chickens and dogs — though we cannot become immortals ourselves, at least we may escape the commonplace. That is why I have respectfully obeyed your summons and come with all humility." Wenze said: "This is also a grand undertaking of like-minded friends — gazing in admiration at the beauty that enthralls a city, we are merely offering flowers as tribute to the Buddha." While they were speaking, Lu Sulan, Li Yulin, and Jin Shufang arrived together; shortly afterward, Gao, Shi, Yan, and Wang came in a group. Huifang thanked each of them in turn.

While everyone was exchanging courtesies, they saw the servant who had received the letter lead Ziyu in. Though Huifang did not know him, his heart had already guessed who it was, and he stepped forward to bow in thanks. Ziyu held him back and said: "Is this Meixiang? I, Gengxiang, have long admired your name and yearned for a meeting; never having had the chance to see you in person, today being fortunate enough to stand downwind of your refined presence, I already feel fragrant beauty permeating my whole body." Huifang responded repeatedly with polite demurrals; looking at Ziyu's bearing, he privately thought with admiration: "Truly a sun and stars among men, a phoenix among mortals — there is an air of genuine, harmonious purity about him, bright and brilliant with a quality that stands above the crowd. No wonder Du Yunong is so deeply enchanted by him. Alongside my Tian — they might be called twin paragons, a Chu and a Liang born together!" Ziyu then greeted Lu Sulan and the others; suddenly they heard from outside: "Master Xu and Master Xiao have arrived!" Everyone went out from the hall together to welcome them; there came Ziyun and Cixian, gracefully as sons of great families draped in their furs, with Yuan Baozhu and Wang Lanbao following behind. Further behind were eight bright-faced and handsome young attendants carrying clothing bundles, bronze basins, and rinsing vessels.

Huifang stepped forward several paces to bow; Ziyun and Cixian each helped him up from one side and said: "Meixiang has always been easy and unconstrained — why so formal today? Surely you're putting us to too much trouble." They then entered the room and exchanged greetings with all present; all the young dan actors also completed their introductions, seated themselves by seniority, and settled down. Ziyun said: "Thanks to the kind invitation by letter from Gengxiang, Qianzhou, and all the gentlemen, today's occasion is truly a grand gathering. Yesterday I had my fill of reading Gengxiang's pearls and jade; even today my teeth still carry a lingering fragrance. And now we find ourselves in this great assembly of flowers, allowing your humble guests to follow in the rear and bask in the fragrance — truly a fine matter." Cixian said: "Colored phoenixes of the scarlet mountains, black-robed families of the deep lanes — no elegance of sash and sandal could surpass this. And yet may the cold-hilltop wild cranes also soar freely among them?" Wenze, Wang Xun, and the others all said together: "Duxiang and Jingyi — foremost among celebrated men, leaders of the literary and poetic assembly — there is no moment we do not long for your refined example. That you do not disdain our humble abode and have come so graciously today is all we could wish for in this life!" Nanxiang said: "Between friends, what matters is congenial rapport; in my humble view, there is no need to maintain the formalities of mere social calls."

Zhongqing said: "Zhujun is a candid and open-hearted person — his opening words show it immediately. Today's gathering is all the closest friends; it would be best to speak freely of what lies in the heart, without standing on ceremony." Then Gao Pin laughed and said: "Today the Queen Mother of the West has arrived early — it is only the Old Immortal of the South Pole who is coming late and late. Could it be that halfway here he ran into the somersault cloud of the Little Pilgrim (Sun Wukong) and was struck and injured the little longevity-star, making it inconvenient to walk? Or perhaps he slipped and fell again in a carriage rut." Everyone burst out laughing: "Zhuoran's wit is priceless — when the birthday-star arrives, let him be fined three great cups." Huifang seemed to blush slightly. Baozhu said: "How many guests are still to arrive today?" Wenze said: "Just the birthday-star, one person. Among the flower contingent, four are absent: Qinyan and Qiguan are both ill and sent their regrets early this morning; Guibao and Chunxi will certainly be here. Once Xiangfan arrives, we can be seated."

Before Wenze had finished speaking, Chunhang had already arrived. Everyone exchanged greetings afresh; all the beauties were also introduced. The banter and teasing naturally followed. Baozhu and Sulan drew out a red felt mat and placed it on the floor, setting up two folding chairs, intending to ask Chunhang and Huifang to sit together and receive obeisance. The two of them absolutely refused to sit; the mat was quickly put away. By now Chunhang and Huifang felt they were outnumbered and simply submitted to the general raillery; to answer back only invited more. Ziyu felt a deep admiration for Chunhang's free and easy bearing — and, paired with Huifang, the two were truly ice-jar and autumn moon, each enhancing the other's radiance. He also observed all the beauties in turn, each with their own loveliness — the only thing that diminished his pleasure was the absence of Qinyan, which left him with an unsettled flutter in his chest.

Wenze then gave the order for the servants to lay out three tables. He asked: "Today's seating — shall we go by seniority, or shall we put the birthday honorees at the head?" Chunhang and Huifang said together: "We absolutely cannot accept that — seniority is the natural arrangement." Everyone agreed to go by seniority. Wenze arranged the wines; the center table was first set in order. By age, Cixian was eldest; knowing he could not refuse, he took the seat of honor, with Gao Pin beside him, then Zhongqing. The left-hand table: Ziyun at the head, then Nanxiang, then Ziyu. The right-hand table: Tian Chunhang at the head, then Wang Xun, with Wenze as host. Three guests at each table.

Once the seating was settled, Wang Guibao and Lin Chunxi arrived and were introduced all around. At the center table, Shufang, Yulin, and Chunxi attended; at the left table, Baozhu, Lanbao, and Sulan; at the right table, Huifang and Guibao. The assignments made, everyone took their seats and began to pour slowly and drink at a leisurely pace. It was truly:

Scholars of the Jade Continent gather first before the Dragon Gate; the assembled immortals of the Jasper Isle pay court together at the Golden Palace. Embroidered hearts and brocaded mouths — pearls from the ninth heaven falling profusely; moon-shaped countenance and flower-petal skin — at every table the court robes are immaculate. No less fine than wind-soup and unicorn-dried-meat — drinking the wine that grants long life, wisdom certified through three lifetimes; no need for jade chimes and cloud music-frames — singing the song that transcends old age, each hoping to grow old together. The Jade-Capital Gentleman, the Cloud-Feasting Gentleman, the Wind-Riding Gentleman, the Phoenix-Driving Gentleman — in the crimson dust and the azure beyond, spanning the present life and past lives; the Eyebrow-Painter, the Gauze-Washer, the Song-Treader, the Lotus-Picker — iridescent phoenix and brocaded phoenix, manifesting in different forms. Surpassing the forest-and-mountain hermit who, taking the plum tree as wife and the crane as child, merely counts as romantic; sufficient to melt the heart of iron and stone — encountering just the flicker of an eye's corner or the curve of a brow, the ice breaks at once. Guessing games and drinking forfeits — printing your heart against mine. Golden waves and jade-clear liquor — try the lord's lips again against the lady's. Humor and play at will, turning everything upside down; brief arias and long songs at the table, exhausting all artistry and making white even of brown. Most are here to borrow the famous flower to express their pride — with no concern at all for the common people's welfare. With this they enjoy themselves without limit; youth has its own time of waiting. Rightly one feels the Western Garden's elegant gathering had only household entertainers; even the Winding-Stream Cup-Floating had no such intimate companions.

As cups and chopsticks flew and sandals and shoes lay scattered, every pleasure of a youthful and refined assembly was had to the full. Several were already in a state of half-collapsed jade mountains, like crabapple blossoms about to doze. At the tables, everyone's heart was at ease, every face glowing with joy. Only Ziyu kept thinking of Qinyan lying ill in bed — he knew he would be listless and depressed, his medicine-stove half-burned, his green window shut tight; he could not imagine the degree of his tedium. And he knew that Qinyan, aware of today's festive scene, would certainly be yearning for quiet and solitude. How he wished he could slip over there at this moment and offer a few words of comfort, brew tea and add incense, and help restore his spirits. And yet Qinyan might suspect that Ziyu, in the midst of pleasure, had simply forgotten his sorrows — and, thinking so in this grand flower gathering, would feel all the more wretched. To make matters worse, Sulan was right here too; otherwise, he might at least have gone to him for comfort. He sighed: though the scene before the eyes was full of people like clouds, yet he whom his heart sought was not here. At this moment Ziyu's expression grew somber; he merely pleaded drunkenness and withdrew from his seat to lie down by the heated kang, and no one paid him much attention. Besides, the food and wine had been plentiful and most had drunk beyond their capacity, so each left their seat and scattered to sit where they liked.

The servants cleared away the remaining dishes and set out fragrant tea and fresh fruit. Chunxi and Guibao went to the Taihu stone rockery and sat chatting by the peony hedge. Yulin and Shufang had drunk themselves into a stupor and lay asleep beneath the crabapple blossoms. Lanbao was fishing by the pond. Baozhu and Huifang were playing chess, with Sulan watching the game, while Nanxiang and Gao Pin stood by giving Baozhu advice. Huifang said: "Three of you against my one — even if you win it's hardly a surprise." Baozhu said: "I can win you without any coaching at all." Wenze said: "Today we have had real pleasure — it is a pity that two are absent from the flower contingent. The other is no great matter, but first and foremost Qinyan's absence has prevented Gengxiang from being fully at ease." Ziyun said: "Indeed! Qinyan's illness is rather mysterious — listless in spirit, no desire to eat or drink; it has been over ten days with no improvement." Cixian said: "Yesterday I took his pulse — it appeared to be accumulated fatigue compounded by pent-up emotion and melancholy. And yesterday there were specks of blood in his phlegm; without several months of complete rest he cannot recover."

Ziyu, lying on the kang, heard all this clearly and could not help feeling more agitated. Zhongqing said: "Today's occasion cannot be without some literary record. If Jingyi would paint a picture and write a preface to commemorate this fine gathering, we could all bask in reflected glory." Cixian said: "As for the painting — your humble servant will do his best. As for a high and classical composition, there are naturally the weighty brushes of all you honored gentlemen. The poor hermit's sparse and lean style is not suited to the luxuriance of the Golden Valley feast — it would only make the famous flowers lose some of their luster." Everyone said: "Too modest." Ziyun said: "Today's gathering arose from Meixiang as its inspiration, causing all the flowers to bloom in unison — surpassing the paper flowers of the Tang palace. My humble idea is that we first seek to admire all your honored ones' pearls and jade by composing a chain poem (liánjù) — what say you?" Everyone agreed: "Excellent." Chunhang said: "Classical (gǔtǐ) or regulated form (jìntǐ)?" Cixian said: "The regulated form is harder to develop fully; with so many people, it tends toward flatness and directness. Better to use the classical form."

So they went in order of age, with Cixian again taking the lead, followed by Ziyun, then Gao Pin, Nanxiang, Wenze, Zhongqing, Chunhang, Wang Xun, and Ziyu — nine people in all. Wang Xun had already roused Ziyu; Sulan produced a hangover-dispelling pill and gave it to Ziyu to take. Ziyu was embarrassed and had no choice but to make an effort to rouse himself. Sulan, seeing that Ziyu sat silent without speaking, in that not-quite-drunk state, guessed in his heart that it was because of Qinyan's absence. On the one hand, with so many people present it was not convenient to offer consolation; on the other, if she brought up Qinyan it might only stir Ziyu's inner preoccupations more, and rather than bringing relief would only deepen the gloom. So she moved away and went to talk to others. Wenze ordered the pages to place a small side-table before each person's seat, with a brush-and-inkstone set and several sheets of flower-patterned writing paper, the ink already ground. Everyone then asked Cixian to compose the opening line. Cixian said: "Let's set aside the theme of longevity (shòu)." He then said, "Forgive the presumption!" — raised his brush and wrote a line, then read it aloud: "The jade-tree sings in the clear dawn — orioles call in confusion."

Everyone listened, wrote their lines, and noted the character "jìng" (静, Cixian's courtesy name). It was now Ziyun's turn; Ziyun said: "Should each person not write two lines?" He hesitated briefly, then wrote: "Day by day the spring wind blows yet does not scatter. / Flower-scattering celestial maidens, fresh and wondrous." Everyone wrote them down, marked the character "yún" (云, Ziyun's name), and all said: "Beautifully connected — the third line opens it up, giving the imagination room to flourish." It came to Gao Pin's turn; without pausing to think, he wrote: "Cut silk as flowers, scattered to the heavens' halfway point. / Flower-heart and flower-face — the more spirited they seem." Everyone said: "Wonderful!" — and each wrote it down.

Nanxiang said: "This line needs to shift the rhyme. These flowers are after all different from true flowers; if one simply treats them as flowers, then what sort of flowers would Xi Shi and Lady Yang Guifei be?" He then wrote: "Yet one feels the flower-heart is somehow still short of the real. / Butterflies have males — who will discern them carefully?" Everyone clapped their hands: "Magnificent! This line establishes the boundary precisely, preventing vagueness and confusion. Zhujun is always the inspired one." Nanxiang said: "I dare not claim so — I merely read the topic clearly enough." It was now Wenze's turn; Wenze said: "This line needs to match well to provide a pivot, or else the energy will dissipate. That 'male butterfly' is rather difficult to match." He concentrated in careful thought. Zhongqing said: "Hurry up and hand in your paper — outside the music is about to begin with the door about to open." Wenze said: "I have it. 'Mandarin ducks, though small, always cleave to each other.'" Cixian and Ziyun said: "That matches excellently — both skilled and apt." Nanxiang said: "You did well enough with it." Wenze put down his brush; Zhongqing said: "How can you stop at one line?" Reminded, Wenze laughed: "You urged me on so quickly, I forgot." He thought again and wrote: "Creation's craft — selecting the flawless and polishing fine." Everyone said: "This line too is well produced — and the rhyme shifts again." Zhongqing continued and wrote: "Each one carved and engraved, setting eyebrows and eyes. / Spent ten measures of pearl from the dragon-palace storeroom." Chunhang's turn came; he continued: "Cleaved from the azure sea a pair of jade branches. / Little Jade vexed, Jade-Green grieving." Everyone praised again: "Fine! The distinction is kept clearly." Next was Wang Xun, who thought for a while and wrote: "The jade person fears too that eternity will take her. / Cicada-slender, suspected of stealing Chang'e's elixir." Everyone was about to praise this when Gao Pin said: "That's too vicious an insult — does this mean every one of them is like the rabbit in the moon-palace?" Everyone burst out laughing. Wang Xun felt embarrassed. The young beauties all scolded Gao Pin, saying: "Of all people, it is always him — never a good word his whole life. In the future let him be punished by becoming mute."

Gao Pin said: "How strange — other people scold you all, and I defend your honor, yet you scold me in return. Truly no good deed goes unpunished." It was now Ziyu's turn; Ziyu's mind was on Qinyan, feeling hollow and listless — as everyone well understood. Though Ziyu tried his utmost to conceal it, his inspiration was at a low ebb; he had no choice but to strain and write: "A glance in passing — that jade-girl's eyes turned back once. / Luan-comb sweeping gently across cloudy-green tresses." Chunhang said: "This takes us to the dressing scene — the continuation will need to describe things finely." Ziyu at this moment was thinking of the expression Qinyan had worn while performing "The Startled Dream," which accounted for the line "that jade-girl's eyes turned back once." The others did not understand why; they only saw that his spirits were poor and his mind not really on the poetry, so the lines came out somewhat flat and diffuse. It was again Cixian's turn; he wrote: "In the mirror, the lotus face — fresh from sleep. / The charming voice forms the white-ramie song." Next came Ziyun: "Lithe and pliant, knowing how to sing the red-gauze melody. / A clear and nimble beauty — touched by chance, the soul is lost." Gao Pin said: "'The soul is lost — alas, what can be done?' " And wrote: "In the Bronze Sparrow's deep spring — the greater and lesser Qiao. / Where flowers are joined on a single branch — the sisters." Nanxiang said: "Fine as it is, that Bronze Sparrow line is a little muddled." He matched it immediately: "Jade-like, a pair of discs — surely Qiongyao and jasper. / Slender waist swaying into the Magic Harmony willow." Everyone praised: "This 'sister flowers,' 'Qiongyao and jade' — the match is excellent. The poem's energy has been pulled back into shape." Wenze then continued: "A city-toppling beauty without equal in the world. / Melts even this world's heart of iron and stone." Everyone said: "Wonderful! This line needs a match of equal force." Zhongqing thought a moment, then laughed slightly and wrote: "Spring returns on earth, to the tottering old man." Chunhang said: "That is truly a marvelous match." Then he saw the next line: "How a single smile gives birth to a hundred charms." He continued: "At the banquet, silks compete — gold lightly tossed. / Zheng Yingtao — truly so remarkable."

Wang Xun matched: "Feng Zidu — his fame no empty claim. / Slowly the long days pass in the early summer." Wenze said: "Feng Zidu — nowadays there is a Feng Zipei; they seem like brothers." Ziyun said: "Feng Zipei is actually not bad; he has one particular trait — he simply refuses to take pleasure among the flower company." Wang Lanbao said with a cold laugh: "Of course he won't join our crowd — he actually gets annoyed when he sees us." Ziyu said: "Why so?" Guibao cut in: "He has his own feelings about it." Ziyu continued: "Brocade seat and flower banquet — day turns easily to night. / In splendor and beauty, worthy of the 'Same Carriage' ode." The second round came to Cixian, who wrote: "Yuan and Bai's poems — originally formed amid lotus gatherings. / On the red felt stage-carpet — passion and sentiment in abundance." Ziyun continued: "Practiced in singing 'Ding Niang's Ten Requests.' / The plain and beautiful — no humble part left unremarked." Gao Pin said: "Wonderful! For this line let me produce a good match." The young beauties, hearing this, suspected another jest at their expense and all crowded around to watch what Gao Pin was writing.

Gao Pin, smiling, wrote out slowly: "Rain and clouds — one travels all the way to Zhong'a." Everyone burst out laughing; the beauties all spat at him and pummeled him in a flurry. Ziyun laughed: "It is my fault — I provoked that line from him." Nanxiang said: "Even as playful writing, it should not be so cutting. Change one character and it can be saved — change 'traveled' (得) to 'how could' (岂)." The beauties finally agreed to this. Gao Pin said: "Very well — the wrath of the multitude must not be incurred." He then wrote: "Heaven-born loveliness — to be cherished and preserved." Nanxiang said: "A brigand reciting scripture, a butcher becoming Buddha — Zhuoran has actually developed a kind heart and knows how to cherish! That too is remarkable." He continued: "Let not the flower branch be lightly cast aside. / Would that we could, like Wang Xian, buy a Taogen." Wenze linked: "How laughable that Wang Rong bored through the plum pits." Zhongqing laughed: "Here we go again, straying into impurity — your hearts still have something unclean in them." Then he saw what Wenze had written next: "Once heaven gives birth to a fine jade youth." Zhongqing linked: "Must heaven and earth make the yin and yang wrong? / Only heard of the female rainbow becoming a divine maiden." Everyone said: "This is cautionary and admonishing verse — not vicious mockery. Such things do indeed exist in this world, though not among us." Chunhang linked: "Let not the male wind turn into a great king. / In the painted hall, the fine banquet is held day by day." Everyone laughed again. Gao Pin said: "Poetry expresses the will — the one who ties the bell must untie it. If I were to write this one, it would be wrong again." Wang Xun's turn came; he said: "We can close now — let it fall to Gengxiang to write the conclusion." He wrote: "Through the fan-base, peering at my lord — half a face glimpsed. / Picking up the piece of moonlight — a single bright sliver." Everyone exclaimed in praise: "Fine! It is time to conclude — this conclusion is no small achievement. The closing lines must be able to hold together the whole poem." Ziyu thought for a moment and wrote: "Snowflakes fly up on the jade branches bright." Everyone praised it unanimously as a powerful close, able to carry the whole poem in a single breath.

Cixian made a fair copy and read through it several times aloud: "It truly flows in one unbroken breath, with no sign of stitching or joint." He said: "Tomorrow I will paint a 'Picture of the Hundred Flowers Competing in Beauty' (群花斗艳图) — how would that be?" Everyone responded enthusiastically: "Wonderful! Why don't we now make a comparison between the people and the flowers — fairly assessed, without personal bias." So they deliberated together and decided: Baozhu as the peony; Huifang as the herbaceous peony; Sulan as the lotus; Yulin as the green peach blossom; Shufang as the crabapple; Lanbao as the rose; Guibao as the cotton rose. Chunxi, small in stature yet richly talented and beloved by all, was assigned the orchid. The eight assignments all felt fitting. They then wondered what flowers Qinyan and Qiguan should be. Ziyun said: "Qinyan's art and beauty are exceptional, yet his temperament is exceedingly cool — likening him to the plum blossom is most apt; and since his passionate love of the plum is not for Gengxiang's sake, then whose?" Everyone agreed: "Quite right." Gao Pin said: "Only I fear that Lin Hejing (the hermit who called the plum his wife) would not consent — Gengxiang would have taken away his leather-shod little son."

Ziyu could not help blushing. Zhongqing said: "And Qiguan?" Ziyun said: "Qiguan's temperament is fierce and upright, his appearance extremely fine — he somewhat lacks a gentle, flowing charm. Let him be likened to the chrysanthemum." Gao Pin said: "Chrysanthemums vary in variety — some white, some yellow, some red, some purple; the white ones are fine, but the rest tend to give a feeling of austere autumnal severity. Qiguan, though his temperament is fierce, has a tenderness in him that is quite winning. Better to liken him to the apricot blossom." Everyone agreed: "A fine apricot blossom — perfectly fitting." Wenze said: "Speaking of chrysanthemums, yellow or white — do you all know that in the eastern garden a new courtesan has recently arrived, called White Chrysanthemum (Bái Júhuā)? Have you heard anything about her?" Everyone said they had not.

Gao Pin said: "There is nothing in the world that I cannot find out. I know that she came from Guangxi with a company commander who brought her to the capital; the commander has now abandoned her and left the city, so she ended up in the pleasure quarter. She is actually rather fine-looking in a pure, unadorned way — hence the refined nickname. But people from the two Guangs seldom bind their feet; they all have natural six-inch feet, smooth and round, with a double-footed gait, walking like the wind. As a person, she is exceedingly vivacious and charming." Zhongqing said: "That explains your official title of Inspector-General of Intelligence from All Quarters." Everyone laughed again. Ziyun said: "After today's gathering, such a magnificent feast will be hard to convene again. On the eighteenth, Yaoqing is moving to new lodgings — would all the gentlemen be willing to move the wine-jars there for another gathering?" Everyone agreed: "There is absolutely no reason not to come — anyone who fails to attend will be fined to host a day's feast, and we'll have another gathering." Baozhu said: "I fear I don't have that standing; surely not everyone will come on my account." Chunhang said: "Why would they not? And besides, you are the king of flowers — these assembled blossoms will naturally come to pay homage. As for us flower-admirers, when we encounter beauty as striking as national-fragrance and heavenly-perfume, how could we not be eager and enthusiastic?" Nanxiang said: "Leave it to me — if one person fails to come, I'll be fined to host a day; two people, two days' hosting." Baozhu said: "Truly? After tomorrow's wine wears off, don't tell me you'll have forgotten." Only Ziyu sat in silence. The others went on talking and laughing; the bright moon was now directly overhead, the red lanterns about to burn out, and it was past the third watch.

Cixian said: "The night is already deep — we can disperse." So everyone rose; Baozhu confirmed the date of the eighteenth, everyone agreed, come rain or shine; then they each mounted their carriages and scattered in all directions. The next day, Huifang went around to call and express thanks; only Ziyu was ill and did not receive him.

On the eighteenth, all the celebrated gentlemen and the celebrated young dan actors did indeed come to Baozhu's new lodgings, from the wu hour (11 a.m.) until the zi hour (midnight). That day the entertainment was confined to drinking games and guessing forfeits, clear singing and clapper-beat — and they dispersed having fully enjoyed themselves. Among them Ziyu did not come owing to illness. Zhang Zhongyu was added to the company — always the most eager to please in a festive scene.

Qiguan was added to the flower register; only Qinyan had still not recovered. Gao Pin and Wenze held Nanxiang to what he had said — "If one guest is absent, I'll be fined to host a day." Since Ziyu did not come that day, Nanxiang was fined a day of hosting; Nanxiang was thoroughly happy to oblige. Another day's gathering was held at his residence. Only Ziyu and Qinyan had both not yet recovered. Truly: a few plum blossoms, tender and about to fall; the moon has dipped once more below the bamboo bridge in the west. As for what happened next — let the following chapter tell the tale.
