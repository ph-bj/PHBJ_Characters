Chapter 29 — A broken moon rounds again and true feeling smiles alone; in strict seclusion among pearls, who can pity divided longing?

As for Qinyan's coming to the Mei house—his heart had been terrified; he fully expected abuse this time. Yet after meeting Lady Yan—not only was there no scolding—there was pity—and she ordered him go comfort Ziyu—which he had not foreseen. Joy and grief surged; he did not know Ziyu's state—how he could comfort—only obeyed Lady Yan, braced his face, and walked to Ziyu's bedchamber. The curtains hung unrolled, the desk dusted thick, the medicine tripod smoking, the incense ash cold—a small nanmu bed with a sheer white gauze curtain. Yun'er first lifted the bed-curtain and called: "Young Master! Qinyan has come to see you." Ziyu was half asleep; he made two sounds—like answer, like not. Qinyan came to the bedside, sat on the edge, raised his eyes and looked carefully—Ziyu's face was sallow and thin—wasted. Qinyan leaned to the pillow and called low—tears burst like a spring—drenching Ziyu's face. Suddenly Ziyu gave a soft laugh and said: "'On the seventh day of the seventh month, in the Palace of Long Life—at midnight when no one hears whispering'—this is the very hour." He laughed twice more. Qinyan knew delirium—his heart ached—patted him twice—Lady was outside—couldn't say Gengxiang—changed to "Young Master!" Ziyu still in dream—thought it Seventh Eve—already met Qinyan at Sulan's—the three in the courtyard—fruit, tea, talk—so he recited those two lines from Song of Everlasting Regret—the dream soul so deep hard to wake. Qinyan saw him laugh again—heard him say: "I thought 'Yellow Springs and azure heaven—both hard to seek.'" At that he slapped with his hand—turned inward sleeping again. Qinyan's tears flowed harder—could only stare—dared not call again—saw Ziyu shake his head—"Such heavy rain—if tomorrow morning too—what then—the boat so far."

After a pause he said: "Below thoroughwort and stephania—I ought add one more dose of angelica root." Outside Lady Yan heard—knew delirium—couldn't parse all—still grief—two streams of tears—only stared blank at Pincai—Pincai too felt bleak—and said: "Yuyi—wake him." Qinyan called twice "Young Master!" Ziyu tittered: "How foolish you are!"—and said: "Yun'er—why keep calling—such a short road—what fear—you still think Great East Gate?" Qinyan wanted to shout—throat strangled—only patted him. Suddenly Ziyu opened his eyes toward Qinyan: "Xiangwan—once again you saved me—such heart—I can rest easy after." Qinyan leaned closer—hand on shoulder—"Young Master—Qinyan is here—are you better?" Ziyu's mind blurred—eyes colorful—couldn't see—sneered coldly. Qinyan repeated—Ziyu burst laughing: "You've tested me once—think I don't know you?" Lady Yan in the next room—heard—heart breaking—couldn't bear—came to door—saw Qinyan on bed holding Ziyu's hand weeping—Ziyu only laughing.

Lady Yan said: "He doesn't know people—what can we do?" Pincai had to come bedside—called several times: "Dear brother—the Qinyan you long for has come especially—I'll raise you to sit—you'll talk—be well." He told Yun'er wring a hot towel—wash his face—wipe eyes—raised him sitting—folded brocade quilt—propped behind.

Lady Yan would not enter—lest her son feel shame—and Wei Pincai kept his distance.

After sitting Ziyu felt somewhat clear—eyes suddenly sharp—saw Qinyan beside—and asked: "Who are you—sitting here?" Qinyan wept: "How can you not know me?" Seeing windows closed—and sitting back to light—naturally unclear—he shifted outward—half profile to Ziyu—"I am Yuyi—Madame sent me—I never thought ten-odd days—you'd waste so." Speaking—whimpered again—Ziyu heard clearly—heart jumped—straightened—looked—"You're Yuyi—I don't believe—how can you come—must this be dream?" Qinyan held tears: "I am Qinyan—Madame sent—why ill like this?" Ziyu sneered coldly: "You do resemble Yuyi somewhat."

Lady Yan hearing—said to Pincai: "Those words sound strange." She heard Qinyan: "I came for your sickness." Ziyu laughed: "You truly Yuyi—how could you come—even if willing—would they release you?" Qinyan: "I truly am Yuyi—I've been here long—Madame's order—Wei secretary brought me—I urge you ease mind—don't brood—body matters—heal fast—I've moved once—I can often visit." More tears fell—Lady Yan seeing Ziyu clearer—felt glad—had a maid move a chair outside the curtain—Pincai stood behind Lady Yan—Ziyu clearer still—leaned to Qinyan—studied—laughed: "Yuyi—you truly came—not false?" Qinyan turned to answer—choked—only wept—Pincai outside whispered: "Yuyi—steel yourself—don't draw more tears." Qinyan hid face with handkerchief—forced one sentence: "It's true." Ziyu: "Truly true?" Qinyan: "True—true—true." Ziyu wild laugh—lunged forward—fell on Qinyan's shoulder—still giggling without end.

Pincai couldn't help laughing—the maids and matrons all laughed—Lady Yan nodded and sighed—saw Ziyu both hands on Qinyan's shoulders wanting rise—first laughed awhile.

Qinyan said: "What illness is this—I urge you—don't be ill—from today heal—spare so many suffering you—and Madame worry." More tears—Ziyu laughed: "What illness—my illness—comes when it likes—goes when it likes—never mattered." Qinyan: "Don't say never mattered—this isn't before—your face all sickness—ten thousand words one line—ease your heart—you used say you saw through—why today not?" Ziyu laughed: "When did I not see through—if I didn't—I'd be thoroughwort's opposite—lucky I see through—there's still today." Laughed again—Qinyan: "I'm well at Hua house—Young Master Hua very proper—high and low treat me well—you needn't fret." Ziyu: "You truly well?" Qinyan: "Truly—ask Wei." Ziyu: "Good then good—why ask—" laughed—Qinyan: "Only your fast heal makes me better—slow heal—I worse—you perfect health—I'm immortal joy." End forced smile—Ziyu great joy—almost dancing—Qinyan: "There they allow leave—not like teacher's bonds—never came—now came—I'll often visit—if you well I sit longer—if ill I fear tire you—and I grow drearier seeing." Ziyu: "You truly can take leave?" Qinyan: "Today isn't leave?" Ziyu: "Wonderful—I thought once you entered—we'd never meet—never dreamed you could come—here—strange—wonderful—Heaven!" Clapped laughing—Qinyan wondered if this laugh too was sickness—heart sad—held in.

Now Lady Yan saw Ziyu only laughing—much of her gloom lifted.

She thought—if he could laugh so—his mind must open—and the illness could mend—yet Qinyan always wretched—she could not fathom why—Ziyu laughed again: "Inside—what did you do?" Qinyan: "Nothing—they keep me at Quiet-Green—the mansion's grounds—another world from Garden—rules may not be slipped."

"No singing needed—we do nothing—I only serve as a study page boy." Ziyu: "Did they know you left?" Qinyan: "He's often in upper halls—several studies—rest days—then one place—sits briefly—gone—servants don't enter those rooms unbidden." Qinyan usually spoke truth—today to cure Ziyu—threaded lies—said Hua house joyful—could visit often—only to ease Ziyu's heart—Ziyu hearing—mind steadied—truly happy—laughed—Qinyan: "Before—teacher—going out tired—never came—dared not—today I saw Madame—she favors me—orders frequent visits—under command—who blocks—free coming and going." Ziyu heard—brow knit—flustered—low: "You've seen Madame—she didn't scold—who brought you—permits entry?" Qinyan: "Wei secretary brought—I told Madame I can cure your illness—she rejoiced—instructed—if you heal—not only allow entry—often come—when lord returns—discuss buying me to serve you—asked willing—I said willing—only beg grace." Ziyu: "You never lied—today—lies to coax me?" Qinyan: "Don't believe—I'll fetch Madame—face to face." Stepped—Ziyu waved urgently—no—"every sentence true?" Qinyan: "When have I lied?" Ziyu nodded: "Never false." Steadied—more joy—great laughter—joy reached outer room—Lady laughed—Pincai swollen proud—low: "Nephew sees dear brother may heal today—credit Qinyan's teacher—though he vexed us—worth it." Ziyu didn't heed outer—attention thin—didn't hear—slowly stepped off bed—Qinyan helped—two steps—legs soft—laughed: "I'm well—I had no illness—summer muddle—head fog—they took for grave illness—fed medicine—more stifling—stifled to death." Called Yun: "I'm hungry—bring food."

Lady Yan lightly slipped out—Pincai followed—Lady: "Strange—strange—can't read those two little ones—today truly owed Qinyan—I'll reward heavy." Pincai grinned: "Rare—madame see—they're open—nothing shameful—today owed him—or Ye Tianshi reborn couldn't cure so fast." Lady: "Still nephew's power—called him out—credit nephew's—grateful." Pincai: "Dare not—nephew owes house favor—should serve—not just this—even fire water—" face beaming—Lady thanked—ordered Yun lotus porridge two bowls—Qinyan keep Ziyu company—Ziyu seeing Qinyan—joy—hearing words—all gloom gone—ate bowl—clear spirit—said to Qinyan: "I'm healed—you rest—leave early today—don't give gossip—hard to take leave later—I heed every word—you too take care—yield upward—better than singing—I thought never meet—today so fast—all worry gone—only shame never met Young Master Hua—or I could visit—Wei went with you—" asked where—Qinyan: "He came—called—raised you—missed—now upper with Madame." Ziyu low: "Past grudges—forget—friend him—today owed him." Qinyan nodded—hard parting—Ziyu finally few tears—Qinyan urged—Ziyu promised—saw to door—Qinyan: "you just well—don't come—I'll see Madame." Ziyu anxious—"speak carefully—not all truth."

Qinyan agreed—came out—upper hall—Lady smiled: "Today owed you—cured him—don't let him sick again." Qinyan flushed—paused—"Young Master bright—sees through—after—no illness." Lady studied him: "When return?" Qinyan: "Leave permits—come—ease madame." Sigh—"two little ones—I can't guess—today one cry one laugh—little said—if not witnessed—anyone would gossip—say I spoil—indulge son—nephew—you saw—funny—how could I be harsh—let him waste?" Pincai about speak—Lady turned Qinyan: "Only you understand—must rely you serve him whole." Qinyan low assent—thought: never thought Madame so merciful—others wouldn't—even loving son—not extend to me—deep gratitude.

Pincai saw time long—would return with Qinyan—Qinyan anxious—bid farewell—Lady: "Wait—I have words—" entered—called Xu Shun—weigh two hundred silver—"give Wei young gentleman—" Pincai had Four hold—servant brought bundle—Lady to Qinyan: "Madame rewards—take—then thanks." Silver knife—large purses pair—small pair—handkerchief—foreign watch—ten plum ingots—fan—Qinyan took—with Pincai thanked—Pincai vague thanks—out—Lady escorted corridor—instructed—gate Xu Shun grinning: "Today's affair—what cause—can't guess—" asked Qinyan's troupe—Pincai: "once Lianjin—now not acting—Hua mansion duty—cause later know—" Xu sent out—carriage—Qinyan thanked endlessly—Pincai modest—very pleased.

Entering city past shen hour—at gate—followed Pincai—superintendent struck register—Qinyan knew upper book—Pincai had Four carry silver to room—lock chest—found Lin Shanzhi—Shanzhi seeing Qinyan: "So long—must have healed." Qinyan ashamed—"Young Master summon me?" Shanzhi: "Summoned thrice—no return—great anger—sent for your teacher." Qinyan shock—face red—speechless—Pincai: "He can't take scare—don't scare—" Shanzhi grave: "Why scare—at wei hour Young Master emerged—missing—asked—I said teacher's birthday—Qinyan home felicitation—waited your descent to ask leave—heard you not coming—couldn't wait—asked me report—Young Master angry—if truly birthday—fine—if lie for other outing—I won't spare—sent man your teacher—investigate—returns—I'll be scolded—you worse—gate book shows Wei escort—Wei won't escape fair." Qinyan's heart hammered—eyes red—truth found—scold mild—shame worse—Pincai half believed: "Third—don't bluff—I'm clean—you sent—ask you first." Shanzhi sneered: "Ask me—I tell straight—what you plotted?" Qinyan tears—begged mercy—Pincai laughed—fanned—bowed—"Good brother—cover—brother's face—owe you." Shanzhi seeing real panic—chuckled: "Ease—true—not lie—arranged Zhu Gui—don't investigate—roam gate—return say truly teacher birthday—soon back—if you won prizes—split thanks—" Qinyan: "I'll give him silver." Shanzhi to Pincai: "Erase gate book—or seen later." Pincai: "beg Third Master magic—I can't ask." Shanzhi: "Too cheap you—yesterday two bolts gauze—I disdain—colors bad—return." Pincai: "gauze finest—change pattern can—" Shanzhi: "Enough clothes—undied dozen sets—I want give away—few taels favor—I lately saw two things—love—can't buy out—"

Before done Pincai pressed: "What things—I'll fetch." Shanzhi: "Sha Huizi's—gold wire flower basket—two liang—fine work—his Shi Dabin Yixing teapot—jade in lid—my thumb ring not match—basket offered forty—he agreed—teapot twenty-four—refused—tomorrow bring—Sha says teapot treasure—Shi Dabin century—spout drips not—brew with or without leaf—boiling water—fine tea—green—I dumped leaves—tested—true—odd?"

Pincai: "Old pots ripen—I'll buy both—gauze keep—gift." Shanzhi laughed: "Can't refuse empty—I'll accept grudging—" returned room.

At Quiet-Green Shanzhi asked—Qinyan outlined.

Shanzhi doubted—"Why ill for nothing—many saw your play—not all ill—your bond—or other?" Qinyan ashamed—couldn't answer—bucket in his well—swallowed anger.

As for Ziyu's grave illness—and Qinyan's excursion from the Hua mansion—Wei Pincai swelled with pride—thought it striking—and hawked it outside the walls until folk took it for fresh news. Actors who disliked Qinyan or lived to sow tales reshaped and embroidered until Ziyu and Qinyan sounded unspeakable. Wang Wenhui ran into Rongguan and Erxi at someone's feast and heard the yarn spun vivid as living dragons; he had seen Ziyu's sickness himself—thought it odd—but never guessed love-sickness—and listening now his heart sank—and he thought:

"Young men's affairs resist restraint—you only play along at the feast. Besides, Ziyu is only eighteen—the bud just forming—why sink into this? Last year we betrothed our beloved daughter to him—the first unhappy thing in marriage is wandering fancy—future peace between qin and se won't stay pure." Home he summoned his son Wang Xun—who answered vaguely—and argued Ziyu's side—said never so bad—Wenhui still doubted. Lady Lu overheard—though not yet wed—already vinegar for her daughter—and said to Wenhui: "Brother Yu's looks are superb—last year I agreed to Sun's match—now you wash hands—how well? Betrothal not sealed—already jokes—if he keeps debauch—how will Qionggu live—his father home can leash—his mother spoils—follow whims—later good-for-nothing—so young—shameless love for young dan—those old roués carousing outside—no wonder young men."

"What good are these young dan—have they no shame?" In her heat she lectured Wang Wenhui too—he only smiled—dared not speak—and said: "The matter is strange—I looked into it—even Xun knows—they've only met two or three times."

"Only longing for each other—nothing else—and children so young—how grasp such things—ease yourself—I'll tell cousin Yan—stricter gate—no outings—fine—marry this winter—once Qionggu crosses—maybe he'll leash." Lady Lu sneered: "Such habits come from the heart—I've seen—body leashed—not heart—thieves don't change nature." Wenhui heard himself targeted—laughed hollow—escaped to study—Lady Lu railed at daughter Qionggu—daughter silent—impatient—thought groom dissolute—"talented men trip short"—remembered brothers praising Ziyu's goodness—maybe only lust for beauty—blushed—pondered—Lady pitied—stopped—and left.

Again—after Qinyan's visit Ziyu was eased—knew his mother did not blame—and that she had commissioned Pincai to bring Qinyan—his heart rejoiced—he mended in under ten days—only before his mother he still looked ashamed—Lady Yan loved him finely—knew no shameful deed—and never spoke Qinyan's name—Wang Wenhui came several times—Lady Lu came—and before Lady Yan could say outright—needled with barbs—as if telling kin to watch—don't indulge—yet seeing Ziyu healed—handsome—graceful—a worthy son-in-law—and seeming honest—half her doubt melted.

Days later Ziyu still could not rest—begged his mother—on pretext of visiting Pincai—to see Qinyan—and met Pincai—begged Shanzhi—called Qinyan out—they talked more than an hour—then felt easy—but within the Hua mansion mouths were many—each heart its own—and after several days someone carried the tale to Young Master Hua—who grew furious—summoned Shanzhi—Shanzhi pled—got scolded—and afterward barred Qinyan from going out—assigned him to Crimson Wash Residence—given into the Ten Pearls maids' watching—cut off from outsiders—like a cage—the Pearls were kindly—yet Qinyan—young—and oddly pure—even guarded himself against them—so Young Master trusted his honesty among maids—treated him almost as another maid—forbade outside dealings—even Lin Shanzhi unless summoned might not reach him—to say nothing of others—Qinyan could only endure the long gate—by day chatting with the Pearls—few words—by night lone lamp—only resenting autumn wind and rain—as to what followed—listen to the following chapter for the explanation.
