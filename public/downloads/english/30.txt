Chapter 30 — Under moonlit lanterns, a banquet appraises the flowers; in trial costume and makeup, a brilliant stage piece is presented.

Of Qinyan's return from Ziyu's, the Young Master Hua knew no particulars, yet blamed him for quitting the mansion on his own. Thinking it a first offense, he waived punishment for the moment and reassigned him to the inner chambers—a secluded place isolated from the outside. One day, Qinyan sat alone beside the crystal rockery, weeping as he gazed at clusters of balsam flowers. He reflected that men live in this world, and if they cannot establish a name or achieve anything, mingling only with superficial people, the years will easily slip away and they will decay like grass and wood. Take the balsam for example: it blossoms, everyone finds it lovely, so they pluck it to place in a vase or pin in their hair. In a day or half a day it withers. They call it loving the flower, but it actually destroys it. But if the balsam remains planted here, the flowers leaning against each other like siblings, they possess a natural charm; even when wind and rain batter them, it is but a temporary trial that hardly harms their roots.

But if you uproot them and rear them in golden basins and jade urns, they lose their very nature. Then there are those who artificially manipulate them: trimming branches, plucking leaves, binding them with ropes and threads, pulling the grass, and peeling the moss. This pleases the eyes of men but diminishes the flower's natural color. How is this any different from imprisoning and shackling a person, making him afraid to laugh or cry?

Thinking further, a man is even less fortunate than a flower. The flower blooms this year and will bloom again the next, but a man deteriorates year by year. Just like me, fallen into this dusty world, trampled upon at whim, enduring every kind of affectation and tasting all sorts of hardships and bitterness. When the day comes that people despise me, I will be like a fallen flower swept into the privy or the mud—soiled and adrift with nowhere to end up. Does this thought not ache and bite? As he pondered, tears fell. He then thought of Yuxiang: though his illness had mended, I once fed him lies. If he knew my current plight—that he cannot come and I cannot go—I fear his old illness might flare up again. And if he calls for me then, I'm afraid I won't be able to go. Pondering over this back and forth, he felt dreary all day long. As days dragged on, time flew by; in a blink, over a month had passed, and the sweet fragrance of cassia filled the courtyard.

Today was the twelfth of the eighth month. Young Master Hua remembered the play at Yiyuan on the twenty-first of the sixth month, when he had said he would invite Duxiang over once the autumn air cooled. Thinking that the fifteenth would be a family banquet and inconvenient for guests, he set the date for the fourteenth, inviting Ziyun, Cixian, Wenze, and others. He arranged several spots in the West Garden and prepared lantern plays. Because they lived outside the city, a daytime gathering would definitely not be enough for them to fully enjoy themselves, so he specified a night banquet in the invitation. Today happened to be the second session of the autumn examinations. Why didn't Liu Wenze participate? The chief examiner this time was Wenze's father, Grand Secretary Liu Shouzheng, and the associate examiner was Wang Wenhui, who had been promoted to Grand Secretariat. Lu Zongyuan, Yang Fangyou, Zhou Xijue, and Sun Lianggong—this group of notable figures all served as associate examiners, which meant more than half of the well-known scholars had to avoid the exams due to conflict of interest. Among them, except for Xu Ziyun and Shi Nanxiang, who were already provincial graduates from the previous exam, and Xiao Cixian, who considered himself above worldly affairs and had no intention of participating, only Tian Chunhang and Gao Pin entered the examination hall. Ziyu, Wang Xun, Wenze, and Zhongqing all withdrew according to the rules. Ziyu stayed at home, brooding unhappily. Moreover, because there was no news from Qinyan and they were completely cut off from each other, unable to exchange messages, he wanted to visit Pincai several times. However, deterred by the strictness of the Hua mansion and the arrogance of its servants, Ziyu refused to go and could only sit quietly and gloomily in his study.

On the morning of the fourteenth, Ziyun discussed with Cixian: "Today Young Master Hua has invited us for an all-night feast and to view lanterns. There must be quite a lively scene today, and I heard five famous troupes will perform together." He then ordered his servants to assign attendants, check clothes and belongings, and prepare assorted treasures and tips. The steward got everything ready and had the family members double-check, to avoid missing anything at the last minute. By early afternoon, the two of them had already eaten their morning meal, dressed, and taken a carriage straight to the Hua mansion.\n\tMeanwhile, Young Master Hua had personally decorated various spots. The scenery in the West Garden was marvelous; though not as expansive as Yiyuan, its exquisite craftsmanship and splendor were quite comparable. The only drawback was its narrow footprint, being only a third the size of Yiyuan. The garden contained twelve pavilions. The West Flower Hall where Pincai had previously been was just the first entry point. A close friend of the late Lord Hua named Xie Lishan, an excellent painter, had spent twelve years designing it, achieving a perfect balance of light and shade, density and sparseness. As Young Master Hua grew up with a love for extravagance, he altered much of Lishan's original work. Now, it was entirely about dazzling embellishments, leaving no trace of rustic simplicity.

That day, Zhang Zhongyu arrived early. After having breakfast at Pincai's place, he spent half the day chatting and laughing with Zhang, Gu, and the others. Around noon, he pulled Pincai and Lin Shanzhi along to stroll through the West Garden. Zhongyu had only visited one or two spots before, and although Pincai had toured it twice, he hadn't seen everything either.

The beauty of this garden lay in its winding, layered pathways that connected the entire grounds. Covering twenty mu in a square shape, a pond named the Jade Belt River had been excavated. It meandered through six bends, with a bridge built at each bend, making six bridges in total. Along the pond were long corridors and winding pavilions, shielding and complementing each other from front to back, displaying beauty from every angle. Three or five small boats were moored at the shore. The bank along the pond was called the Little Su Causeway. There were many large trees in the garden, including twisting pines and tall bamboo. The artificial mountains came in two styles: the smaller ones were built from Taihu stones, intricately hollowed and exquisite; the larger ones were stacked with yellow stones, reaching several zhang high, covered in green moss and creeping vines, with pavilions leaning against them and flowers and trees setting them off. To summarize its layout: where there was water, there were mountains; below the mountains lay water; empty spaces were filled with houses, and the connecting areas were planted with trees. There were places where you couldn't see the sky when looking up, and places where you couldn't see the ground when looking down.

Zhongyu and Pincai, following Shanzhi, walked along the mountain paths. Following the high and low winding paths, they entered a fairy cave. Going up from the left side, past a few cassia trees and less than a dozen steps, they reached a curved corridor shaped like a concave 'U'. Light curtains and blinds half-concealed the interior. When Shanzhi led them in to look, there were seven rooms in total: the two side wings stretched out like wings, with three flat rooms in the middle, and large glass windows at the back, close to the edge of the pond. The furnishings inside were magnificent, and the place was named 'Returning Geese Islet'. Below were a few lines of postscript, written by Lord Hua himself. The two admired it for a while, then walked out through the small door at the west end of the right corridor. It was a small courtyard with a few spiritual rocks and banana trees. They saw a small building with an octagonal moon gate made of nanmu wood in an ice-plum pattern. Inside, a flight of zigzagging stairs led up. Once upstairs, there were carved windows on all four sides. Dozens of bookshelves were arranged inside, stacked and placed neatly, containing tens of thousands of scrolls, emitting a fragrant scent. There were two such buildings, forming a 'T' shape—one with three stories and the other with two, both with bright windows all around, and four large cabinets locked in the middle. Below, a long table was set, with a precious tripod emitting incense and vase flowers smiling brightly.

The three of them sat for a moment and then came down the stairs behind the screen door. Following a stretch of red railings, a row of weeping willows was planted below, with a few parasol trees in front. The pavilion they entered was extremely elegant. Several jade lutes were hung on the walls, adorned with ancient, colorful brocades; the tables displayed ancient bronze tripods and vessels, completely devoid of any vulgarity. After admiring it for a while, they walked down again to an open area right by the pond. The room was spotless, with tables and chairs made of Xiang bamboo and lacquered with an unpolished finish. Two rows of tall bamboo stood on the sides, the autumn breeze rustling through them movingly. A chess table was placed in front of the railing, with two foreign-lacquer chess boxes. Zhongyu said, "This place is quite secluded; it really washes away all the extravagant atmosphere." Shanzhi replied, "Although the Young Master loves liveliness, he actually enjoys tranquility as well." Zhongyu walked down the steps, went along the pond, and crossed the red bridge. Opposite was a white stone terrace with jade-like carved railings. On it stood a flat pavilion with three rooms, with Xiang blinds hanging down. Upon entering, they felt a dazzling light piercing their eyes and a chill penetrating their skin; it was a summer retreat. All the tables, stools, and desks had glass surfaces. On both sides were mica folding screens, and in the middle was a large tank of clear water containing some large goldfish, with a crystal mountain over four feet high placed inside. Now, in the cool autumn, it felt ominously cold. As they walked out, they heard the distant sound of watchman's clappers. Shanzhi said, "This is the servants getting ready. The Young Master is about to come out and the guests are arriving. Lest I'm needed, I'll go out first." With that, he left. Zhongyu and Pincai also came out, returning to the East Garden to change clothes and wait.

As for Young Master Hua's banquet today, it was held in three places: during the day, a banquet and theatrical performance in the Enqing Hall.

Between five and seven in the evening, a variety show at Xiaoping Mountain in the West Garden. At night, a lantern play at the Liuqing Retreat. Young Master Hua had already come out fully dressed and was waiting for the guests in front of the Enqing Hall. Right then, Xiao, Xu, and Liu arrived together. They entered the main gate, got out of their carriages, changed into sedan chairs inside, and went straight through the festooned gate, alighting at the main hall. Young Master Hua came out to greet them and exchange pleasantries. He opened the middle gate, and the host and the three guests walked slowly inside, passing through two more courtyards before reaching the Enqing Hall. Since this was Xiao Cixian's first time there, he looked around carefully. The Enqing Hall was extremely magnificent, with towering roofs and carved walls, grandly and beautifully furnished with colorful decorations. The foundation of the hall was deep and spacious. In the middle, facing outwards, were railings on three sides. Colorful curtains hung above, and a plush carpet was laid below—this was the stage. The retiring rooms on both sides connected to the backstage. The host and guests exchanged greetings again. Just as they were about to sit, Wei Pincai and Zhang Zhongyu came out. They all greeted each other one by one, and then sat down according to age. After some small talk and three rounds of tea, two men in sixth-rank official attire led four people in to set the tables, arranging two banquets. The musicians backstage began to play, and soon silver plates, golden bowls, and fine wine were served. Young Master Hua stood up to seat the guests. Ziyun, Wenze, and the others politely declined, suggesting they combine into one table and use a round one instead, but Young Master Hua insisted. Consequently, Cixian took the seat of honor at the first table, with Wenze next to him. At the other table, Ziyun took the seat of honor, followed by Zhongyu, while Pincai and the host acted as companions.

Today, the five great troupes were performing together. A man holding an ivory tablet came up to kowtow and ask them to select plays. Each person picked one, and they were performed in order. The opening acts were just the usual crowd-pleasers, which everyone knows, so we'll skip them. The host offered wine, and the four guests drank a few cups. Several dishes were served, featuring a spread of mountain and sea delicacies—it's impossible to describe all the rich, fresh, and savory flavors. Refined conversation and witty remarks were paired with humorous jokes. At the other table, Pincai asked Cixian about the sights of Yiyuan. Cixian briefly described a few places. Next came Baozhu, Huifang, Sulan, Shufang, Yulin, Lanbao, Guibao, Chunxi, Qiguan—nine in total—plus one more, performing the play 'The Flower-Viewing Assembly at Qinhuai River'. Some were demure, some coquettish, some quiet and gentle, and some romantic, exhibiting the utmost alluring charm. Everyone applauded. Ziyun, Cixian, and the others gave rewards right in the middle of the performance. Young Master Hua smiled and said, "This is plagiarized from my older brother's old writings; it feels very familiar." Ziyun said, "They sang very well. The demure ones were extremely demure, and the wild ones were extremely wild; no two were alike." Wenze added, "I've never seen them perform this play before." Cixian said, "The Qinhuai River has declined nowadays. Even Ma Xianglan's looks back then were only average, and there were remarks about her unbound feet not matching." Ziyun replied, "I've seen a portrait of Xianglan; she definitely had literary grace and charm." Pincai and Zhongyu echoed their agreement and they discussed it for a while. Young Master Hua's mood for drinking rose, and he urged everyone to drink heartily. Ziyun noticed that Qinyan was nowhere to be seen today, so he asked, "I heard that Qinyan is currently at your esteemed residence. Why don't I see him today?" Young Master Hua replied, "This boy's temper is a bit odd, but he's honest. He's now assigned to the inner study and will come out shortly." Ziyun looked around again and didn't see Lin Shanzhi or the Eight-Age Troupe either. He wondered to himself, on such a grand occasion today, why are these people missing too? Could they all be backstage getting in makeup? After the play ended, Young Master Hua summoned the ten Dan actors to come up and offer wine. They all came up together—plump, slender, delicate, or voluptuous, each beautiful in their own way. Ziyun saw that besides the nine, there was an additional one: Quan Gui from the Quanfu Troupe, who was also very alluring and charming. After paying their respects, they all held golden cups and went to different people to offer wine.

When Huifang offered wine to Ziyun, Ziyun asked how Chunhang's examination essays went. Huifang replied, "The other day, Shi Zhujun said his essays were very good and he will definitely pass." Hearing this from the other table, Wenze laughed and said, "I heard you burn incense and pray every day at home. Even if Xiangfan's essays are poor, he will still pass." Huifang smiled, "Who said that? If passing exams could be achieved by praying, I'd be willing to pray for everyone." Young Master Hua asked, "What are you talking about?" Ziyun was about to answer, but Huifang hurriedly poured a cup of wine to urge Ziyun to drink, entangling him so he couldn't speak. Young Master Hua stared blankly at Huifang, waiting for Ziyun to explain. Seeing this, Wenze said, "Let me tell you." Huifang shot a glance at Wenze, while Zhang Zhongyu laughed, "Meixiang, there are so many people and mouths here today; even if you want to silence someone, you can't silence all of them." Huifang replied, "Why would I want to silence anyone? I have nothing to be afraid of; say whatever you like." He smiled and walked over to offer wine to Wenze. Over there, Young Master Hua rewarded Baozhu with a cup of wine, which Baozhu drank and expressed thanks for. Young Master Hua said, "The singing in today's play was excellent too; whether lightly powdered or heavily made up, each suited their role." Baozhu smiled but said nothing. Young Master Hua then asked about Huifang's situation. Baozhu smiled, "I don't know." Young Master Hua laughed, "You all protect each other; it's so detestable. I will definitely find out eventually." He then called Huifang over. Huifang had no choice but to comply. Young Master Hua said, "I'm impatient and can't stand confusing matters. Whatever hidden situation you have, why must you keep it from me?" Huifang lowered his head and said, "Young Master, don't listen to their nonsense; they are just making fun of me." Ziyun smiled and said, "Meixiang, everyone outside the city knows about your affairs. Even inside the city, I'm afraid some people know too. Why not tell the Young Master?"

Huifang said, "What do I have to say?" Zhongyu suddenly laughed, "When you were in a crisis, you used someone as a shield, and now you are being ungrateful." This puzzled everyone. Huifang paused for a moment, his face unwittingly flushing red. Young Master Hua saw this and, recalling what was said a few days ago, felt a bit of pity. He guessed there was some hidden matter that was hard to speak of, and decided he would slowly ask Duxiang later, so he changed the subject. Amidst the chatting and laughing, they drank a lot of wine, watched several plays, and had two courses of snacks. Young Master Hua stood up and said, "Let's take a stroll in the garden." Cixian and Ziyun agreed, "Very well, we've already had plenty of wine." The guests all stood up together. Four or five servants hurried through a shortcut under the corridor to inform the garden gatekeepers to get ready.

The East and West Gardens flanked the main hall, with doorways connecting them everywhere. Young Master Hua led the group through the covered walkways, bypassing several courtyards until they reached a winding corridor. There, inset into the wall, was a woodblock carving of Zhu Zhishan's cursive script, over six feet tall. Just as everyone was about to admire it, a servant came over and pushed it; it turned out to be a door. Ahead of them was a vista filled with green shade and the murmuring sound of water. To the left were rocks, and on the earthen slope grew dozens of cassia trees; to the right, a winding stream flowed, its banks overgrown with bamboo and trees, blocking the view.

Above sat a small pavilion with three rooms. Inside, a plaque read 'Deep Pool and Mountain Dwelling'; it was extremely tranquil and elegant.

Ziyun and the others were about to sit down, but Young Master Hua ushered them further inside. Walking behind a screen, they found a space that was narrow on the inside and wide on the outside, shaped like a fan on three sides. Several burners of fine incense were lit in the room, and fragrant tea was served. As everyone sat down, they felt the autumn scenery was like a painting, cleansing their hearts and minds. Ziyun saw Qinyan, who was now lowering his eyebrows and bowing his head, lacking his former proud demeanor. Zipei continued:

Young Master Hua said, "Strange! He actually looks like a fine woman; today he's going to outshine all the beauties." Hearing the praise, Zipei felt a bit bashful. He saw Huifang and Baozhu standing next to Xu Ziyun; noticing Huifang looking at him, he leaned over to exchange a few words with Ziyun, and then with Baozhu, seeing Baozhu smile. He also saw Liu Wenze and Xiao Cixian standing together, whispering in each other's ears, probably discussing him. In fact, Wenze and Huifang were not talking about Feng Zipei at all; they were talking about Gui Zirong.

This Gui Zirong was originally from Jiangxi but registered in Zhili, and had earned the title of Xiucai. In his youth, he was quite handsome. He was now twenty-seven or twenty-eight years old, and had a history of many shady dealings. His family was originally wealthy, his father having been a great merchant. In his youth, he had bought his way to becoming a Juren, and later purchased a position as a secretary in the Grand Secretariat, currently enjoying life in his hometown. Zirong had been in the capital for eight years without returning, squandering a vast amount of money. He already had a wife at home, but lied that he was a widower and married Wu Dashan's sister. Still unsatisfied, he took Ye Maolin's daughter as a concubine and lived separately in the southern part of the city. When the Ye girl was at home, she was not one to abide by the rules and loved to socialize, but Zirong doted on her obsessively. Her dowry was quite substantial, but Zirong squandered it recklessly, and within two years it was completely gone.

Neither of the couple could endure poverty, which inevitably led to quarrels and mutual recriminations. The Ye girl then began to flaunt herself at the door, flirting and selling her charms, and those old acquaintances gradually started visiting again. At first, Zirong was furious, but later, seeing the money roll in, he accepted it and openly played the role of a pimp, even hiring a helper at home. The scholarly circles had long despised him, yet Zirong often boasted of calling a certain Censor his mentor and a certain provincial graduate his study mate. And a group of shameless lechers also wanted to mingle with him. The residence of Gui Zirong and the Ye girl was close to Huifang's, so Huifang knew the details very well. Liu Wenze had also gone there to drink. However, when people went to drink, Zirong insisted on acting as the host and keeping them company, so some people were unwilling to go.

Zhang Zhongyu was even more familiar with him, only Pincai didn't know about it yet.

Young Master Hua disliked hearing about these matters, so he ignored them and just focused intently on Zipei. Suddenly, he called for a large cup of wine. A servant brought a large jade goblet, and Young Master Hua told him to present it to Ziyun. Whether Ziyun drinks it or not, listen to the next chapter to find out.
