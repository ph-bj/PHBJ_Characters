Chapter 31 — After drink, the flowers keep the night moon in lingering company; old sorrow returns as scholars sing of autumn graves.

Young Master Hua—watching his delight—raised wine to Ziyun and company—the whole table drained a cup together—praising Pincai and Zipai for acting beyond mortal players—beyond common troupers' reach.

Soon both left the stage—Zipai pointed at Wenze and swore: "You can't tell good from ill—I sweat on stage—you sit judging my every fault." Wenze protested: "When did I judge you—picking blind faults? I chatted with Master Jingyi." Cixian said: "True idle chat—besides you sang so superbly—we couldn't stop praising—what critique?" Young Master Hua laughed: "I heard them say—you truly resembled Yan Poxi—had you woman's form—you'd be restless too." Zipai: "Fine—you drag me on stage—then mock." Xu Ziyun asked Pincai: "Brother Wei—your music is exquisite—I must study later—when free visit my garden." Pincai bowed: "I had no teacher—all learned by ear—first time on stage—don't laugh." They guessed fists and drinking games—made merry—clock struck midnight—Ziyun said: "Only autumn equinox—nights shouldn't run short." Cixian: "Feels long—try sitting alone till now—quite tedious." Ziyun: "We've crossed midnight into fifteenth—dawn nears—clear the feast—stop opera—talk till light—we'll scatter." Zhang Zhongyu: "City gates already open—could leave now." Young Master Hua: "What's the rush—scatter at chen is fine." Ordered feast cleared—opera stopped—servants brought tea—Ziyun noticed Qinyan gone—only Shanzhi leaned on screen weary—Young Master Hua asked—Shanzhi said: "Unwell—asleep." Truth—Qinyan in crowds recalled his cares—grew sore—seeing Feng Zipai act with Pincai—despised them—asked Shanzhi plead illness—and left.

Young Master Hua summoned the dans again—no stage hats or robes—everyday dress—seated by age in a row—said: "Beyond opera each of you excels—verse or painting or calligraphy or silk-and-bamboo. Today and last time wine and drama blocked your gifts—still early—who writes—compose lines—who paints—brush a few strokes—no modesty." Dans silent—Ziyun and Wenze rose: "Splendid—we'll assign." To Huifang: "Meixiang excels verse—Yaoqing painting—Jingfang sword-dance—Xiangwan calligraphy—Peixian lyrics—Ruixiang riddles and wit—Shouxiang vertical flute—Xiaomei mouth-organ. Pity Yuyi ails—yet he plays "Wild Geese on the Sand"." Young Master ordered him roused—told Shanzhi fetch pipa—servants spread brushes instruments at stations. Cixian said: "I'll marshal generals—first Yuyi and Shouxiang blend qin and xiao—Yaoqing paints—Meixiang, Xiangwan, Peixian improvise poems on the scene—inscribe—then Shanzhi and Xiaomei sheng and pipa in contest—Ruixiang riddles and jest—last Jingfang sword-dance—bright as Yuyang drum—how say you?" All approved—dans performed in order.

Qinyan—no choice—drew brows tight—matched strings—Shufang blew xiao by score beside him.

Qinyan's mind was troubled—fingers rusty—he ground through one set "Wild Geese on the Sand"—halting and harsh.

The xiao flowed mild. Young Master Hua shook his head: "Qin poor—xiao fair." Ziyun: "Qin is hardest—still credit him." Cixian: "You seldom practice—rust showed." Qinyan: "Half a year idle—fourth movement third phrase nearly forgot—Shouxiang's flute improved on before." Shufang: "I study under master—Master Jingyi tutors my blowing every third day—so no rust." Qinyan silent—past and present ached—tears nearly fell—drew back standing—Cixian and Ziyun pitied deep.

Meanwhile Yuan Baozhu spread silk on painting desk—pondered left and right—what to paint?

Long thought—no theme. Huifang and Sulan whispered before him: "What will you paint—we'll fix themes—draft in mind." Baozhu stalled—pulled them to railing—consulted—time pressed—couldn't use fine gongbi—few strokes orchid bamboo wouldn't suit scene. Huifang: "I have a theme—unsure if it fits?—paint a small handscroll—ink a few towers—add thickets bamboo—near and far—moon above—wash indigo clouds and mist—tonight's air—title 'Fine-Night Wind and Moon'?" Baozhu rejoiced—bowed behind others—seated—bold—three craft seven freehand—Wang Lutai manner—brushed freely.

Cixian and guests dared not crowd—feared spoil his work—watched from distance—brush moved fast—unfettered—already brightening face.

Huifang's party of three clustered—Li Yulin bowed in thought—Sulan told prayer-beads without end—Huifang watched Baozhu's brush—hinting still.

Within half a shichen—done—two feet odd small horizontal scroll. Young Master Hua and Ziyun drew near—unceasing praise. Young Master delighted: "What labor—how achieve this—no wonder lately all praise their talent and looks—puts the old money-grubbing xianggong to shame." Cixian touched up a few spots—fine painting. Young Master asked Huifang: "Your verse ready?" Huifang: "We have—but poor." Stood by desk—found paper—wrote quatrain. Young Master read: Fine night lanterns and moon—autumn light—silk-bamboo clamor both wings. I said Consort Chang'e fears solitude—sent wind to waft her to splendid hall. Clapped marvel—Cixian Wenze Ziyun praised: "You romped all day—he sums in twenty-eight words—more than enough—we could not." Read twice—admired.

Wenze: "Superb—third line wants a tweak." Huifang: "Edit for us." Wenze: "Try 'Perhaps Chang'e feared lonely still'—mood more remote." All cried fine. Zhongyu and Pincai secretly startled—never guessed such depth—suspected ghost writers—tonight's test proved real. Feng Zipai never looked—heaped osmanthus from plate—got red thread from page—held one end—page other—threaded blooms—till great ball formed—tossed light—hung on Young Master Hua's lapel—Young Master plucked—smelled—laughed: "Your labor—you keep it." Zipai took—back to fiddling pipa. Sulan and Yulin also finished. First Sulan's: Gold cups brim jade nectar thick—autumn glow gentle as spring face. Chang'e's palace gates unlatch tier on tier—light pierces pearl blinds twelvefold.

Young Master praised same: "Matched craft—like Yuan Zhen and Bai Juyi in one age." Ziyun: "Two characters shift—third line 'Chang'e' repeats first poem—better 'Cold Moon palace gates unlatch tier on tier'?" Sulan: "That improves." Ziyun had feared Sulan short of Huifang—now eased. Yulin's lyric—"One-Trace Sand"—tender dance deep courtyard—brocade curtains screens soft—pearled guests three thousand—gather worthies—if moon has heart—hold fast—if man has heart—do not depart—

Hear not dawn cock—random cries. Readers beamed—praised—"Such tender mouths—alive on paper—who could not adore." Young Master: "Fine—yet I'd swap—'gather worthies' → 'meet immortals'—'random cries' → 'only third watch'—good?" All: "Good." Cixian told them ink quickly—Huifang Yulin asked Sulan copy—Young Master refused—they wrote themselves—admired again—sat listening—Shanzhi's pipa and Chunxi's sheng—Shanzhi leaned—struck sandalwood frets—fingers like rain on plantain—tone like shoal-water—spring chaos filled hall—sheng answered high low—every limb thrilled—they played "Moon Ascending High" suite—praised again.

Wind instruments done—Guibao's riddles next. Guibao: "Others guess me—or I guess others?" Young Master: "I'll guess yours." Recited offhand: "Green ripples smile shallow—uneven—this spring later than last. I say Bashang poet's mood thin—better Red Cliff night boat time." Guibao pondered—laughed: "Master names wind flower snow moon—masterfully wrought." Young Master: "Sharp—first guess." Zipai: "Mine—unused wrapped thousand layers—in use shattered bone—who knew black heart inside—still vaults sky." Guibao: "Firecrackers." Young Master: "Such broken riddle?" Zipai: "Why broken?" Young Master: "Crackers must bang—yours silent—use?" Laughter. Pincai: "My broken one—guess—thunder rage shakes earth—gone never returns—like battlefield sigh—ancient wars—when return?" Guibao: "Also crackers." Zhongyu: "You mocked Zipai's mute—so your first line leads with noise." Dawn breeze—bells tolling—east whitening—Young Master urged Lanbao sword-dance—Lanbao rolled sleeves—drew bright blade—opened stance—rhythm rose—light blazed—lastly cold gleam—man invisible—cheered—done—crimson sky—sun rising—Mid-Autumn—each had business—rose to leave—Young Master saw them out—dressed to escort.

Ziyun distributed trinkets among dans—guests scattered—Young Master Hua escorted to vestibule—only Feng Zipai exhausted—asleep on Quiet-Green couch—Pincai to his rooms—Young Master told pages tend Zipai—then inner chambers.

The dans agreed to leave city together—pause that thread.

Fifteenth passed—sixteenth morning Wang Xun and Yan Zhongqing brought Shi Nanxiang to visit Ziyu. Since mid-seventh moon recovery—twenty-eight days nursing—Ziyu was strong again.

Knowing Qinyan trapped Hua mansion—could not emerge—he nursed world-weary mood—banquets and opera—never joined—sat alone with tea bowl and censer—spun round them. Called renunciation—heart still bound—thought of Qinyan—tears streaming—couldn't loose. Fifteenth visited Wang Wenhui—Xun Zhongqing set next noon for Tian Chunhang and Gao Pin. Ziyu breakfasted—waited study—soon Shi Yan arrived—Nanxiang sat—talked with Ziyu—Zhongqing Wang Xun inner first—Lady Yan—tea—then urged Ziyu go.

Carriage waited—Ziyu changed no clothes—Yun'er feared cold—wrapped extra cotton—rode to Chunhang Gao Pin lodgings—both home—entered together—Gao Pin's laughter—birdsong within—Chunhang study—Su Huifang Li Yulin within—Gao Chunhang delighted—seated four—Su Li greeted.

Brief talk—Zhongqing asked exams—Chunhang Gao Pin confident. Zhongqing: "Xiangfan's essays I've seen—sure success—Zhuoran's—show—must tower above." Gao Pin: "Poor—don't look." Wang Xun: "Nonsense—show anyway." Nanxiang: "Three topics—Zhuoran shines—even unseen fine." Ziyu: "Still look—several approaches—some annotations fit—some not." Gao Pin: "That day mind fog—no characters—night scribbled—submitted—done—first essay draft exists—somewhere—" fetched—verses attached—Nanxiang half—stopped—Wang Xun: "Superb—short—four hundred odd words." Ziyu: "Brush mature—tone high—rare in hall." Gao Pin nodded Ziyu: "Yuxiang has eye." Zhongqing: "Zhuoran honest verdict—good or bad?" Gao Pin: "Good works—bad works—halls don't judge essays—pass no fluke—fail no injustice." Zhongqing asked Nanxiang Xiangfan—Nanxiang: "Xiangfan tops list—Zhuoran might strike slack reader—or head case—who knows." Zhongqing shook head—Gao Pin tore manuscript: "Fortune fixed—don't debate—I'll pass one to spite you—talk else." Silence—knowing Pin vexed—changed topics.

Huifang told Hua mansion verse and painting night—detail—delighted all—had them recite—praised—especially Yulin's lyric finest. Gao Pin: "Strong general—no weak troops—Peixian's opening lyric—could those scholar-cap hacks match?" Ziyu: "True—even Young Master Hua's edits fine." Asked Qinyan—Yulin Huifang told—Ziyu froze—Gao Pin's boy summoned him—guest—Pin left long—Nanxiang: "Who talks so long?" Chunhang: "Lately Pin odd—seekers vulgar—lots of silver packets—don't know whose." Zhongqing: "Pin broke—must sell essays—that toss-off piece—why else rush." Nanxiang: "He said he'll pass—means it." Pin returned—all hushed.

Huifang: "Rare all gentlemen here—I'll host today—what say?" Wang Xun: "Good." Gao Pin: "Xianggong—not hitting us for subscriptions?" Huifang laughed: "Caught—I want subscriptions." They thought jest—agreed—Huifang ordered feast from restaurant—servants spread—Huifang seated them solemn—all wondered: "Meixiang stern—subscriptions real?" Huifang: "When drank xianggong wine free?" Laughter—Pin: "Missing Pan San." Huifang spat—poured rounds—Yulin helped—dishes.

Huifang drew papers from boot—like essay—handed Nanxiang first: "Master Zhujun—I beg subscriptions for this—read—I'll explain." Crowded—title "Biography of Master Fragrant-Snow." Page-boy brought bundle—opened—"Fragrant-Snow Posthumous Draft"—two volumes verse and prose. Nanxiang read line by line—Huifang's teacher who taught letters and opera—once scholar—failed exams—burned brush—entered troupe—great writings—Huifang came capital—kin as bone—all arts from him—died date—coffin traveler—lodged south-city Shoufo Temple—widow and son arrived destitute—learned Fragrant-Snow dead years—Huifang emptied purse—two hundred taels—beyond travel enough for household—asked Xiao Cixian portrait and poems—son Yuanzuo thirteen—too poor for school—bright—overhears tutors—memorizes—Five Classics half learned—Huifang wished notables verse epitaph biography—honor hidden virtue—raise descendants—reading—Huifang wept—listeners stirred—stood: "Such righteousness rare—we'll wash hands and write—praise him—such pupil and son—even poor death—no regret." Chunhang Ziyu doubly praised—Nanxiang: "You wrote this biography?" Huifang: "Facts—needs polish." Zhongqing: "Xiangfan could refine."—"I'll elegy." Wang Xun: "I'll elegiac poems." Nanxiang: "I'll tomb inscription." Chunhang: "expand his draft—I new biography?" Huifang: "Better—his draft mere outline." Ziyu: "Big pieces yours—I write praise." Gao Pin: "I'll funeral ode—heavy grief." Zhongqing: "We disciples gather—twilight quiet—offer sacrifice—pool travel cash?" Chunhang agreed—all treated Huifang serious friend—little jest—Nanxiang: "When soul departs?" Huifang: "Midnight thirtieth—twenty-ninth deep night." Nanxiang: "Essays soon—printed collection—dozens copies—homeward—subscriptions each means—or Du Xiang Jingyi Qianzhou contribute—

We'll gather twenty-ninth second watch—one rite—done—other disciples I'll arrange." All: "Good." Huifang: "Sacrifice unnecessary—I dare not—temple cramped—your brush honor enough—outside gossip—would shame you." Nanxiang: "No harm—scholar circles know—decide nearer—Xiangfan needs this—we follow tail." Huifang's plea stirred pity—little joy—first watch scattered.

Next day Nanxiang Zhongqing wrote Ziyun Qianzhou etc.—days later subscriptions arrived—verses—counted—Ziyun twenty-four—Wenze sixteen—Cixian twelve—fifty-two taels total. Zhongqing: "We six shares—eight taels each—hundred taels enough." Nanxiang: "Plenty." Letters sent—all pooled in days—writings and anthology carved—hundred copies—sixty taels—Huifang paid alone.

Flower-register pupils of Fragrant-Snow—four remain—Yuan Baozhu Wang Guibao Jin Shufang Lu Sulan—painting or verse—all advanced—these four pooled hundred taels—with Huifang four hundred total—mother son old servant three—boat canal home—ample.

Twenty-eighth Zhongqing visited Nanxiang—planned morrow—"some won't come—Yong'an timid—fears Father learn—Yuxiang needless—ruined temple midnight—can't ask him." Nanxiang: "Idea—rite pretext—country stroll—daytime bring wine—after offering—Brook Autumn Mound Haoran Pavilion—meet xianggong—fine?" Zhongqing: "Excellent—I missed—if Yong'an Yuxiang absent—we four suffice." Went Chunhang—set noon feast waiting.

Next morning Nanxiang Zhongqing rode out—Shoufo Temple gate—dismounted—grooms tied horses—five-six carriages parked—entered—ancient shrine desolate—grass knee-deep—mules grazing—broken walls wells—tilted Buddha images.

Entered Amitabha hall—empty—great Buddha hall west corner collapsed—wind shook trees—leaf heaps—desolate—one man emerged rear—Zhongqing knew Huifang's servant—hands down—asked where—"Behind—follow." Past hall—west door—ruined loft—no doors windows—beyond three huts—stacked coffins—twenty-thirty—one coffin—small table—incense candles—that must be it.

East courtyard—another small door—three-four rooms—Chunhang Gao Pin Huifang party inside—old monk with them—Chunhang Huifang came out.

Nanxiang: "Grim place—no wonder some refused." Huifang dragged bench—sat—Zhongqing: "All here—pour libation—then Brook Mound stroll." Huifang spread offering in room—Huifang incense—Sulan wine—Shufang pitcher—Baozhu dishes—Guibao paper—Chunhang Nanxiang Gao Pin bowed together—five dans kowtowed thanks.

All restless—ordered pack—one string cash to monk—left temple—Nanxiang Zhongqing mounted—others carriages—porters poles—straight Brook Autumn Mound—sparse woods yellow leaves—bleak everywhere.

About a li—reached mound—hill-like—temple above—summit great pavilion Haoran—gaze round—dozens li walls villages—interesting. Chunhang: "Today's desolation pains—here mind clears." Gao Pin: "Never visited Brook Mound—Zhujun you toured?" Nanxiang: "First time—read ancients' poem on mound—far—who comes?" Zhongqing: "Good change—always bustle—need cold spell." Nanxiang: "Shoufo cold enough—Jiantan—you said only pure hearts pierce silence—only dull wills probe depths—dare lodge that temple nights?" Zhongqing laughed: "At that pass—must lodge—moonlit calm—maybe fox ghosts visit—not bad." Gao Pin: "Jiantan loves contrary talk." Sulan: "Alone—daytime I dare not." Guibao: "Many broken coffins—nights must haunt." Shufang: "Monk one novice one altar—unafraid—if ghosts—why monk fine?" Huifang: "When saw ghost eat man? Monk said—wind rain nights—deep hours—cries common." Baozhu: "Yiyuan ghost story heard?" Huifang: "No." Sulan: "What ghost?" Baozhu: "Osmanthus Hall page Chunxi loved fruit—saved banquet fruit in jar—night big monkey stole—Chunxi asleep—heard fruit scatter—called—no answer—lamp—nothing—again noise—again looked—monkey hid in warming brazier—

Chunxi grabbed knife—neared brazier—monkey bolted—shouldered brazier fled—fire out—knife dropped—howled ghosts—neighbor Qing'er ran—smashed monkey—fluffy—tripped—all woke—only brazier floor—puzzled—Chunxi swore five-foot yellow-haired ghost—Qing'er green-fanged ghost—slapped him—two days gossip—third dusk saw monkey sneak for fruit—understood—else masters would hear." All laughed—Huifang prepared two vegetarian tables—four snacks—borrowed temple kitchen—nine sat mats on ground—Chunhang Huifang half-year friends—now saw his generosity—old loyalties—revered more—Chunhang recalled last spring poverty—near ditch—without Huifang—talent useless—west wind bleak—autumn drear—mist rising rain threatening—clouds dark—grief rose—tears—others puzzled—Huifang guessed—tearful—Baozhu asked—burst sobbing—remembered hardship—without Fragrant-Snow—nine deaths—wailed—all hearts sore.

Nanxiang sick at heart—drank deep bowl wine—howled toward distant hills—wind rose—trees shook—Gao Pin said: "You weep and laugh—every breast holds lumps—mine alone—Zhuoran—empty as a belly with no guts."

Sun Deng's whistle—mere frenzy—Ruan Ji's grief—spirit drained—ancients climbed heights—composed—grief bound us—shall we improvise verse today?" Nanxiang: "Good—you open." Gao Pin: "Stirring pours through He Xin Liang—I hold the tune—listen—does the age ask you?" Ages turn mulberry seas—cannot bear glance back—Gao: Only poets keep bright whim—daily wild songs with wine—Shi: Autumn stands in shred cloud and ruined willows.

Test horses on border moor—idle gaze—Yan—Ask Golden Terrace—want kirin's tread? Soul fled—who guards still?

Chunhang—Long I've drifted world's edge—with dawn dusk—chess board tea bowls—two three friends. Gao—Dead thousand years—gone—what fame undying? Shi—Grief piles my chest like a peck—Sing by autumn graves as tears—Yan—Hear wailing smash Qin man's jar—drink our fill—heed no dawn's call— All chanted—laughed—rain threatened—packed—Nanxiang rode Huifang's carriage—Zhongqing Sulan's—home—fine drizzle falling—to be continued.
