Chapter 33 — Inspector Mei sends a family letter instructing his son; Young Master Hua bids farewell and dismisses his guests.

It is said that Censor Shi had been appointed as the Daoming Intendant, and Nanxiang accompanied him to his new post, planning to return for the metropolitan examination the following year. The various scholars and renowned actors saw them off, lingering for a few days to reminisce. Time flew by, and soon it was mid-December. With Nanxiang and Gaopin out of the capital, Ziyu found himself missing two close confidants. Last month, Grand Secretary Wang visited Madam Yan, proposing that Ziyu should be married either by the end of winter or early spring.

Madam Yan replied that she could not make the decision alone; she must send a letter to Jiangxi and wait for a reply before settling the matter. Because of this, Ziyu seldom visited the Wang residence. Recently, Xu Ziyun had filled a vacancy, which added public duties to his work in the yamen, preventing him from spending every day in the garden.

That day, the weather was mild and clear; the snow had melted, and the wind was calm. Ziyu wanted to visit Pincai to ask for news about Qinyan.

After breakfast, he reported to his mother, took a carriage into the city, but before traveling half a mile, he began to hesitate. He thought Pincai might not even be home. The more he thought about it, the less enthusiastic he felt, so he said, 'I won't go. Let's head out of the city and go back!' Yun'er reined in his horse, the driver turned the carriage around, and they left the city. Suddenly, several carriages blocked the road, with a herd of camels squeezed among them. The drivers shouted loudly, unable to clear the way. Ziyu lowered the curtain of his carriage as it pulled alongside another. Looking through the glass window, he saw the person inside turning to look at him as well—it was Baozhu. Seeing him, Ziyu couldn't help but smile. Baozhu asked, 'Where are you coming from? And where are you going?' Ziyu replied, 'I'm coming back from the city; I'm not going anywhere.' Baozhu suggested, 'Why not come to my place for a chat? We haven't seen each other in two months.' Ziyu thought it was still early to return and that it might be a good chance to relax, so he said, 'Very well!' One carriage moved aside, with Ziyu in front and Baozhu following. They arrived at the entrance, got off their carriages, and Baozhu led him inside.

It was Ziyu's first time visiting. Upon reaching the inner courtyard, he saw three main rooms facing forward, with the west room serving as a study. A plaque hung above, reading “Little Langgan Studio.” Entering, Ziyu felt a fragrant breeze that was untainted by dust; two pots of daffodils were in full bloom. On the table sat an antique bronze vase holding a sprig of heavenly bamboo and two sprigs of wintersweet, while over there were two pots of Tang flowers. The calligraphy and paintings on the walls were all authentic works of past masters, certainly not the typical pieces of common officials. In a small alcove, on the left hung a horizontal scroll of Baozhu's portrait leaning against bamboo, and on the right hung four small screens featuring flowers painted by his art teacher, Jin Su. Upon seeing this, Ziyu sighed softly, 'There is indeed fortune and misfortune in the world. To think that such a great scholar has met this fate—heaven truly envies talent!' He turned to Baozhu and added, 'I have heard that the way you treat this gentleman, and the way he treats you, is no less than the devotion Huifang showed to Xiangfan. Moreover, since his misfortune, you have grown closer, fully providing for his travel expenses. Such a heart is exceedingly rare and puts the so-called scholars of our time to shame. This gentleman is also deeply grateful.' As Ziyu spoke, Baozhu remained silent; his eyes reddened, and he let out a long sigh. 'We are both wanderers in this world; why must we have known each other before meeting?' he quoted, unable to hold back his tears. Ziyu's unintentional words had touched upon Baozhu's secret sorrows. Realizing his abruptness, Ziyu smiled and pointed to the bamboo outside the window, saying, 'In the freezing winter, comparing this gentleman to the Tang flowers shows his natural elegance, holding true like the pine and cypress.' Hearing this, Baozhu broke into a smile through his tears, and Ziyu felt relieved. He then remarked, 'Time passes so quickly; in the blink of an eye, it's already the end of the year. Time truly flows like water.' Baozhu agreed, 'Indeed, the new year is approaching. The other day, Jiantan mentioned that right after the new year you will be celebrating—will you invite us to your wedding feast?' Ziyu replied, 'It hasn't been decided yet; we'll wait for the letter from the elders.' Baozhu then said, 'I just got two nice dishes today; would you do me the honor of dining here? If so, I'll invite Xiangwan over, and we can catch up.'

Ziyu hesitated, saying, 'If I dine here, I'll be late returning. There was a heavy snowfall the other day; you must have collected some snow water. Why don't we boil snow to brew tea, and invite Xiangwan for a refined conversation—wouldn't that be nice?' Baozhu smiled, 'Very well, you are indeed different from others.' He sent someone to invite Sulan while ordering the brazier to be moved outside and the tea stove brought over, along with the complete set of tea utensils. Ziyu saw a large bronze tray placed on the floor, upon which sat an antique bronze tea stove. The stove was over a foot high, round like a bushel, with tripod legs; it had two openings and a small, round mouth. After adding some glowing coals from the brazier and some raw charcoal, a lively fire roared up. Another servant presented a large azure porcelain bowl and an engraved Yixing clay teapot with an overhead handle, filling it with snow water. Ziyu admired the sight, remarking, 'Even before the tea is brewed, seeing this tea set is enough to clear the mind and quench the thirst.' Just then, Sulan arrived, and everyone exchanged greetings. Sulan smiled at Baozhu, 'You are so elegant today; surely it is for a refined guest. But with a vulgar person like me joining, I hope I don't spoil the elegance.' Baozhu replied, 'You are extremely elegant yourself.' Sulan asked Ziyu, 'Why have you been staying indoors lately? Have you seen Yunong?' Ziyu answered, 'No. How could Yunong come out now? I never expected he would find his place there.' Baozhu smiled, 'I fear that place isn't where Yunong belongs. With his aspirations, how could he endure being wronged for long?' Ziyu replied, 'I heard they treat him very well; what wrongs could he suffer?' Baozhu said, 'Being treated well is one thing, but Young Master Hua is not entirely considerate. Even Duxiang's treatment couldn't win Yunong's heart; how could that place compare to Duxiang's?

“The situations are completely different. That place strictly follows the rules at every step, which is inconvenient for someone accustomed to leisure. On the fourteenth of the eighth month, I saw Yunong come out to serve; it was clearly forced, a matter of having no other choice.” Sulan added, “Now when he sees us, there is a strangeness about him; it feels like his heart is always clouded with a lingering melancholy.” Ziyu listened and could not help but sigh. When Baozhu saw the water boiling, he took a jade tea caddy from the curio cabinet, blended four famous teas, and brewed it himself, placing the lid on. He then brought out three Ding-kiln porcelain cups, divided the tea into three portions, filled the caddy with boiling water again, and covered it. Ziyu remarked, “You went to the trouble of preparing it yourself; it must have been exhausting.” Baozhu said, “Taste it; how is the flavor?” Ziyu and Sulan took a couple of sips and felt the fresh fragrance fill their mouths, seeping into their hearts and spleens. They both exclaimed, “This tea is excellent, and it doesn't taste like just one kind.” Baozhu revealed, “I combined various fine teas into one bowl.” Ziyu said, “No wonder it is so fragrant and delicious.” Baozhu then brought out a fruit box to accompany the tea. Ziyu noted, “This is even better than wine.” The three chatted idly for a while before Sulan asked Ziyu, “Have you seen your family friend Wei Pincai lately?” Ziyu replied, “I haven't seen him in two months. I specifically meant to visit him today. I had already entered the city, but I figured he is often out and about, so I suddenly lost interest and turned back; that's when I ran into Yaoqing.” Baozhu gave a charming smile and said, “You were wrong; you should have gone. Even if Pincai wasn't home, the person in your heart never goes out; if he knew you were there, he would surely come out to see you.” Ziyu remained silent. Sulan asked, “Do you not know about Wei Pincai's recent affair?” Ziyu questioned, “What affair?” Sulan laughed, “This Wei Pincai used to incite people to cause trouble for Yunong, and I hated him for it. But when Yunong moved in there, nothing much happened. I see that he isn't a great villain, just petty in his opinions. Little did he know that after ruining others before, he is now being ruined by someone else, and he won't have the face to see anyone in the future.” Startled, Ziyu quickly asked, “What shameful thing happened?” Baozhu, also unaware, chimed in to ask what occurred. Sulan explained, “Wei Pincai wasn't good to begin with, but it's hard to make true friends nowadays; many have the faces of men but the hearts of beasts. Do you really not know that Wei Pincai visited a prostitute and was caught by the ward official, who sent him to the Ministry of Punishments?” Astonished, Ziyu asked, “Such a thing happened! How did it go to the Ministry of Punishments?” Sulan replied, “I heard it from Zhang Zhongyu. Zhongyu is now a commander, so he knows about this; it happened four or five days ago.

That day, Wei Pincai invited Lord Fu Third to drink at Rongguan's residence. Lord Fu suddenly remembered something and went into the city first. Sulan replied, 'I heard a patrolling censor discovered the official was extorting bribes, altering testimony, and releasing people privately, so he escalated the case and sent it to the Ministry of Punishments.' Baozhu added, 'So Wei Pincai is now in prison? He deserves it. But why hasn't Young Master Hua taken care of him?'

Sulan said, 'According to Zhongyu, they are keeping it a secret from Young Master Hua, and besides, he used a fake name. It's likely he's just lost face, and it won't amount to a major crime. Hearing this, Ziyu felt anxious for Pincai and repeatedly said, 'This is terrible! Even our friend Li has been messing around outside. I never expected Wei Pincai to get caught up in something like this.' Sulan commented, 'It's good he suffers a bit; the retribution is swift. Who told him to use the coachmen to torment others? Now he's getting it paid back with interest.' Ziyu laughed again.

The three of them chatted for quite a while. Seeing it was getting late, Ziyu bid the two farewell and headed home.

Upon reaching the main room, he saw Madam Yan, who appeared displeased. Ziyu dared not ask and stood dumbly to the side. Madam Yan said, “A letter from your father has arrived. He knows everything you've done and says I haven't disciplined you properly. See for yourself.” She handed the letter to Ziyu. Before he even read it, he was already terrified and speechless. He walked over to the window, respectfully held the letter, and read it through. His cheeks flushed bright red; he remained silent, just looking at Madam Yan. Seeing him in this state, she truly felt pity but forced a cold smile, saying, “Since you know to be afraid, wouldn't it have been better not to do those things in the first place! Whether you learn to be good or not is up to you; either way, I cannot follow you when you go out. If you continue to misbehave, your father may not spare you when he returns.” Ziyu could only reply repeatedly, “Yes!” not daring to sit down or leave. Madam Yan, finding it inconvenient to comfort him, could only ask if he had seen Wei Pincai today.

Hearing this, Ziyu hesitated, appearing as if he wanted to speak but couldn't. When Madam Yan asked again, Ziyu said, “I didn't see him, but I received word that Wei Pincai has gotten into some trouble and was reported; the day before yesterday, he was detained in the Ministry of Punishments prison.” Startled, Madam Yan hurriedly asked, “Who told you this? What happened, and where did you hear it?” Ziyu casually replied, “It was an acquaintance, Zhang Zhongyu, a relative of Wei. He wasn't very clear, but it sounds like he was caught drinking with prostitutes by the ward official.” Hearing this, Madam Yan cursed, “What a despicable creature! Doing such shameless things—what will happen now? Is the Hua family not going to intervene?” Ziyu said, “I heard Wei spends a lot of time outside the city, and he used a fake name, claiming his surname is Li, likely keeping it from the Hua family. Others say he recently purchased a minor official rank. Even though he claims to be Li Sancai, people know he is Wei Pincai.” Madam Yan's face turned red with anger. After a pause, she said, “Great, they are all useless. Even Li is never home; who knows what he does outside, probably nothing proper, and I can't even scold him. As for Pincai, I assume he knows the details.” Ziyu replied, “According to Li, he hasn't seen Pincai in two or three months; they have grown quite distant recently.” Madam Yan said, “But what about Pincai's situation? Although he may not be worth much pity, he is still the son of your father's old friend. We need to find out the truth.” She called for a maid to fetch Mei Jin, who soon arrived and stood below the steps. Madam Yan explained Pincai's situation, instructing him to go to Elder Wang and ask him to keep an eye on things, or perhaps put in a good word at the Ministry. Mei Jin replied, “I will go at once. But although Young Master Wei's matter is small, he is already locked up, and even his family isn't allowed to bring him food. I don't understand why it's so strict. I fear Elder Wang may not be willing to intercede. Perhaps someone from the Hua family will assist him.” Madam Yan asked, “Do you know the specifics of what happened?” Mei Jin said, “I heard people talking about it yesterday.” He then detailed Pincai's situation. Madam Yan responded, “Even so, we must do our part. Go to Elder Wang's place first, and we can discuss whether it's possible later.” After Mei Jin left, Madam Yan sneered, “This is what comes from liking to visit those young masters' homes.” Ziyu's face turned bright red with shame, and he could only sit down on a stool below. He accompanied Madam Yan for dinner, then returned to his study. From then on, Ziyu was filled with fear and dared not entertain any wild thoughts for several days.

Mei Jin arrived at the Wang residence. Wenhui called him in and asked the reason for his visit. Mei Jin explained the situation, and Wenhui sneered, saying, 'That Wei Pincai—I knew the moment I saw him that he was nothing good. Your master insisted on keeping him; fortunately, he has left now. How can I possibly intervene? Besides, I have no acquaintances in the Ministry of Punishments. Go back and pay your respects to the madam, and tell her I can only ask someone else to try his luck.' Mei Jin returned and reported exactly what was said. Madam Yan was at a loss and could only let things take their course. Meanwhile, in prison, Pincai promised Rongguan and Yu Tianxian a large sum of silver, instructing them to align their testimonies with his. He told them to claim that the official had invited him to drink at Rongguan's residence and had called for the woman, Yu Tianxian. During the drinking, the official asked Pincai for a loan of one thousand taels; when Pincai refused, an argument ensued.

Official Yu had brought soldiers with him beforehand; they immediately chained them up and took them back to his quarters. They then fabricated the charge of being caught during a night inspection, extorted bribes for their release, and forced them to sign promissory notes. Yu Tianxian also testified that Official Yu frequently visited her home to smoke opium and drink; half a month earlier, he had sent an invitation for a share of the money, but when it didn't arrive, he harbored a grudge and set a trap to arrest them.

After the arrest that day, he extorted another five hundred strings of cash to alter his testimony and release them. Rongguan's testimony was identical. The Ministry held two hearings, and their testimonies matched. Young Master Hua had already learned of this; initially wanting to ignore it, he felt uneasy and had to send someone to the Ministry of Punishments to put in a good word, which resulted in a much lighter sentence. Official Yu was dismissed from his post, Pincai received twenty strokes of the cane, Yu Tianxian was expelled from the jurisdiction, and Rongguan was released to go home, thus concluding the case.

Pincai returned to the city triumphant, claiming he had won the lawsuit, and went straight back to the Hua residence.

Upon seeing him, the gatekeepers offered many words of comfort. Pincai boasted, 'I didn't suffer the slightest injustice. Those officials are all on good terms with me, and the prison warden is a close friend; everything was thanks to them. They took great care of me these past few days. I wonder if the young master knows about this?' The crowd could only reply that they didn't know.

After Pincai entered his room, groups of people came to inquire about him, but he saw no one sent by Young Master Hua. Pincai genuinely believed he was unaware of the matter, which put his mind at ease. On the third day, Lin Shanzhi entered, holding a large package wrapped in both hands, resembling silver. He placed it on the table and said, “This is a gift from the young master.” With that, he turned and walked away. Pincai hadn't even managed to say the words “thank you” before he was long gone. Seeing this unusual behavior, Pincai felt suspicious and looked at the silver package. On it was written: “Farewell gift, two hundred taels.” His heart skipped a beat, and after a moment of deep thought, he understood, but for a moment, he couldn't decide what to do. He wanted to wait for Shanzhi to return and clarify things.

However, after waiting two days, no one came, not even the usual acquaintances Gu Yueqing and Zhang Xiaomei. After pondering for half the night, he finally made up his mind. Early the next morning, he wrote a letter of thanks: first expressing his gratitude, then stating that there were matters at the Mei residence requiring him to return and manage the household affairs. It was difficult to refuse, so he had to leave temporarily and would return in the spring. After writing, he went to the gatehouse and told the gatekeeper to deliver the letter. About half an hour later, the gatekeeper returned, saying: “The young master has read your letter and says returning to the Mei residence is appropriate. The young master is busy and cannot see you off personally.” Pincai still harbored hopes of reconciliation, but hearing these words, his heart turned to ash. He could only say: “Many thanks to the young master and all the gentlemen for your care over the past half year. I will move out today and won't be able to bid farewell in person.” The gatekeeper agreed and left. With no other choice, Pincai packed his luggage and belongings, asking the steward for a large carriage to load them. He had a horse and carriage, two young servants, a cook, and a driver; together, they left the city and temporarily stayed at an inn, planning to find a new residence later.

Pincai had only been in the Hua residence for ten months, yet through deceit and trickery outside, he had amassed quite a sum of money. Aside from his regular stipend, Young Master Hua had bestowed many gifts upon him. Pincai spent over four hundred taels to buy a minor rank of the ninth grade, and squandered another two thousand on clothes and extravagance. By this time, excluding the two hundred taels from the previous day, he still had three hundred taels left, along with various trinkets. Fortunately, the rank he purchased placed him high on the list, meaning he could be selected within about half a year. Consequently, his courage and confidence grew, vastly different from before. After staying at the inn for two days and finding it too noisy, he rented the house at Hongji Temple where Chunhang used to live. With a grand carriage and horses, he began to live extravagantly. He didn't even visit the Mei residence. Rongguan and Yu Tianxian visited frequently; Pincai distributed a hundred taels between them and gave them miscellaneous trinkets. Engaging in daily drinking and singing, he naturally drew close to a group of like-minded individuals.

Meanwhile, Lord Fu Third, upon hearing of Pincai's trouble, spent several days inquiring at the Ministry but found no way through. He only felt relieved after learning that Young Master Hua had interceded on his behalf. Later, hearing that Pincai had left his patron, his concerns returned, and he intended to invite him home. One morning, he went out of the city early to find Pincai and saw a group of people making a racket at the temple gate. As Lord Fu Third stepped down from his carriage, he noticed a man in a blue cloth old sheepskin coat and a worn autumn hat, in his thirties, cursing vulgarities.
