Chapter 37 — In a minor game, one character transforms into three; in theater-title antiphony, two words expand into four.

To return to our story: Qinyan had returned to his lodgings in a fury. He wept for half the day before lying down on his bed fully clothed, pulling the quilt over himself. He cursed himself a thousand times over for having gone to see Pincai, realizing too late that Pincai was in league with the rest of them and had deliberately set out to humiliate him. His wretchedness was so profound that he wished he could die. He went on seething and weeping through half the night. Morning came and he was still asleep, so his young manservant had to come in and shake him awake. 'The master from the Garden of Ease, Mr Xu, has sent for you,' the boy said. 'He says you should go quickly, as Young Master Mei has already arrived.' Qinyan got up, and the boy folded away the bedding while Qinyan washed his face and drank a bowl of tea. After spending the previous day in a rage and half the night in tears, following two days of exhaustion, he now felt dizzy and light-headed. His mouth was dry and he felt utterly wretched. He forced himself to collect his strength, changed his clothes, and glanced at himself in the mirror, noticing that he looked a little thinner and paler than usual. He sat for a while longer in listless reluctance. By the time mid-morning approached, he made himself get into a carriage and set off for the Garden of Ease.

It was the first day of the second month. In the garden the plum blossoms had not yet fully opened, though the camellia and magnolia were in bloom.

For today's gathering, Liu Wenze, Yan Zhongqing, and Tian Chunhang did not attend. Chunhang was attending a New Year gathering of his examination year-mates. Since Wenze and Wang Xun were sons of the examiners, they had all invited him. Chunhang had also invited Zhongqing, but Zhongqing had recently caught a chill, so he declined both invitations. Wang Xun also declined his invitation, having arranged early in the morning to accompany Ziyu to the Garden of Ease. Cixian and Ziyun welcomed them into the Plum Blossom Retreat and they took their seats. This retreat was laid out in the shape of a plum blossom, comprising five sections of five rooms each. Winding corridors and curved balustrades connected them, surrounded by a sea of green calyxes and crimson fragrance. Looking from the outside, the buildings were indistinguishable; one perceived only an expanse of fragrant snow. Whenever Ziyu visited the garden, he always took time to admire several such spots. Ziyun remarked, 'We are rather short of company today, as there are many theatricals and banquets this month. I imagine that since Yunong has more than twenty days before he must return, these plum blossoms will last another ten. I intend to host a ten-day gathering where, regardless of numbers, whoever is free may come. Even if I happen to be occupied outside the garden, Jingyi will always be here to act as host. What do Yongan and Yuxiang think of this?' Wang Xun replied, 'That sounds ideal. I shall certainly come whenever I have the time.' Ziyu said, 'If I may say so, our host need not trouble himself every single day. Whoever feels the inclination might simply bring his wine and join the company, or perhaps we might find some other exceptional way to pass the time.' Cixian added, 'There are ways enough to pass the time, it is merely that our particular circle lacks any single devotion, making us seem rather more austere than most. For instance, some people gather to play cards or roll dice, or even gamble on Baobao and Yaotan. Others might beat gongs and drums, watch juggling, or listen to blind storytellers; these are all common amusements. Failing that, they might hire a theatrical troupe to perform, filling the stage with spears and swords and tumbling acrobats, before binding their own heads, painting their faces, and strutting about the stage themselves in high triumph. That too is a way to pass the time. And then there are the green bowers and winding alleys, where one might embrace powdered faces and oiled hair, flirting and jesting in a great tumult. Not only am I incapable of such things, but I expect none of you gentlemen care for them either.' Ziyun and the others murmured their agreement, noting that by this measure they could hardly be called lovers of excitement. Yet they agreed that nothing in the world brought greater joy than drinking wine and admiring flowers. Wang Xun turned to Ziyun and said, 'I have a question for you to judge.' Ziyun invited him to speak. Wang Xun asked, 'Between the flower among men and the flower among flowers, which is the more beautiful?' Ziyun smiled and replied, 'Each has its own beauty.' Wang Xun pressed, 'If one cannot have both, does one choose the person or the flower?' Ziyun laughed, 'What nonsense you speak! Naturally the person is precious and the flower is cheap, why even ask such a thing?' Cixian interjected, 'He surely has some hidden meaning in this; he is not speaking idly.'

Ziyun smiled faintly. Wang Xun laughed, 'I see your garden is filled with blossoms, yet in all the time we have been talking not a single flower among men has appeared. Does this not show you prefer flowers to people?' Ziyun replied with a chuckle, 'You are just talking for the sake of it. We made perfectly good arrangements the other day, so why have they not arrived yet?' A short while later, Baozhu and Guibao arrived and exchanged greetings. Ziyun asked, 'How is it that only the two of you have come at this hour?' Baozhu answered, 'I fear some may be unable to attend today. Has Yunong not arrived either?' Guibao explained, 'Today Lianjin has five private engagements and Lianzhu has four. Since they are almost exclusively performing Kunqu operas, the roles cannot be easily distributed. We have both already performed our share.' Wang Xun inquired why there were so many engagements today, to which Guibao replied that they would be busy for another half month before things quieted down. Baozhu added, 'I saw Xiangfan and Qianzhou there; why did Jiantan not come?' Wang Xun explained that he was unwell. Guibao turned to Ziyu and observed, 'This is the first time we have met this year.' Ziyu agreed, noting that although he had been out several times, he had never run into him. Baozhu remarked, 'Xiangwan and Jingfang are having a hard time of it today, as they are scheduled to perform everywhere and simply cannot get away.' Ziyun sighed, 'I thought I could count on six or seven people, but who could have known they would all be unavailable?' As midday approached, Guibao looked out and announced, 'Yunong is here!' Everyone turned to watch him enter. Ziyu saw that he had grown considerably taller since last year. Dressed in elegantly plain clothes as he walked through the plum grove, he seemed to blend with the blossoms in a dazzling display of radiant beauty. The company rose to welcome him with smiles. Qinyan first greeted Cixian, Ziyun, and Wang Xun, then turned to Ziyu to exchange pleasantries about the weather. Ziyun laughed and said, 'He has grown taller now and his learning has advanced. Look at him exchanging pleasantries with Yuxiang; he would hardly have been able to do that last year.' Hearing this, Qinyan felt somewhat embarrassed and replied, 'I have not seen him for half a year.' Ziyun teased, 'And when did we ever see each other often?' Qinyan smiled, 'Did we not see each other when you and Jingyi came to pay New Year calls?' Cixian laughed, 'Ah, yes, seeing each other once is apparently enough to absolve one of further conversation.' This made Qinyan laugh. Wang Xun added, 'I am the one who sees Yunong the least.' Qinyan nodded in agreement, then sat down alongside Baozhu and Guibao. Baozhu urged him to take the upper seat, which he eventually did.

Ziyun gave orders for the table to be laid, but did not yet circulate the wine. He said to Wang Xun, 'By seniority you are older than Yuxiang, but today's gathering has been arranged especially in honour of Yunong. Therefore, I want Yunong to sit in the place of honour, with Yuxiang beside him.' Qinyan protested, 'That cannot be, I shall not sit there.' Ziyu suggested that Yongan should have the top seat. Ziyun replied, 'On other occasions, yes, but today we are reversing the order.' He took Qinyan by the arm and settled him firmly in the seat of honour, placing Ziyu beside him. Guibao took the second seat, with Wang Xun alongside. No further demurral was to be permitted, and anyone who deferred would be penalized ten cups of wine. Ziyun then called on Baozhu to sit in the upper position. Just as Baozhu was about to decline, Huifang arrived. Ziyun welcomed him warmly, 'Splendid, do sit down, Cixian will be beside you.' Huifang was reluctant to sit above Cixian, but Cixian reassured him, 'Today's arrangement puts all of you above us. Have you not seen that two have already been settled?' Huifang had to comply, and Baozhu below likewise had no choice but to take the seat above Ziyun. Once everyone was seated, Wang Xun laughed and remarked, 'If a restaurant were to seat its guests this way, it would soon be paying them to eat rather than the other way round.' Everyone smiled, toasted one another a few rounds, and leisurely enjoyed a few dishes.

Baozhu noticed that Qinyan's eyes looked swollen from weeping and guessed it must be on account of his teacher. Ziyun had also noticed this and sighed quietly. 'Yunong is truly a tender-hearted soul,' he observed. 'Changqing did not treat him particularly well, and yet he has wept like this for him; that is rare indeed.' Everyone present sighed in sympathy. When Qinyan heard this, it stirred up yesterday's resentment, casting an expression of anger across his face. Then, catching sight of Ziyu beside him, who was the very cause of all that had happened, a surge of bitter feeling welled up within him and tears spilled down his cheeks. Everyone at the table hastened to offer words of comfort, not realizing that Qinyan's grief had quite a different source and had nothing whatsoever to do with Changqing. Since they did not know the truth and he could hardly explain it to them, he kept it all locked inside himself. The more he thought about it, the angrier he became, until he could no longer hold back. Pressing his handkerchief to his face, he thought to himself: 'That creature Wei Pincai is sure to spread rumors that I was drinking and entertaining at his rooms, that Qi Shiyi gave me a bracelet, and so forth. It would be better to speak up myself and let people know the truth. Besides, surely no one here would laugh at me.' He waited another moment, then asked Ziyu, 'When did you last see Pincai?' Ziyu replied, 'Not since sometime in the tenth month of last year. He is living now at Hongji Temple outside the city wall and never comes to my house.'

Qinyan said, 'I saw him yesterday and he told me he had met you three times this year.' Ziyu replied, 'That is quite untrue. The most absurd instance was at dawn on New Year's Day when he came banging on the gate. The doorman had only just gotten out of bed and dressed. Pincai said a word or two, left a calling card, and to this day we have not seen each other. Where did you see him?' Qinyan uttered a curse under his breath, 'That Wei Pincai has never been anything but trouble.' Huifang agreed, 'He has always been trouble, you need hardly tell us that.' Ziyu pressed Qinyan to continue. With tears in his eyes, Qinyan confessed, 'The fault was mine. I went to his lodgings and asked him to come with me to see you.' At these words a sharp pang went through Ziyu, and a flush of red appeared at his eyelids. Everyone listened intently as Qinyan continued. Wang Xun asked, 'And how did he treat you?' Qinyan explained, 'Pincai himself was alright at first, but there is a set of bad characters who have got their claws into him and are leading him astray.' Ziyun inquired about their identities. Qinyan revealed, 'One is Qi Shiyi, another is Pan Qiguan, and then there is a monk who is the landlord of the place where Pincai is staying.'

Huifang frowned and asked, 'And what happened to you?' Qinyan's anger had by now reached such a pitch that he went ahead and recounted the whole story in detail: how Qi Shiyi had made a point of leaving early, how Pincai had then sent Pan San away, how Qi Shiyi had unexpectedly turned up again with a gift of food, and how, one after another, Qi Shiyi, Pan San, and the monk had all barged in. He detailed every manner of insult and mockery they had subjected him to at the table, and how they had ultimately smashed his bracelet. Listening to this, Ziyu was furious. 'This is all Pincai's doing,' he declared. 'He must have arranged the whole thing on purpose, deliberately setting those men on to ill-use you.' Qinyan agreed, 'Of course he was in it with them. Fortunately, I no longer perform on stage, so they dared not go too far. Otherwise it would have been far worse, and I doubt you would be seeing me here today.' Ziyun remarked, 'What an ill fortune to encounter all three of those fiends at once. That must have been a dreadful ordeal.' Cixian and Wang Xun both laughed sympathetically. Guibao added, 'That Qi Shiyi, I have never had dealings with him myself, but Peixian and Yuyan came badly unstuck with him.' Qinyan noted, 'For me it has been twice now.' Wang Xun asked Guibao what he would do if he ran into Qi Shiyi. Guibao replied, 'If I ran into him, I would make him look into a bucket and have a cart-driver play a trick on him.' Everyone burst out laughing. Huifang wondered aloud, 'What scheme could we devise to deal with him?' Cixian laughed and suggested, 'If you really want to deal with him, you would have to use the stratagem of the flesh, but I doubt you would be willing to.' Huifang spat in contempt, prompting Cixian to laugh again. Ziyun asked what he found so amusing. Cixian explained, 'I was thinking that Qi Shiyi is only so mischievous because of a certain attribute of his. Why not simply have it cut off? That would calm him down well enough.' Wang Xun laughed, 'That would be no easy matter; it could only be done if Meixiang were willing to employ the flesh stratagem.' Huifang retorted, 'Why do you not do it yourself?' Wang Xun replied, 'I have no quarrel with the man, so what reason would I have? Never mind cutting Qi Shiyi, cut Pan San first and you would spare yourself a great deal of terror.' Huifang spat several times in disgust, then suddenly poured a cup of wine and offered it to Cixian, saying, 'It is entirely your fault; who told you to bring up those people?' Cixian accepted the cup without demur, smiled, and drank it down.

Just then, Ziyu and Qinyan caught each other's gaze, and each raised his cup to drink half. Ziyu could not help breaking into a gentle smile. Ziyun turned to ask him, 'How many times have you and Yunong sat at the same table now?' Ziyu replied, 'This is the second time, and it has been nearly a year since the first.' Ziyun lamented, 'Only twice! How sad! How much more parted than together.' Qinyan smiled and gently corrected him, 'This is in fact the third time.' Cixian observed, 'Yuxiang is being a little greedy, counting fewer than there have been. Last year you two slipped away to stroll along the canal without telling anyone; does that not count?' Ziyu admitted, 'I had momentarily forgotten.' Ziyun then proposed, 'I should like to invite my younger brother and Yunong to enjoy ten days of happiness together. I trust you will not find it tiresome?' Ziyu graciously responded, 'To spend time often in a fine garden among good friends is more than I could wish for; how could it possibly be tiresome? I only fear I may lack the felicity for it, and that my thin fortune might invite misfortune.'

Ziyun laughed heartily. Cixian quipped, 'He already lacks the felicity for ten days of gatherings; if he had the fortune of the Star of the North, now that would be a truly sea-vast happiness.' Everyone laughed at this remark. At that moment, both Qinyan and Ziyu felt a deep and expansive contentment.

Wang Guibao turned to Ziyun with a smile and proposed, 'I have a word-game where one character is transformed into three. I will tell it to you, and anyone who cannot produce a match must drink a penalty cup.' Ziyun invited him to proceed. Guibao explained, 'Take the character for "great" (大) and add a dot to make it "extreme" (太). Move the dot to the top and it becomes "dog" (犬). Now, everyone must offer a sequence just like this.' Ziyun laughed, 'This is a dog's game; who has the patience for it?' Guibao grinned at Huifang and prompted, 'You give one.' Huifang thought for a moment and offered, 'Take the character for "king" (王) and add a dot to make it "jade" (玉). Move the dot to the top and it becomes "master" (主). Is that not better than your dog?' Guibao nodded in agreement, 'Very good.' Then he suddenly laughed again, 'You shouldn't have done that. Duxiang just scolded me, and now you have scolded Duxiang.' Huifang was puzzled, 'When did I scold him?' The others were equally confused, so Guibao explained, 'He is the host, and you spoke the word "master" right after the word "dog". Does that not amount to scolding him?' Huifang smiled, while Ziyun chided Guibao, 'You little fox-spirit, you have been quite mischievous lately, full of this glib and oily talk.' Baozhu chimed in, 'I have one: take the character for "wood" (木), add a stroke to make it "root" (本), and move it to the top to make it "not yet" (未).' Ziyun laughed, 'I have a method of reverse incarnation: take "not yet" (未), remove a stroke to make "wood" (木), and move it to the bottom to make "root" (本).' Everyone burst into laughter.

Qinyan offered, 'I have the character for "water" (水), add a dot to make it "ice" (冰), and move it to the top to make it "eternal" (永).' Cixian critiqued, 'That "eternal" is lacking a little something; it can only be considered a thin "ice". Still, there are not many of these readily available.' Wang Xun noted, 'I fear there are only a few, and they have all been taken.' Guibao proposed another, 'I have the character for "ten" (十), add a stroke to make it "scholar" (士), and move it to the top to make it "shield" (干).' Everyone approved. Huifang added, 'I have the character for "dark" (杳), add a stroke to make it "investigate" (查), and move it to the top to make it "fragrant" (香).' The company praised this one even more highly. Baozhu suggested, 'I have the character for "man" (丁), add a stroke to make it "in" (于), and move it to the top to make it "step" (亍).' Ziyun observed, 'That character is rather obscure.' Ziyu countered, 'It is acceptable enough.' Baozhu defended it, 'The characters 彳 and 亍 are not so very obscure.' Qinyan offered another, 'I have the character for "divine" (卜), add a stroke to make it "above" (上), and move it to the top to make it "below" (下).' Cixian praised this one warmly. Guibao said, 'I have the character for "white" (白), add a stroke to make it "self" (自), and move it to the top to make it "hundred" (百).' Huifang noted it was slightly lacking. Wang Xun then offered, 'I have the character for "speak" (曰), add a stroke to make it "field" (田), and move it to the top...' He paused mid-sentence. Guibao prompted, 'Move it to the top and it becomes what character?' Wang Xun burst out laughing, and Ziyu explained, 'If you push it straight through to the top, it becomes "reason" (由).' Ziyun added, 'I would pull it down to make it "armour" (甲).' Cixian laughed, 'One of you wants it to go up, the other wants it to go down, and you will end up quarrelling. I say let it go up at one end and down at the other, making the character "explain" (申). How is that?' Everyone burst into laughter.

They ate a few pastries and drank several more cups of wine. Wang Xun asked Huifang, 'Have you seen Xiangfan and Qianzhou?' Huifang replied, 'I was in fact with them for quite a while, which is why I was delayed. My part in the performance was moved up before I could get away. I saw a remarkable old gentleman of letters there today, said to be Qianzhou's former teacher. He had a refined and antique bearing and appeared to be well past sixty.' Ziyun inquired about his family name. Huifang answered, 'It is an unusual name. Let me think, something like Qu, I believe. He was dressed in the insignia of the sixth rank, and his conversation was so brilliant and wide-ranging that everyone present held him in admiration.' Ziyun thought for a moment and corrected him, 'It would be Qu, not Qu.' Huifang agreed, 'Yes, Qu, I had it wrong.' Cixian pondered, 'Could it be Qu Daosheng?' Ziyun confirmed, 'Almost certainly, I had heard he was in the capital.' Ziyu asked, 'Would his given name be Benli?' Ziyun affirmed, 'Exactly! Do you know him?' Ziyu explained, 'I do not know him personally, but I have read several of his letters to my father, discussing obscure points of history. In them, he advanced wholly original views that illuminated the ages. My father had them mounted as a booklet and I have often looked at them.' Cixian shared, 'Master Daosheng is sixty years old this year. He and my late elder brother were both recommended through the "filial and upright" examination. He served as a county magistrate in Jiangxi; what brings him to the capital?' Ziyun replied, 'He was promoted to assistant prefect last year and has presumably come to be presented at court. One of these days I shall invite him here and we can all meet him. He may be a man of principle, but he is also very fond of flowers and wine.' Ziyu hesitated, 'He is a friend of my father's generation, so I fear it may not be quite appropriate for me to keep him company.' Ziyun reassured him, 'What harm could there be?' Cixian added, 'Daosheng may be rather traditional in his ways, but in his writing and painting he is very playful. Besides his major works, he has produced various miscellaneous pieces, including one collection called *Forgetting Death* and another called *The Awakening from Sleep Collection*, all of it written in a spirit of amusement.' Qinyan noted, 'Those two titles alone are remarkable.' Wang Xun asked, 'What is in them?'

Cixian explained, 'I once flipped through them briefly on someone's desk, but did not read them closely. I recall that the *The Awakening from Sleep Collection* collection contained pieces composed by collaging lines from *ci* poems to make new *ci*, and from *qu* lyrics to make new *qu*, as well as couplets assembled from the classics and poetic anthologies. It was quite amusing. I believe towards the end there was a section of couplets matching opera titles, most of them two-character titles, but I regret I did not examine it carefully.' Ziyun asked, 'How would you compare Daosheng's poetry and prose with Hou Shiweng's?' Cixian offered his judgment, 'In my estimation, Daosheng is the superior of the two. Shiweng had great talent, but his standards were not as high, making his work rather mixed and impure. Daosheng possesses no less talent, yet his standards are pure and consistent, ensuring his body of work will last. Even Shiweng himself greatly admired him.' Wang Xun inquired, 'Do you mean our Nanjing man, Hou Shiweng? He considers himself the foremost genius under heaven, but from what I have seen, I am not sure that is warranted.' Ziyun defended him mildly, 'The talent is great and the learning is broad. To achieve what he has achieved is no small thing.' Then he proposed a new game, 'I think opera titles are quite amenable to being paired into couplets. For instance, *Watching a Painting* could be paired with *Stealing the Poem*, and *Stealing the Poem* could also be paired with *Picking Up a Painting*. It is a rather amusing game. Let the eight of us divide into four pairs, with each person pairing their partner's title. Anyone who produces a pairing not quite right in one character is penalized one cup; two characters off, two cups; half a character off or a partial answer, half a cup. A particularly clever or perfect pairing earns a toast of congratulations.'

Cixian agreed enthusiastically, 'Excellent. Let Yuxiang and Yunong start.' Ziyu suggested, 'It would be better if you and Meixiang go first, then Duxiang and Yaoqing, then Yongan and Ruixiang, and we shall go last.' Ziyun agreed to this arrangement, 'So we are placed in the middle, making it easy enough to look well in that position.' Cixian sighed, 'The first mover has the hard task, and I cannot think of anything brilliant on the spot. The day before yesterday I saw Shou Xiang perform *Inscribing the Song* and he sang it beautifully, so I shall take that as my opening.' Huifang immediately responded, '*Inscribing the Song* could certainly be paired with *Stealing the Poem*.' Baozhu interjected, 'Someone else just paired those; you are borrowing a ready-made answer. The pairing is muddled, so the penalty is one cup.' Huifang protested, 'If it does not count, it simply does not count. Why should there be a penalty?' Ziyun insisted, 'There must be a penalty, otherwise people would just pair haphazardly and refuse to drink.' Thus, Huifang was made to drink a cup as forfeit. After a moment of thought, Huifang offered, 'Will *Teaching Song* do as a pairing?' Cixian approved, 'Good.' Everyone agreed it was a fitting match. Huifang then declared, 'Since it is agreed to be good, there ought to be a toast.' Ziyun concurred, 'Indeed there should.' A toast was drunk by all at the table. Huifang then put forward *Burying the Jade*, and Cixian matched it with *Finding Gold*. Wang Xun praised it, 'That is perfectly matched, let us toast again.' And so each person toasted again.

It was now Ziyun's turn to put forward a title, and he offered the first half of a couplet: *Treading on the Moon*. Baozhu thought for a moment and paired it with *Sweeping Flowers*. Guibao applauded, 'Excellent.' Ziyun offered a mild critique, 'The pairing is good in terms of meaning, but when two-character opera titles are matched, the tones should also correspond. The word "sweep" is also an oblique tone, so there is a slight imperfection here.' Cixian concurred, 'You are right to point that out. However, when I think about it, although there are many opera titles, those that pair well are actually quite few. The second character ought to observe tonal correspondence strictly, while the first may be forgiven. This is unlike a general couplet matching historical references, where one may range freely in search of material; here the choices are finite. It is better to have a pairing where the characters match well but the tones are slightly off than one where the tones are perfect but the characters are weak. As for the second character, no error can be excused.' Ziyun reflected on this, agreed that the pickings were indeed slim, and accepted the answer. Baozhu then put forward *The Monastery Gate*, and after some deliberation Ziyun matched it with *The Stone Grotto*. This was deemed acceptably well-matched, earning a toast. Next came the turn of Wang Xun and Guibao. Wang Xun offered *Singing the Ballad*, and Guibao matched it with *Composing the Score*. Cixian remarked, 'I feel the opening title ought to be something fresh and unexpected; too conventional and it lacks novelty.' Guibao turned to Wang Xun and said, 'Let me give you a fresh one: *Stealing the Chicken*.' Wang Xun replied, 'I shall match it with *Subduing the Tiger*.' Everyone praised this as a fine match and toasted. Cixian observed, 'It is indeed a good pairing, but *Stealing the Chicken* is small and petty, while *Subduing the Tiger* is bold and grand. What do you all think?' Wang Xun acknowledged the point, 'That is a precise observation. I shall change mine to *Visiting the Rat*.' Cixian declared, 'Now that deserves a toast.' Everyone drank. When it came to Ziyu's turn, he offered *Looking at the Stocking*, and Qinyan gracefully paired it with *Borrowing the Boot*. The company exclaimed, 'Beautifully paired! That deserves two toasts.' Huifang interjected, 'One is enough. The pairing came quickly, it is true, but if we go on toasting two cups at a time we shall drink everyone senseless and nobody will be able to pair anything.' Cixian agreed, 'Quite right. Henceforth, we shall have a full toast for the very best pairings, half a cup for good ones, and nothing for ordinary ones.' And so each person drank a cup.

Qinyan then put forward *The Drunken Consort*. Ziyu, whose ear had been caught by Wang Xun's *Subduing the Tiger* a moment earlier, brilliantly paired it with *Sobering the Courtesan*. Everyone exclaimed, 'That pairing is delightful! The wit of setting "drunk" against "sober" is admirable. A toast all round!' Qinyan added, 'The charm is in the one being drunk and the other sober; it is a lucky find.' When it came to Cixian's turn, he offered, 'I shall propose *Flinging the Dipper*.' Huifang mused, 'What a *Flinging the Dipper*.' After a moment's thought, he replied, 'I shall match it with *Searching the Cup*.' Cixian praised him, 'And what a *Searching the Cup*! The pairing is precise and tight inside. A toast, a full cup!' Everyone drank joyfully.

After a pause, Cixian pressed Huifang to produce the next title. Huifang hesitated, 'I have one in mind, but I fear it may have no possible answer.' Cixian encouraged him, 'If it truly has none, we shall simply drink a cup and move on. Say it out, and let me think.' Huifang explained, '*Female Thief* has another name: *Hinny Bandit*. Those two characters are quite original. If you can produce a pairing, I will willingly drink three cups.' Cixian challenged, 'Is that a promise?' Everyone mentally tried and failed to come up with a match. Ziyun admitted, 'I cannot think of a pairing.' Suddenly, Cixian burst out laughing and said to Huifang, 'You drink your three cups first, then I shall give you the pairing.' Huifang countered, 'You pair it first, then I drink.'

Cixian said, 'You will drink. Now: *Power and Profit* also goes by the name *Powerful Monk*. Is that not a perfect pairing?' Huifang said, 'How can *power* pair with *female*?' Ziyu thought for a moment, then broke into a loud clap of the hands and burst out laughing. 'Marvellous, utterly marvellous! Only power could possibly pair with female. A truly perfect match.' Qinyan and Baozhu had not yet understood it. Ziyun and Wang Xun, having worked it out, also began laughing and said, 'A brilliant conceit, to treat those two characters as those two things: a truly inspired stroke of imagination.' The four performers had not yet grasped it; Huifang was still sitting blankly thinking. Wang Xun said, 'You have not worked it out, do you not know that for a man, yang is power?' At this Huifang and the others suddenly understood and began to laugh as well, all agreeing it was splendid. Huifang drank his promised three cups; everyone else toasted one.

Ziyun then offered *Attacking the Inn*, and Baozhu cleverly matched it with *Fleeing the Pass*. Baozhu proposed *Seizing the Beauty*, which Ziyun neatly countered with *Killing Xishi*. Both were deemed robust and steady pairings, and each earned a cup. Wang Xun brought forth *Grass Bridge*, and Guibao paired it with *Hemp Ground*, then suddenly paused. 'The word for "field" here is slightly mismatched,' he mused. 'Let me change it to *The Pavilion of Gossamer*.' Wang Xun praised the revision: 'This borrowed pairing of *The Pavilion of Gossamer* is excellent. It deserves a half cup.' Guibao next proposed *Flower Lady*. Wang Xun thought for a moment and offered *Fire Judge*. The group was already applauding and calling for a toast when Wang Xun held up a hand. 'Wait, I have a better idea,' he announced, and changed his match to *Grass Chancellor*. The others exclaimed, 'Even better! That is remarkably fresh and inventive.' They all toasted in agreement. Ziyu offered *Sealing the Chamber*, and Qinyan smoothly matched it with *Declining the Pavilion*. This too was judged a solid pairing and rewarded with a half cup. Qinyan then proposed *Removing Armour*. Ziyu reflected but could think of nothing entirely novel. Suddenly, he remembered a scene from *The Peach Blossom Fan* called *Duping the Ding Clan* and offered it as a borrowed match. Everyone was full of admiration, and each drank a full cup in toast. Cixian offered *The Rice-Shop*, and Huifang matched it with *The Tea-House*. Huifang offered *Plucking the Eyebrow*, and Ziyun said, 'This is even harder to match.' Cixian matched it with *Piercing the Eye* and *Opening the Eye*. Huifang said, 'Now that is truly clever and precise.' Cixian said, 'I also considered *Piercing the Eye*, which might be even better, except that the character for "piercing" carries an oblique tone.' Ziyu said, 'Both are excellent. They feel as if heaven and earth made them for each other, and nothing could surpass them.'

It came round to Ziyun again, and he offered *Tumbling in the Snow*. Baozhu confidently stated, 'That is a broad one, making it easy for me.' He quickly added, 'Though the word "tumble" is not so easy either.' After a moment's thought, he matched it with *Falling on the Ice*. Everyone praised this in unison, 'What a fine pair! *Tumbling in the Snow* and *Falling on the Ice*: one idea rendered beautifully in two layers.' Huifang urged Baozhu, 'Give him a hard one.' Baozhu nodded, but Ziyun objected playfully, 'Why are you asking him to make it difficult for me? You just want me to be penalized wine.' Huifang laughed, 'Exactly.' Ziyun challenged Baozhu, 'Do bring on the hardest you have.' Baozhu thought for a while before offering *Supporting the Head*. Ziyun chuckled, 'That is truly not easy.' Then he suddenly slapped the table in triumph, 'I have it! I shall match it with *Cutting the Foot*.' Ziyu exclaimed, 'Superb! Superb! This and *Plucking the Eyebrow* matched with *Piercing the Eye* and *Opening the Eye* can both be called perfect pairings.' Cixian added, 'It is even better than *Plucking the Eyebrow* and *Piercing the Eye* and *Opening the Eye*. In this pair, "head" and "foot" are both used in their secondary meanings, their internal linguistic meaning is identical, and the tones correspond perfectly. Since it was Meixiang who provoked it, we should toast double cups.' And so everyone drank and happily resumed eating.

It was Wang Xun's turn again, and he offered *Flower Drum*. Guibao thought and thought, but could not come up with a match, his face growing red with the effort.

Wang Xun pressed him gently. Guibao sighed, 'I did not expect this one to be impossible. The only thing I can think of is *Hearing the Bells* and *Bell in the Rain*, but that is a prelude and not an opera title. *Grass Bridge* is not really a perfect match either, as the "bridge" does not quite fit. I cannot think of anything else, so I shall simply drink a cup.' Guibao drank half a cup, then offered *Kneeling at the Pool*. Wang Xun promptly matched it with *Leaping into the Well*. Everyone approved, and half a cup was toasted. When it came to Ziyu's turn, he offered *Plucking a Willow Branch*. Ziyun laughed, 'Yuxiang has had a tender eye on Yunong and has produced a perfectly easy title.' Ziyu confessed, 'I could not think of anything original in the moment. I find producing titles harder than matching them.' Qinyan gracefully matched it with *Sweeping the Pine*. Ziyu smiled, 'My match has made even my own title look better.' Everyone toasted half a cup. Qinyan then proposed, 'I shall take the word "sweep" for my next title: *Sweeping Away Qin*.' The company murmured, 'Now that is a hard one.' Ziyu agreed, 'Very difficult indeed. "Qin" is both a surname and the name of a dynasty, which makes it particularly tricky.' Suddenly, he thought of a match and looked quite pleased with himself. 'There happens to be one just right here. I shall match it with *Checking Han*.' Everyone exclaimed in delight, 'Perfect! It is entirely natural: the Qin and Han dynasties pair beautifully, and the verbs "sweep" and "check" are matched just as well. A double toast!' By this point, they had completed three full rounds. It had been quite a long session, and the lamps had now been lit. They paused for a short rest and exchanged some light conversation.

Cixian announced, 'It is my turn again. I shall follow Yuxiang's example and make it easy for someone.' He offered *The Tavern*, and Huifang easily matched it with *Book Shop*. Huifang then declared, 'Following Yunong's trick of the linked chain, I shall take the word "book" and offer *Revising the Letter*.' Cixian grumbled playfully, 'You are putting me in difficulty. Well then, I shall make myself produce a good one.' After thinking for a moment, he matched it with *Chasing the Messenger*.

Wang Xun nodded approvingly, '"Book" and "letter" form a fine pair of characters.' Cixian added, 'I also thought of *Releasing the Easy*, since "easy" is much like "letter" in meaning.' Everyone cried out together in praise, 'That one is even better! It deserves double cups.' And so they toasted. Ziyun casually remarked, '*Seeing the Ghost*.' No one paid attention. After a pause, Baozhu pressed him to produce a title. Ziyun laughed, 'You are not matching it, yet you are pressing me. What is there for you to match when I have not yet given a title?' Baozhu countered, 'You have not given one, so what am I supposed to match?' Ziyun clarified, 'I said it just now: *Seeing the Ghost* is my title.' Baozhu reflected, recalled that such an opera title indeed existed, and seamlessly matched it with *Departing the Soul*. Ziyun nodded approvingly, 'A good match.' Half a cup was toasted. Baozhu then offered *Eating Chaff*, which Ziyun quickly matched with *Splashing Gruel*.

It was Wang Xun's turn, and he offered *The Infernal Judge*. Cixian warned, 'This is not easy. That word for "judge" is half concrete and half abstract; Ruixiang may well be penalized.' Guibao thought for a while and announced, 'I have a good match. It is slightly unusual and not an established opera, but in *Empty Valley Fragrance* there is a scene called *The Buddhist Healer*. I shall match it with *The Buddhist Healer*.' Cixian praised him, 'Excellent indeed. You not only escape a penalty, you deserve a toast.' Guibao then proposed, 'Now I have thought of a difficult one: I offer *Frightening the Clown*.' Wang Xun pondered for a while and replied, 'I have a good match. Of the four pairings so far, these two have precisely the same dark coloring, so you should toast double cups. I match it with *Terrifying the Simpleton*.' Everyone laughed and agreed, 'Indeed, it is dark and gloomy, exactly the same color. Double cups are in order.' And so everyone toasted.

Ziyu suggested, 'We can end the game here, as the night is not young. Besides, I left the house early this morning, and if I stay too late my mother will worry. Let us close with this pairing.' Wang Xun agreed, 'It is time. After the pairing, let us eat.' Ziyun protested, 'Wait, it is actually still quite early.' Ziyu countered, 'Since we plan to meet for several more days, we ought to save our energy for tomorrow. It is better to break up early today.' In truth, Ziyu had noticed that Qinyan was beginning to look tired, and that was his real reason for wishing to end the game. Ziyun had to agree. Ziyu proposed, 'Let me offer a three-character title for the close.' He offered *The Flying Bear Dream*. Everyone murmured, 'Three characters will be harder, as fewer good matches exist.' Qinyan thought for a while and elegantly matched it with *The Crouching Tiger Scroll*.

Everyone praised this warmly and drank a final toast. Qinyan smiled and said, 'With that one pair we are done. Now, let me offer a four-character title.' The company sighed, 'Four characters will be even harder.' Qinyan reassured them, 'The penalty for a failed match is still only one cup. If everyone were trying to match four-character titles at once it would, of course, be far harder, but for one or two rounds there may still be possibilities.' He offered *Selling One's Son to Cast Himself into the Abyss*. Ziyu pondered for a while, then offered *Longing for One's Parents, Abandoning the Feast*. Everyone struck the table in admiration. Ziyun observed, 'Heart expressed through words! Yuxiang said just now he was afraid of being home late and his mother worrying, and here is *Longing for One's Parents, Abandoning the Feast*. A pairing straight from life; let us each toast three cups and then eat.' In this round, each person had taken four turns, producing a total of thirty-two paired couplets and sixty-four opera titles in all. It had been no small effort of the mind, and a good many cups of wine had been drunk. Everyone was beginning to feel the warmth of the wine and was no longer in a condition to eat a proper dinner. After the three cups of toast they had their meal, sat for a while, and then Ziyu and Wang Xun took their leave. Ziyun made arrangements for the following day. The next day, Wenze and Chunhang joined the party, as did several more of the famous performers, and they spent another day together at the Garden of Ease. Lu Sulan gave a small private gathering for Ziyu and Qinyan alone, a day of quiet conversation and light company that was, in its own way, even more delightful. Over three days in a row they met and feasted, and Ziyu felt perfectly content, though by now he was quite worn out. Xu Ziyun had been planning to invite Qu Daosheng, and just at this point Shi Nanxiang arrived in the capital, providing the occasion for a grand literary gathering of wine and verse. Ziyu could not decline, and had no choice but to attend. As to what happened next, listen to the following chapter for the explanation.
