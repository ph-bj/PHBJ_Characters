Chapter 60 — Jin Jifu brings Pinhua Baojian to its conclusion; Yuan Baozhu leads the invocation honoring the literary stars.

The new year had passed, and Lantern Festival arrived once more. Along the six streets and three markets, silver trees and flowered lanterns blazed in bustling splendor. On the thirteenth, Ziyun hosted a grand gathering for Lord Hua, Tian Chunhang, Mei Ziyu, Shi Nanxiang, Gao Pin, Yan Zhongqing, Liu Wenze, Wang Xun, Xiao Cixian, Jin Su, Qu Qinxian, and the entire company of Nine-Fragrance Garden. Seeing Lord Hua, Qinxian still felt a trace of unease, yet Hua asked nothing of past affairs, treating him with utmost courtesy. By now, Qinxian had left the circle of performers to enter the society of scholars; where once he had looked down on all with the proud aloofness of a young dan, he now carried himself with modest reserve. Thus, everyone respected him all the more, and none offered a word of mockery. Toward the famous dans, Qinxian remained as before, showing no estrangement. All day, cups and lots passed back and forth; at night, the lanterns shone in intersecting glory. After Lord Hua returned to the city, Ziyun tested his lantern mechanisms once more, making it seem as if ten thousand flowers had bloomed at once. The sound of firecrackers echoed for miles. Only after the second watch did they brew tea for quiet conversation.

Having tasted every hardship, Qinxian finally felt his trials melt away. Yet looking back on the bleak monastery, the lone lamp, and the waning moon, it all seemed like waking from a dream. Cixian then recounted to Jifu in detail the dreamscapes Qinxian had once described. Smiling, Jifu turned to Ziyun and Cixian: "Nine-Fragrance Tower is a superb garden holding every kind of flower, yet currently there is only one flower-god tablet hidden away in a secluded corner like an earth-god shrine. Is that not a slight to the flower deity? I propose we borrow these nine to serve as a Nine-Fragrance Floral History—what do you all think?"

Everyone found the idea marvelously novel and asked for details. Cixian said, "I have long harbored this thought; I wished to paint small portraits of the nine of them. Since you now propose it as well, nothing could be more perfect. Tomorrow, I will paint them one by one, and ask you to refine the brushwork. Then we can carve them into stone and enshrine them beneath Nine-Fragrance Tower as flower deities. But with only nine, we cannot make a full set of twelve." Everyone agreed this was an excellent plan. Jifu added, "I do have an idea, though I'm not sure if it will work."

Ziyun asked what it was. Jifu explained, "If we must have twelve flower deities, we can make up the number. We need only cast Qu Daoweng as the Lord of Furong City, and borrow the Fairy Du from Yulang's past-life vision. Add Yulang himself, and does that not make twelve?" Chunhang exclaimed, "Brilliant, brilliant! The portraits must capture their likenesses, but we need not use their true names—we can append style names instead. Each will have a eulogy, written to sound half-real and half-illusory, blending the person and the flower into one." Ziyu asked, "How should the compositions be handled? Should it be figures alone, or figures with a background?" Zhongqing answered, "Naturally, figures alone. We paint them in sequence, then append a short biography to each. Once carved in stone, rubbings can be made and mounted as album leaves or handscrolls to be passed down through generations." Wenze suggested, "It would be best to make two stone panels, inset into the eastern and western pillars." Wang Xun added, "If painting Fairy Du, paint her as she appeared on the lotus-picking boat." Jifu mused, "Yulang's dream of that mirror cannot be without meaning. I shall paint a dragon holding that mirror for the opening panel—how does that sound?" Everyone voiced their approval. Ziyu noted, "People will surely suspect a hidden allegory in this cloud-dragon." Ziyun declared, "Let us divide the eleven biographies and eulogies among ourselves." Cixian agreed: "Yes. This great work must rely on Jifu's brush to be transmitted." Jifu answered modestly, "I would not dare assume so much."

Cixian insisted, "There is no need for excessive modesty. With Master Daosheng gone, the realm of brush and ink naturally yields to you; this is public consensus, so why decline? I will draft the cloud-dragon panel; once it is done, you can refine it for me." Ziyun said, "Naturally, we must rely on you both. As for the other ten pieces, let us draw lots to see who gets which. We should ask Hua Xingbei to write one as well." Nanxiang agreed it was a fine idea. And so they prepared the lots, grouping Qu Daoweng, Fairy Du, and Qu Qinxian into two draws, and the remaining nine into nine draws. Curiously enough, as if the written word possessed a spirit and past-life affinities were at play, Ziyun drew Daoweng; Ziyu drew Fairy Du and Qinxian; Jin Su drew Baozhu; Chunhang drew Huifang; Zhongqing drew Qiguan; Wenze drew Chunxi; Nanxiang drew Lanbao; Wang Xun drew Guibao; Gao Pin drew Yulin; and Cixian drew Shoujiu. Sulan alone was not drawn, so that assignment was sent to Lord Hua. The tasks distributed, Ziyu remarked, "Writing biographies is easy, but painting is difficult, and carving stone takes even longer. How many days until it is finished?" Jifu replied, "It will not take many. The steles are already polished. We can paint and carve simultaneously by having Ji the Little Dwarf find artisans. It should take about a dozen days, plus a few more to inset the stones. Since our gathering is here at Nine-Fragrance Garden anyway, let us stay a few more days so I can devote myself to the painting. We shall reconvene here on the first day of the second month." All agreed this made sense, and thus they dispersed.

Ziyu returned home with Qinxian. With a graceful wife within and a handsome friend without, his fame established and his station secure, he was in the highest of spirits. Ziyun wrote a letter to Lord Hua, requesting a biography for Sulan. Meanwhile, Cixian, wielding his wondrous brush and nimble mind, spent ten full days completing the paintings. As each was finished, Jifu meticulously refined it; they painted one and carved one, proceeding with remarkable speed. Ziyun, suffering from a severe cold, dared not overexert himself, so he asked that Daoweng, Qinxian, and Fairy Du be combined into a single panel, and requested Ziyu to compose the eulogy. By the twenty-seventh, all the biographies and eulogies had been carved, each inscribed on the stone in the author's own hand. On the twenty-eighth, the stones were moved to Nine-Fragrance Tower, and the insetting was completed in a single day.

On the thirtieth, Qinxian arrived early at Nine-Fragrance Garden to view the steles, and the nine former dans came downstairs to join him. Qinxian said, "Today we ought to offer sacrifices to the flower gods before we hold our gathering tomorrow. Yet these flower gods bear our own likenesses; we could not bear it if others came to worship them. We ten must perform the rites ourselves." Huifang and the others agreed completely. They set out wine and fruit, burned fine incense, and all ten bowed in unison. Qinxian then examined the first panel inset on the eastern pillar: clouds above and water below, with a dragon hidden in the space between, revealing a single claw holding a mirror. The inscription read: *Pinhua Baojian*. Cixian's eulogy was carved beside it:

"Above, not in heaven; below, not in the fields.
Clouds rise in the nine heavens; water surges from the deep abyss.
Its transformations are miraculous, its aspects ten thousandfold.
The roundness of a spirit pearl, the suspension of a bright mirror.
It illuminates the subtle and the hidden, revealing both the treacherous and the worthy.
Like the descent of the moon, like the freshness of water.
It can conceal what must be concealed, and beautify what is beautiful."

The second panel depicted a figure in a scholar's kerchief and Daoist robes, flanked by a male and female immortal attendant. The title read:

"Portrait of the Master of Nine-Fragrance Flowers, Daoist Lord of Sanlu, with Attendants and Flower Historian Fairy Du."
Beneath it was a eulogy in Ziyu's handwriting:

"Your breath becomes the clouds, your spirit becomes the water;
In heaven and on earth, endless and unceasing.
As an official you govern letters, as a lord you govern flowers.
The essence of the constellations, the ancestor of all blooms.
To the left, fine jade; to the right, green coral.
One breath, two breaths—returning to the same source by different paths.
Five colors dazzle in splendor, nine blossoms stream their fragrance.
The flower of the heart, the stamen of the mind; an auspicious sign for the destiny of letters."

Baozhu remarked, "These eulogies are truly magnificent. If our actual deeds were recorded within, though it might not disgrace us, it would still reflect our lowly past." Huifang agreed, "Exactly! Look at those flower registers and critiques—though they praise people for their beauty and skill, they can never shake off the taint of the theater. This time, we can finally say we have leapt past that barrier." Sulan and the others nodded, sighing in deep resonance.

Qinxian then turned to the third panel, which depicted a fairy maiden with cloud-like hair and misty garments, of peerless, ethereal beauty, holding a single orchid sprig. Qinxian instantly knew it was Huifang. The title read: "Jinwen Flower Historian, Immortal Su." Beside it was Chunhang's colophon:

"Immortal Su, the Jinwen Flower Historian, possesses a spirit both brilliant and perceptive, with a complexion like the finest jade.
Snow was kneaded to form her skin, the moon carved to shape her bones.
She often scatters blossoms and cuts colored silk, and can toss grains of rice to turn them into pearls.
Her playful magic all springs from true samadhi.
She once roamed the mortal realm, leaving lingering regrets beneath the green peach blossoms.
Her fairy silhouette on the riverbank comes and goes;
The divine light of the Luo River parts and reunites.
Xiao Shi is forever tied to his colorful phoenix;
Pei Hang remains separated at the Blue Bridge.
Thus, it is fitting to weave a tenfold pearl net to guard this golden house atop the jade mountains;
How fortunate we are to draw back the ninefold silver screen and glimpse her unadorned face beneath the moon on the Jasper Terrace."

Qinxian said, "This colophon is profoundly apt; the line 'Her playful magic all springs from true samadhi' is especially brilliant." Huifang laughed, "No matter how they dress it up, how can any of this truly be us?" They moved to the fourth panel, showing a breathtakingly beautiful fairy maiden adorned in rainbow robes and lunar pendants, cradling a luminous pearl. The title read: "Nongzhu Flower Historian, Immortal Yuan." Jin Su's eulogy read:

"Immortal dew in the heavens, a bright pearl emerging from the sea;
Her spirit harmonizes with the spring, her pure essence coalesces into brilliant colors.
It travels without legs, it shines without night.
Jade blossoms and precious stamens, sovereign beauty and heavenly fragrance.
Pearls adorn her carriage, cloud-brocade sews her garments.
Golden branches and kingfisher feathers, jade brilliance clear and bright.
The flowery moon is full, the road to Mount Penglai is long.
She is both beautiful and majestic, elegant and refined.
Among the scholars filling the palace, she is the first of the great houses."

Qinxian noted, "Yaoqing's stunning, youthful radiance has been perfectly captured by Jingyi's brush and Jifu's praise." Baozhu replied, "Even as a flower god, am I truly worthy of this?" They looked at the fifth panel, depicting a fairy maiden with an aura of flowing, unadorned grace, lightly holding a single orchid. The title read: "Suxin Flower Historian, Immortal Lu." Beneath it was a short biography composed by Lord Hua:

"Immortal Lu is quick-witted and perceptive, possessing peerless beauty and extraordinary talent.
She frequently dons robes of purple silk, wandering and chanting amidst the wind and dew.
The pure fragrance that permeates her entire being perfectly mirrors the scent of orchids and the aroma of melilotus.
She is skilled in lyric poetry and excels at calligraphy; whenever her works slip into the mortal world, people cannot purchase them even for a thousand strings of cash.
In the past, when Zhong Rong evaluated poetry, he declared that Yan Yanzhi's 'carved gold and intricate colors' could not compare to Xie Lingyun's 'lotus blooming in the morning sun.'
Her unadorned, natural elegance is the height of supreme beauty; is this immortal not the very incarnation of the lotus?"

Qinxian laughed and said, "These lines capture her likeness even better than Xiangwan's small portrait. The descriptions of 'purple silk robes wandering amidst the wind and dew' and 'the incarnation of the lotus' could apply to no one else—it is truly you." Sulan smiled, "How could I ever deserve such praise? I suppose that since they are writing eulogies for flower gods, they naturally feel compelled to exhaust every word of praise." Qinxian then examined the sixth panel. It showed a fairy maiden with a delicate, fragile constitution, seeming to dance upon the wind with a lightness that could rest on a palm, wearing a golden step-sway hairpin. The title read: "Qianqian Flower Historian, Immortal Jin." Beneath it was a seven-character regulated verse by Xiao Cixian:

"Moth-eyebrows like the crescent moon reveal her delicate, slender form,
Her natural, radiant beauty requires no further adornment.
Mandarin-duck brocade is tailored into a nine-flower canopy,
Mermaid pearls are strung into a tenfold screen.
Hidden in the fairy gardens, she leans against the jade trees,
Returning from the mortal kalpa, she registers her name on the jade tally.
I only fear that even retaining an immortal, she cannot be kept,
And the dawn wind will blow her away to the tips of the green clouds."

Qinxian remarked, "He has captured the very spirit and bone of 'slender fragrance' perfectly." Shoufang laughed, "The word 'slender' bears some resemblance to me, but the rest makes me want to die of shame." They looked at the seventh panel, which depicted a fairy maiden standing gracefully beneath two jade trees, exuding an air of swaying elegance in the wind. The title read: "Juanjuan Flower Historian, Immortal Li," accompanied by a poem by Gao Pin. Qinxian asked, "Is Gao Zhuoran actually capable of saying nice things?" Yulin replied, "For once, he hasn't been caustic." Huifang added, "For Zhuoran, this poem counts as extremely affectionate." Qinxian read it:

"The sentiment of flowers and the color of the moon bring thoughts of her graceful charm,
Leaning against the wind like a jade tree, she is even more lithe and yielding.
Within the bedcurtains, one scarcely notices the preciousness of orchid and musk,
In dreams, she is too shy to play the fairy of clouds and rain.
Upon the coral pillow, a rosy flush begins to rise,
Atop the kingfisher tower, the green mists are locked away.
Banished to Mount Tiantai to endure her solitary lot,
The peach blossoms and flowing waters continue year after year."

Qinxian said, "This is truly well-said; it perfectly captures Peixian's rich fragrance and elegant charm." Yulin countered, "Actually, this poem isn't very good, and it still carries a hint of mockery. Look at lines like 'within the bedcurtains' and 'in dreams'—what is so good about those?" Huifang replied, "That is nothing to worry about. He is merely describing your enchanting beauty and noble status." Baozhu added, "A poem like this from Zhuoran is as close to good intentions as he gets. If you wanted him to be more solemn, he might not be unwilling, but he is too accustomed to his glib, smooth-talking ways. Think about it—how many serious things has he ever said in his life? Jifu told us a joke about his visit to the capital last year. Zhuoran has a distant uncle who invited him to dinner, with several other guests present. The uncle asked him, 'When you went home last year, did you find my family well?' Zhuoran replied, 'Very well! Just last month, my aunt gave birth to another cousin.' Upon hearing this, the uncle was stunned, thinking to himself, 'I haven't been home in three or four years; how could I have a newborn son?' He couldn't very well ask Zhuoran in front of the others, and though the guests found it jarring, they assumed Zhuoran meant a different aunt, and the matter passed. After the guests had left, the uncle asked him, 'What exactly did you mean by what you said earlier?' Imagine how Zhuoran answered! He said, 'It was our first time meeting, Uncle, so I naturally wanted to say something auspicious. I just grabbed the first lucky phrase that came to mind—there's no truth to it at all.' His uncle was furious enough to die, but there was nothing he could do. If he toys with his elder relatives like that, how much more will he mock outsiders?" Everyone burst into laughter, saying, "Jifu's mouth is no match for his, either." They then looked at the eighth panel, depicting a fairy maiden with a face like jade and garments of brocade, an autumn-water sword hanging at her waist, looking very much like the legendary sword-dancer Lady Gongsun. The title read: "Xiayin Flower Historian, Immortal Wang." Qinxian knew this was Lanbao, and read the seven-character ancient verse by Shi Nanxiang below it:

"I watch Immortal Wang dance with her divine sword,
Her hand drawing forth a single thread of cold spring water.
The 'dong-dong' of the Jie drum has only sounded three beats,
When a crisp, sharp wind rises to strike the face.
A rainbow reflects on the water, merging into a perfect sphere,
Flowing lightning pierces the clouds, curving like a thread.
It breaks apart the dotted strikes of dark green spears,
And deflects the flurry of great feathered arrows.
How graceful her brocade garments and jade-like face,
With a pale throat, rosy cheeks, and long, dark eyebrows.
Her cloud-like skirts trail lightly as her brocade boots lift,
Departing like a flying bird, arriving like a thunderbolt.
Spectators gather from all sides, forming a solid wall,
Envying not the hero, but rather her enchanting allure.
Dark clouds of hair pile high, her kingfisher chignon fresh,
Nine ridges adorned with flowers, her step-sway hairpin ancient in style.
This concubine borrows it for defense, caring nothing for fame,
With fragile beauty, I naturally pity my fleeting life.
Pray look at the yellow-shirted swordsmen of this world,
How many debts of gratitude and vengeance have they failed to repay?"

Qinxian praised it, saying, "This seven-character ancient verse is exceptionally well-crafted. Reading it, the details are even more vivid than in 'The Song of Lady Gongsun's Sword Dance.'" Wang Lanbao merely smiled without speaking. Huifang said, "When Xi the Eleventh caused that uproar last year, we were lucky to have her, or I would have been completely at a loss." Sulan asked, "So even you were afraid of Xi the Eleventh? Is he really more formidable than Pan the Third?" Huifang replied, "Pan the Third is a useless coward. But when Xi the Eleventh starts a riot, he's just like the carriage driver Wei Pincai sent the other day—would you be afraid or not?" Lanbao asked, "What would you have done if I hadn't been home that day?" Huifang answered, "I would have simply hidden away and refused to come out." Qinxian asked what Xi the Eleventh was like, and Lanbao mimicked his demeanor for him, which Qinxian found highly amusing. Huifang said, "I heard Xi the Eleventh left the capital. But the other day at the barber's shop, I saw someone who looked exactly like that lackey he brought with him that day. Even if it wasn't him, it must be his brother—I've never seen such a resemblance." Lanbao said, "Perhaps Xi the Eleventh didn't take him along; you never know. That dog of a lackey is only fit to be a barber anyway." Qinxian then looked at the ninth panel, depicting a blossoming plum tree with a magpie perched upon it, and a fairy maiden standing beneath. The title read: "Baochun Flower Historian, Immortal Lin." Below it was a short rhapsody by Liu Wenze:

"Birds announce the spring upon the branches of the plum,
Beneath the plum tree leans a figure of jade.
Du Lanxiang's marriage can no longer be seen,
But we are blessed to gaze upon the true form of Lu E'hua.
Kingfisher sleeves fluttering lightly,
White silk garments naturally beautiful.
Rhythm born in her very bones,
Elegance emerging from nature itself.
She rejects pearls and hairpins, yet grows only more beautiful,
Washes away rouge and powder, and appears even more graceful.
As slender as the new moon among the clouds,
As delicate as the clear mist beyond the flowers.
Autumn waters fill the riverbanks,
Morning clouds adorn the sky.
What cultivation in past lives brought her to this?
If she is not of the mortal world, she must truly be an immortal.
The orchid is naturally elegant, the chrysanthemum naturally fragrant,
Thinking of the beautiful one, when could I ever forget?
The clear shallows of Penglai cannot be reached,
I wish to follow her, riding upon a phoenix.
The heavenly winds blow fiercely at my sleeves,
The cold jade dew soaks my garments.
I moisten the fine hairs of my brush to pour out my thoughts,
Pacing hesitantly before her jade-like visage."

Qinxian said, "A fine rhapsody. It speaks precisely of the wind in the pines and rain on the bamboo, of immortal dew and bright pearls, perfectly capturing your lean elegance and delicate beauty." Chunxi replied, "This is Qianzhou taking his rhapsody too seriously and forgetting the subject entirely." Qinxian noted, "Yet it captures your essence perfectly." He then looked at the tenth panel, showing a fairy maiden standing beneath a cassia tree, exuding a romantic grace with a beautiful, longing gaze. The title read:

"Changong Flower Historian, Immortal Wang." Knowing this was Guibao, he read the five-character ancient verse by Wang Xun:

"Green and verdant, it hangs in the moon,
The flowers have bloomed, and autumn has arrived.
The bright and pure maiden of the Toad Palace,
Often feels sorrow when gazing into her mirror.
Ever since she stole the elixir and fled,
She has had no ties to the mortal world.
The Palace of Vast Cold holds twenty thousand households,
With twelve towers of gleaming pearls.
It rounds and then wanes again,
The wheel turning its cycle every day.
The people of the world can only gaze up in vain,
Unable to see her long, delicate moth-eyebrows.
The waters of Penglai are clear and shallow,
Perhaps one could steer a divine boat there.
Gazing across the banks of the Silver River,
On the Seventh Night, they pour out their sorrows of parting.
But for this single, solitary moon,
Even a rainbow staircase is hard to find.
How can we bring Zhang Lihua here,
To playfully roam in her white silk robes?"

Qinxian exclaimed, "Good poem, good poem! Reading it leaves a lingering fragrance in the mouth. Ruixiang truly looks like Chang'e." Guibao replied, "It's not me; it's the Changong Flower Historian." The others chimed in, "With all these poems, lyrics, and eulogies, they're just competing with each other in cleverness and skill; who among them actually remembers the person they're writing about? Only Zhujun's poem and the eulogies for Jingyi and Yuxiang actually stick to the subject." Qinxian disagreed, "They are all very fitting. If you swapped the poems around to different people, they wouldn't make sense anymore." Baozhu added, "Only Jingfang's piece is absolutely irreplaceable." Qinxian then looked at the eleventh panel, depicting an apricot blossom with a fairy maiden standing beneath it, her pearl sash and jade pendants looking utterly captivating. The title read: "Jidi Flower Historian." Knowing this was Qiguan, he read the preface by Yan Zhongqing:

"The Jidi Flower Historian, Immortal Qin, plays gracefully in the mortal realm. Those who see her compare her to the line, 'On the branches of the red apricot, the spirit of spring is in tumult.'
Her bright eyes are adept at glancing, her smiling dimples are ever present.
Her vibrant powder entangles the emotions, her broken red reflects upon her flesh.
Her swaying hairpins transform into sparrows, her bright mirror reflects soaring phoenixes.
What harm is there in housing her in a golden chamber? She shares the very color of the jade screens.
Yet her heart has long remained unpledged, and her fierce nature endures.
Like the weaving maiden at her loom, she is often seen throwing the shuttle to reject suitors;
Like the river goddess rising from the water, she has yet to meet the one for whom she would untie her jade pendant.
Her cloud-like robes stir in the wind, giving birth to golden lotuses with every step;
Her misty garments trail their fragrance, startling the jade trees morning after morning.
Who would not call her peerless in the mortal world, and why should she feel unworthy of being unequaled among the immortals?
If one were to judge the beauties of the six palaces, she would surely claim the highest rank;
If we speak of the year's governing flowers, she is truly the phoenix tail among them."

Qinxian offered fervent praise for a long while. Huifang observed, "Look at these poems and prose pieces; each has its own distinct style and structure. The meters are never confused, the forms are sharply defined—they are all masterworks, hard to rank in excellence." Qinxian replied, "Though they are but short pieces, they are like auspicious feathers and fragments of divine light scattered across the mortal world, ultimately surpassing a five-colored peacock screen. To receive such eulogies means our years of hardship were not in vain." Everyone was filled with joy.

Qinxian stayed at Nine-Fragrance Tower for a meal, and they sat chatting idly. Baozhu suddenly spoke up: "Today, all of us brothers are gathered together. I was thinking, the ten of us sank together into the mire of the capital's theater world, and now, every one of us has leapt free. Though some among us suffered greatly and others found comfort, from this day forward, as long as each of us minds our own duty, there should be no more storms. However, our group of ten can hardly be considered a rarity in this world. What is truly rare in this world is that circle of famous scholars who elevated us to this position. Tell me, are your hearts not filled with gratitude?" The others replied, "How could we not be grateful?" Baozhu pressed, "If we are grateful, how should we think of repaying them?" None of them had an answer. Baozhu continued, "I have thought of a way to repay them. Since they carved portraits of us and made us flower gods, why shouldn't we carve portraits of them and enshrine them upstairs? They call us flower historians; we shall call them literary stars. Modeled after Sikong Tu's *Poetic Categories*, each of us will compose a four-character eulogy to be carved alongside them. What do you think of this repayment?" Sulan responded, "This is an exceptionally brilliant idea, but our poetry is no match for theirs. And who could we ask to paint these portraits?" Huifang said, "Yaoqing and Little Mei can split the painting between them. There's no need to paint formal attire—depicting them in casual, unbuttoned ease would be best. Let us first draft the eulogies tonight, show them to the scholars tomorrow, and then begin the painting. We will each return the courtesy to our respective patron; writing a single eulogy won't be too taxing."

Qinxian was delighted in his heart. He took his leave and went home, spending the evening composing his thoughts without even mentioning the plan to Ziyu. By the end of that night, everyone had finished composing their eulogies.

The next day, Qinxian arrived first at Nine-Fragrance Tower to show his eulogy to the others. They gathered to evaluate them, each pondering their own words, then collectively polishing the lines until they were perfect. Baozhu then transcribed them all together into a clean copy. The famous scholars began arriving one by one, and Lord Hua and Jin Jifu soon joined them. True to their word, the scholars intended to offer sacrifices to the flower gods, but Baozhu and the others stopped them. Nevertheless, an incense table was set up, and the scholars poured libations and burned incense, though they stopped short of bowing. Afterward, four banquet tables were arranged on the lower floor of Nine-Fragrance Tower, and they took their seats in order of age. This gathering found everyone completely satisfied and joyous, with nothing lacking. Delicacies were spread before them, fine dishes arrayed in abundance, while the fragrance of flowers and the warmth of human presence swirled throughout the hall.

When the wine had brought a pleasant flush to everyone, Baozhu left his seat to address the room: "The ten of us, including myself, entered the delusive ford together, and today we have reached the shore of awakening. If in the future we can strive to amend our past faults, diligently cultivate good karma, and stand proudly among men, it will all be due to the uplifting strength of you noble gentlemen. Yet, while our hearts are full of gratitude, we lack the power to repay your virtue. We can only offer a stick of pure incense each day, praying that you noble gentlemen will enjoy boundless longevity and prosperity until your hair turns white. Yesterday, the ten of us consulted together and resolved that we wish to set up longevity tablets for you noble gentlemen as 'literary stars' on the upper floor of Nine-Fragrance Tower. We wish to carve them in stone just as you did for us, offering our devotions morning and evening, and appending a few words to each so that we may chant them devoutly like Buddhist sutras. We do not know if you noble gentlemen will despise our humble station or find our crude brushwork profane, but will you allow us to fulfill this wish?" The scholars were overjoyed; every one of them was willing, though they offered a few words of polite refusal in return. Baozhu continued, "Master Duxiang promotes and champions elegance and culture; we have no choice but to establish a separate rank for him above all the literary stars, naming him: 'Leader of the Immortal Circle.' We wonder if you noble gentlemen agree?" Everyone replied, "Absolutely." Ziyun protested, "How could I dare accept such a title?" Baozhu then respectfully brought out the poetry manuscript, which had already been transcribed together in neat, upright regular script. Excluding the "Leader of the Immortal Circle," Literary Star Xu, the rest were ordered by age: second was the Escaped Grade Among Immortals, Literary Star Xiao; third, the Witty Grade, Literary Star Gao; fourth, the Wild Grade, Literary Star Shi; fifth, the Lofty Grade, Literary Star Yan; sixth, the Harmonious Grade, Literary Star Liu; seventh, the Joyous Grade, Literary Star Wang; eighth, the Splendid Grade, Literary Star Tian; ninth, the Bold Grade, Literary Star Hua; tenth, the Upper Grade, Literary Star Jin; and eleventh, the Upright Grade, Literary Star Mei. The scholars humbly demurred, "These character grades are far too extravagant in their praise!" They then read the first piece, jointly composed by the ten of them, titled *Leader of the Immortal Circle*:

"The leader of the immortals, capable of embracing all.
Neither detached nor clinging, neither approaching nor leaving.
Attaining great freedom, possessing vast means.
Without personal desire, without selfish intent.
The pale moon flows across the sky, its light missing nothing.
The azure sky is free of clouds, the heavenly dew falls of its own accord.
The great bell is hollow within—strike it with but an inch of wood, and it will ring."

The second was Jin Shoufang's *Escaped Grade Among Immortals*:

"Because he is free, he is therefore plain; because he is free, he is therefore at leisure.
The crane cries in the forest, the clouds sleep on the mountain.
The autumn flowers are graceful and beautiful, the clear breeze comes and goes.
Gazing toward that bamboo grove, a guest bears a smiling face.
Washing his feet in a clear stream, cradling a zither at the Zen gate.
Plum blossoms by the riverbank, chrysanthemums by the hedge.
Boiling tea by a small window, the pine pollen ripens on its own."

The third was Yulin's *Witty Grade Among Immortals*:

"Disheveled hair and coarse clothes are no less than a beautiful adornment.
His laughter, scolding, anger, and curses all transform into elegant prose.
The humor of Dongfang Shuo, the hidden depths of Chunyu Kun.
Turning the four seats upside down, dominating the entire hall.
His words are never cruel, his actions never breach propriety.
Relaxed and unhurried, taking things as they come.
With a single smile from Maitreya, everyone is filled with great joy."

The fourth was Wang Lanbao's *Wild Grade Among Immortals*:

"Summoning dragons to plow the mist, sharpening his blade to carve the clouds.
A fierce gale rises from all sides, scattering the falling blossoms.
His hand grasps the bright moon, his belly bakes in the slanting evening sun.
The vast, endless blue sky above, the sparse and scattered crowds below.
Drunk to death, drunk to life—I pay it no mind.
Riding a whale across the azure sea, his hair unbound in the Jade Capital.
Though robes and caps are fine enough, they cannot bind his immortal bones."

The fifth was Qin Qiguan's *Lofty Grade Among Immortals*:

"A solitary crane pierces the mist, the returning wild goose flies far.
At the distant edge of the sky, the green mountains are faint among the clouds.
Standing alone upon a thousand-fathom cliff, a good wind blows his robes.
Looking up from the autumn courtyard, the moon is bright and the stars are sparse.
The ancient pine stands proudly alone; the green vines find it hard to cling.
Mount Taihua reaches into the clouds, Penglai lies across the waters.
Who can climb its peak? One can only gaze up in awe."

The sixth was Lin Chunxi's *Harmonious Grade Among Immortals*:

"The five flavors are perfectly balanced, the five tones are at peace.
The warm air enters the pitch-pipes, the spring wind blows of its own accord.
The rising sun is soft and gentle, the clear light vies for brightness.
Cloud brilliance gathers like brocade, the moon fills the river to the brim.
One tune of *Nishang*, nine movements of *Xiaoshao*.
Neither boastful nor solemn, sometimes resting, sometimes at leisure.
Kind and pleasing to us—he is truly like Liuxia Hui."

The seventh was Wang Guibao's *Joyous Grade Among Immortals*:

"Pure, centered harmony, his joy is boundless.
The boundaries between fields are utterly erased; frankness is the basis of his friendships.
Getting drunk beneath the autumn moon, embracing flowers in the spring dawn.
The bridal chamber is fragrant and warm, the music in the golden hall rings high.
The incense of his heart spits forth blossoms, the stamens of his thoughts enclose their buds.
He is wealthy and healthy, treating others as guests and friends.
Husband and wife in perfect union, his parents enjoying long life."

The eighth was Su Huifang's *Splendid Grade Among Immortals*:

"Walking by day in brocade robes, his jade-like face adorned with flowers.
The perfect jade moon fills the night, the bright pearl spits forth its brilliance.
The morning sun is dazzling, the flowing clouds are radiant.
The golden basin catches the dew, the whitewashed walls are veiled in gauze.
Majestic and wondrous in form, adorned with the hairpins of a heavenly maiden.
His jade pendants ring of their own accord, marten furs serve as his ornament.
The clouds draw near Penglai, and gazing upon them, they shine in five colors."

The ninth was Lu Sulan's *Bold Grade Among Immortals*:

"Wearing a sword and arraying halberds, casting tallies and splitting seals.
Following my own will, I shatter your coral.
Screens of purple silk, marten robes of red brocade.
Singing loudly and freely, spitting jade and spraying pearls.
Li Taibai admiring himself, striking and chipping the spittoon.
The north wind sweeps across the sky, snowflakes fall as large as palms.
The horn sounds at Luntai, and for a long time, there is no echoing response."

The tenth was Yuan Baozhu's *Upper Grade Among Immortals*:

"The supreme upper grade—this gentleman is first.
The quiet one holds many marvels, floating lightly above the crowd.
Possessing great wisdom, he is broadly learned and widely heard.
Gentle, good, and plain, versed in the *Nine Mounds* and *The Three Tomes*.
Open and upright, holding uncut jade that harbors fragrance within.
Lofty talk and strong debate, discussing swords and analyzing prose.
Though out of step with the times, he remains free and cloud-borne."

The eleventh was Qu Qinxian's *Upright Grade Among Immortals*:

"Vermilion is the true color, elegance is the true sound.
Archery is used to observe virtue; one's own body is the standard.
Sorrow and joy spring from true nature, yet never exceed the bounds of emotion.
The light of pearls and the color of the moon, storing crystal within.
The strings of Yu and the dances of Xia, an auspicious era bright and clear.
The sweet spring is no ordinary water, the lucky fungus is no common grass.
Auspicious stars and celebratory clouds—all say these are forever rare."

After reading them, the scholars' faces lit up with joy, and they offered endless praise, saying, "This can truly be called throwing a wooden peach and receiving a precious jade in return." They drank freely and cheered until they dispersed for the day.

Sulan and Chunxi spent several days painting, then traced the designs onto the stone and inscribed the eulogies in vermilion. In total, the work took over twenty days to complete. They chose the third day of the third month to install the longevity tablets on the upper floor of Nine-Fragrance Tower. Qinxian came to Baozhu to discuss the matter, saying that they must compose a formal invocation text to demonstrate their sincerity; Baozhu and the others agreed completely. Thus, the ten of them deliberated together and pieced together a text, revising and polishing it several times until no trace of patchwork remained. Baozhu said, "For tomorrow's public invocation, we must invite all the famous scholars. Furthermore, now that we have leapt out of the pear orchard, all the belongings we once owned are utterly useless. The roots of sin must be cleansed, the realm of lust entirely eradicated. Let us take all our remaining hairpins and ornaments and, in front of the scholars, melt them down together; as for our dancing sleeves and singing skirts, we shall burn them in a single fire—would that not be exhilarating?" Everyone replied, "That exactly matches our own thoughts. Only Qinxian has none of these things left; the rest of us will gather ours together to be burned tomorrow." When the third day arrived, the upper floor of Nine-Fragrance Tower was crowded with incense and flowers, and fruits and vegetables were laid out in abundance. The flower historians lined up as sisters, while the scholars took their places in the order of brothers. Baozhu had arranged everything with the utmost piety and reverence. The famous scholars arrived together, and upon coming upstairs, they found paired red candles blazing and fragrant smoke coiling like clouds. The ten flower historians invited them to sit, and then bowed together in unison; how could the scholars accept such a gesture? They hurriedly helped them up. Baozhu said, "Yesterday, Yulang suggested that we should prepare an invocation. We hastily pieced one together, and we beg you to correct it for us."

She then brought out the invocation text. Gao Pin said, "Very well, I shall read it aloud." Gao Pin read it in a loud, clear voice, while the scholars listened closely. They heard Gao Pin read:

"On this day of this month of this year, the disciples of Nine-Fragrance Tower, Flower Historian Yuan Baozhu and the others, respectfully burn incense of a hundred blends and brew wine of a hundred flowers, offering them before the seats of the literary stars, and pray thus: These literary stars are the spirits of rivers and peaks; with Kui on the left and Bi on the right, they weave the classics and histories. Thus, in heaven they are constellations, and in the mortal world, they are the inheritors of transmission. Their brightness is like fire, their warmth is like spring. Rooted in nature, they uphold the moral bonds and human relations; manifested in words, they create miraculous transformations. Their speech must come from the heart, their emotions are naturally wondrous. With one note of heavenly music, ten thousand hollows echo in response. Threads of thought are touched and multiply, silk strands twine and coil. Facing the mirror, they look at themselves; turning to their shadows, they smile alone. Seeking substance within emptiness, distinguishing the bad from the good. Spring winds and autumn moons—what else is there to know? Bright eyes and white teeth—what is to be done with them? Powder-white and dark-green brows, iron horses and golden spears. Clear songs winding and turning, wondrous dances whirling gracefully. Fast as a team of galloping horses, yielding as the passing waves. Grieving over the single rut of past and present, enduring the constant wearing away of sun and moon. Reflecting upon creation, they craft their literature. Dividing things by their kind, gathering them by their category. Matching the jade cup of Li Taibai, turning praise and blame upside down. Harmonizing with the zither and se, clear as the sheng and huang. Lingering and elegant in the style of the *Encountering Sorrow*, unbridled in the manner of Han Yu and Zhuangzi. Neither bizarre nor chaotic, capturing both brilliance and fragrance. Their underlying meaning is strict and upright, their phrasing is bright and clear. Crimson clouds beautify the sky and burst with color; golden blades reflect the sun and flash with light. Springs pour through ravines in a rushing torrent; flowers brave the wind in a frantic dance. Autumn withers a whole courtyard, leaving only a few specks of lingering fragrance. Ghosts weep in the night, and spirits are startled by day. Casting tripods to represent all things, exhausting every form and shape. Demon maidens stand by their side, dragon girls step forward to greet them. Golden branches and kingfisher feathers, lightning flashes and thunder rolls. Huo Xiaoyu brushes the paper, Dong Shuangcheng holds the inkstone. The divine maidens grant them the brush, causing them to compose their works. We pray: The colors of the brush are five; the blossoms of the brush are half-open, half-spitting forth. The crashing of stones sounds like heaven's war drums; the blue luan sings and the purple phoenix dances; these small, chattering words are enough to last a thousand ages."

When the invocation was finished, the flower historians all bowed together. They then took those dancing skirts, singing fans, kingfisher feathers, and gold ornaments, and burned them by the Taihu rocks in the garden. The famous scholars watched the five-colored flames flashing gold and green. Just as the fire was nearly burned out, a sudden gust of fragrant wind blew the ashes high into the air. They fluttered and drifted, reflecting against a round, red sun, like countless flowers and butterflies dancing in the air. Gold dazzled and paper blinded, the fragrance filled the nose. The swirl rose higher and higher until, halfway up the sky, it transformed into ten thousand points of golden light, which vanished in a single flash.

In the garden, all the myriad flowers seemed to smile, trembling as if they were about to speak. Truly:

"Meeting in person the heavenly maiden as she scatters her flowers,
Then receiving from her hand a single brush that brings forth blossoms.
The blue sea has much sorrow, yet remains unfilled;
The road to Mount Penglai is long, with no set date of arrival.
Faces stained with wind and dust, tracks of wheels and hooves;
The prose of Xu and Yu, the poetry of Wen and Li.
I have my own feelings—I beg you, do not ask;
The gains and losses within this, my inch-long heart alone knows."
