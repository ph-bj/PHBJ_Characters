Chapter 60 — Jin Jifu brings Pinhua Baojian to its conclusion; Yuan Baozhu leads the invocation honoring the literary stars.

After New Year came Lantern Festival again: six streets and three markets blazing with silver trees and flowered lamps. On the thirteenth, Ziyun hosted a grand gathering for Lord Hua, Tian Chunhang, Mei Ziyu, Shi Nanxiang, Gao Pin, Yan Zhongqing, Liu Wenze, Wang Xun, Xiao Cixian, Jin Su, Qu Qinxian, and all from Nine-Fragrance Garden. Qinxian still felt uneasy before Lord Hua, yet Hua asked nothing of old affairs and treated him courteously. By then Qinxian had left the dan circle and entered the scholars' circle; once proud and aloof as a young dan, he now kept himself humbly, so everyone respected him more and none mocked him. With the former famous dans he remained as before, without estrangement. Cups and lots crossed all day; at night lamps shone in splendor. After Lord Hua returned to the city, Ziyun tested those lantern mechanisms again: flowers seemed to bloom in ten thousand forms, and firecrackers were heard for miles. Only after second watch did they brew tea and talk quietly.

Qinxian had tasted every hardship before his trials were finally lifted. Looking back on the bleak monastery, lone lamp, and waning moon, it all felt like waking from a dream. Cixian then told Jifu in detail the old dream scenes Qinxian had once related. Jifu smiled and said to Ziyun and Cixian: "Nine-Fragrance Tower is a superb flower garden with all flowers represented, yet there is only one flower-god tablet hidden in an out-of-the-way corner like an earth-god shrine. Is that not disrespectful to the flower deity? I propose we use those nine to create a Nine-Fragrance Floral History - what do you all think?"

Everyone found the idea novel and asked for details. Cixian said: "I've long had this in mind; I wanted to paint their portraits. Since you now propose it too, nothing could be better. Tomorrow I'll paint all nine one by one; then ask you to polish them and carve them on stone to be enshrined below Nine-Fragrance Tower as flower deities. Only with nine, we still cannot make twelve." Everyone agreed it was excellent. Jifu said: "I do have one thought, though I'm not sure it can work."

Ziyun asked how. Jifu said: "If flower deities must be twelve, we can still make the number by adding Qu Daoweng as Lord of Furong City, and by drawing on Yulang's former-life account of Fairy Du, plus Yulang himself - that gives twelve, does it not?" Chunhang said: "Wonderful. The portraits should be likenesses, but we needn't use true names - attach style names instead. Give each a short eulogy, half true half illusory, to merge person and flower." Ziyu said: "How should the composition be - figures alone, or figures with setting?" Zhongqing said: "Naturally figures alone. Put them in sequence and append a brief biography. After carving, rubbings can be made and mounted as album leaves or handscrolls for posterity." Wenze said: "Make two stones and inset them into east and west pillars." Wang Xun said: "If painting Fairy Du, paint her as on the lotus-picking boat." Jifu said: "Yulang's dream mirror cannot be meaningless. I'll paint a dragon holding that mirror as the opening panel - good?" Everyone said good. Ziyu added that readers would surely suspect deeper meaning in the cloud-dragon motif. Ziyun said: "Let's divide the eleven biographies and praises among us." Cixian said yes. "This major work must be transmitted through Jifu." Jifu answered modestly: "I hardly dare."

Cixian said: "No need for false modesty. Since Master Daosheng passed, in brush-and-ink matters you naturally take precedence; this is public judgment, no need to refuse. I'll do the cloud-dragon panel first; once done, you can revise it." Ziyun said: "We must rely on both of you. For the other ten pieces, let's draw lots. Let Hua Xingbei write one as well." Nanxiang agreed. So they wrote the lots: Qu Daoweng, Fairy Du, and Qu Qinxian were split into two draws, the other nine as nine draws. Strangely, as if writing itself had spirit and former-life ties: Ziyun drew Daoweng; Ziyu drew Fairy Du and Qinxian; Jin Su drew Baozhu; Chunhang drew Huifang; Zhongqing drew Qiguan; Wenze drew Chunxi; Nanxiang drew Lanbao; Wang Xun drew Guibao; Gao Pin drew Yulin; Cixian drew Shoujiu. No one drew Sulan, so that one was assigned to Lord Hua. Once all assignments were fixed, Ziyu said: "Writing biographies is easy enough, but painting is hard, and carving takes time. How many days before it's done?" Jifu said: "Not many. The steles are already polished. We'll paint and carve in parallel by having Ji the Little Dwarf find carvers. At least ten-plus days are needed, and several more to inset the stones. Since we're gathered anyway, let's stay at Nine-Fragrance Garden and rest a few more days so I can focus on painting. Let's reconvene on the first day of second month." All agreed and dispersed.

Ziyu returned home with Qinxian. With a graceful wife inside and a handsome friend outside, fame established and station secured, he was in high spirits. Ziyun wrote a note to Lord Hua asking for Sulan's biography. Cixian meanwhile painted with deft hand and fertile mind; ten full days before completion. After each portrait was done, Jifu revised it, and each was carved in turn, so progress was quick. Ziyun had caught a serious cold and dared not overexert; he asked that Daoweng, Qinxian, and Fairy Du be painted in a single panel and asked Ziyu to write the praise. By the twenty-seventh, biographies and eulogies were all carved, each in the author's own hand copied to stone. On the twenty-eighth they were moved to Nine-Fragrance Tower for insetting, and in one day the work was complete.

On the thirtieth, Qinxian arrived early at Nine-Fragrance Garden to view the stones; the nine former dans came down together. Qinxian said: "We should first offer rites to the flower gods today; tomorrow only then can we hold our gathering. These flower gods are our own portraits. If others came to worship, we'd be unworthy - let the ten of us perform the rite ourselves." Huifang and others agreed. They set wine and fruit, burned fine incense, and all ten bowed together. Qinxian looked at the first eastern panel: clouds above, water below, and between them a hidden dragon exposing one claw to support a mirror. Title: *Pinhua Baojian*. Cixian's inscription read:

"Above not in heaven, below not in fields.
Cloud rises in nine heavens, water issues from deep abyss.
Its transformations are numinous, its aspect ten thousandfold.
A spirit pearl rounded, a bright mirror suspended.
It illumines subtle and hidden, reveals treachery and worth.
Like moon descending, like water in freshness.
It can hide what must be hidden, and make radiant what is radiant."

The second panel portrayed a scholar in scarf and Daoist robe, flanked by one male and one female attendant spirit, titled:

"Portrait of Lord Daoweng of Sanlu, Master of the Nine-Fragrance Flowers, with attendants and Flower Historian Fairy Du."
Beneath was a eulogy in Ziyu's hand:

"Your qi became cloud, your spirit became water;
in heaven and on earth, endless and without halt.
As registrar of letters you are a Lang official; as lord of flowers you are their master.
Essence of constellations, ancestor of all blooms.
On the left Ying Qiongyao, on the right Qing Shanshan;
one breath and two breaths, same source by different paths.
Five colors cast brilliance, nine blossoms stream fragrance;
heart-flower and meaning-stamen, omen of literary fortune."

Baozhu said: "These praises are truly excellent. If our actual histories were written in, though not exactly disgraceful, they would still be low traces." Huifang said yes: "Those flower records and critiques praise beauty and skill, yet cannot escape theater habits. This time we have truly jumped beyond that threshold." Sulan and the others all nodded and sighed.

Qinxian then viewed the third panel: an immortal lady with cloud coiffure and mist gauze, pure and stunning, holding a spray of orchid - he knew it was Huifang. The title read: "Jinwen Flower Historian, Immortal Su." Chunhang's colophon read:

"Immortal Su, Jinwen Flower Historian, keen in spirit and swift in understanding, complexion like jade.
Snow kneaded into skin, moon carved into bone.
She scatters blossoms and cuts color; she can toss grain and make pearls.
Her playful supernatural arts all arise from true samadhi.
She once played in the human world, leaving regrets beneath peach blossoms.
Her fairy shadow by riverbank comes and goes;
her Luo-river radiance parts and joins.
Xiao Shi often hangs from colored phoenix;
Pei Hang still remains separated at Blue Bridge.
Thus tenfold pearl nets should be tied to guard a golden house atop Kunlun jade peaks;
how fortunate to lift nine-layer silver screens and glimpse her plain face beneath moonlight on jade terraces."

Qinxian said: "This colophon is apt - especially 'playful supernatural arts all arise from samadhi.'" Huifang laughed: "However he says it, how could that still be us?" Then they viewed the fourth panel: an immortal lady in rainbow robes and moon ornaments, very charming, holding a bright pearl, titled "Nongzhu Flower Historian, Immortal Yuan," with Jin Su's eulogy:

"Immortal dew in sky, bright pearl from sea;
spirit harmonized with spring, pure qi becomes color.
It travels without legs, shines without night.
Jade flower and fragrant stamen, sovereign beauty and celestial fragrance.
Pearls adorn carriage, cloud brocade sews robes.
Golden bough and kingfisher plumes, jade brilliance clear.
Full moonlight, long road to Penglai.
Beautiful and stately, yet elegant and refined.
Among scholars filling the palace, this one is first among great households."

Qinxian said: "Yaoqing's gorgeous youth has been completely drawn by Jingyi and praised by Jifu." Baozhu said: "As flower deity perhaps - am I fit for this?" The fifth panel showed an immortal lady with drifting grace and pale beauty like flowing water, holding a single orchid. Title: "Suxin Flower Historian, Immortal Lu." A short biography by Lord Hua followed:

"Immortal Lu is quick and perceptive, with peerless appearance and talents beyond others.
She often wore violet gauze and wandered chanting among wind and dew.
Her body fragrance matched orchid and cinnamon scent from within and without.
Skilled in lyric and calligraphy; works revealed in the human world could not be bought even for a thousand strings.
Zhong Rong once judged poetry, saying Yan Yanzhi's gilt-and-color craft was not like Xie Lingyun's dawn-lotus freshness.
Such plain-faced elegance is ultimate splendor - is this immortal not lotus incarnate?"

Qinxian laughed: "These lines resemble Xiangwan's portrait even more. 'Violet gauze wandering in wind and dew' and 'lotus incarnation' cannot be shifted to anyone else - truly you." Sulan smiled: "How can I deserve it? Since we praise flower gods, naturally we must extol to the full." Qinxian looked at the sixth panel: an immortal lady, slender and weak, seeming to dance in wind, light as if in the palm, with a golden buyao hairpin. Title: "Qianqian Flower Historian, Immortal Jin." Beneath was Xiao Cixian's regulated heptasyllabic poem:

"New-moon brows so slender and slight,
heaven-born brilliance needing no adornment.
Mandarin brocade made into nine-flower curtains,
shark pearls strung into tenfold screens.
She hides in fairy groves by jade trees,
returns from karma and records in jade registers.
I fear even if immortals are asked to stay, she cannot be kept;
dawn wind may blow her up to green cloud tips."

Qinxian said: "Her spirit and bone are all there." Shoufang laughed: "The thinness resembles me somewhat; the rest shames me." Then they viewed the seventh panel: an immortal lady beneath two jade trees, with swaying grace. Title: "Juanjuan Flower Historian, Immortal Li," with Gao Pin's poem. Qinxian said: "Would Gao Zhuoran ever speak kindly?" Yulin said: "This time he was not satirical." Huifang said: "For him, this is already very favorable." Qinxian read:

"Flower-feeling and moon-color, truly graceful;
jade trees in wind, still more willowy.
In bed she knows not the worth of orchid musk;
in dreams she is shy to be a rain-cloud immortal.
Coral pillow births red flush,
kingfisher tower locks green mist.
Banished to Tiantai to keep lone watch,
azure peach and flowing stream year after year."

Qinxian praised it highly for capturing Peixian's fragrant elegance. Yulin said: "Still not that good, with some sting - those bed and dream lines, what's good there?" Huifang said there was no malice, only writing his delicate nobility. Baozhu said: "For Zhuoran, this already counts as goodwill. If asked to write solemnly he could, but his oily tongue is used to this style. Has he ever spoken a few fully proper words? Jifu told a joke from when he came to the capital last year. Zhuoran had an uncle who invited him to dinner with several guests. The uncle asked, 'When you went home last year, was my household well?' Zhuoran replied, 'Very well. Last month aunt gave birth to another cousin.' The uncle froze, thinking: I've been away three or four years - how could I have a new son? In front of guests he could not challenge. After guests left, he asked what that line meant. Guess Zhuoran's answer? He said, 'Meeting uncle for the first time, naturally I sought an auspicious phrase and this one came to mouth; in fact it didn't happen.' The uncle nearly died of anger but could do nothing. He jokes even with elders - how much more with others?" Everyone burst out laughing: "Jifu's tongue yields to none either." Then they viewed the eighth panel: an immortal lady in brocade, jade-faced, sword at waist, like Gongsun Daniang. Title: "Xiayin Flower Historian, Immortal Wang." Qinxian knew it was Lanbao. Beneath was Shi Nanxiang's ancient-style poem:

"I watch Immortal Wang dance the spirit sword,
hand drawing cold spring like a single thread.
Three beats of Jie drum begin,
wind already meets the face in bright flow.
Rainbow reflects in water and rounds as one,
streaming lightning threads cloud in curved line.
She breaks green-shafted spears to points,
knocks down feathered arrows in a shower.
In brocade and jade how graceful,
white throat, red cheeks, long dark brows.
Cloud skirt lifts, brocade boots rise;
she goes like flying bird, comes like thunder.
Viewers crowd from four sides,
envying not heroes but this alluring force.
Green cloud hair newly piled,
nine-beam flowers and ancient shaking pins.
I borrow defense and not fame,
in my own charm I still cherish life.
Look at all the yellow-robed men in the world -
how many grudges never repaid."

Qinxian praised it as finer in detail than Du Fu's Sword Dance ode. Wang Lanbao only smiled. Huifang said that when Xi Eleven made trouble last year, thanks to Lanbao he had no way otherwise. Sulan asked if he really feared Xi Eleven and whether he was fiercer than Pan Third. Huifang said Pan was useless, but Xi in a rage was like that driver Wei Pincai once sent - fearsome. Lanbao asked what he'd do if he were absent that day. Huifang said he'd hide and not come out. Qinxian asked what Xi Eleven looked like; Lanbao mimed him and Qinxian laughed. Huifang said he heard Xi had left the capital, but saw someone in a barber shop recently who looked just like the lad Xi once brought; if not him, surely his brother - no one could look so similar. Lanbao said perhaps Xi had not taken him after all; that little cur was fit only to shave heads. Qinxian then viewed the ninth panel: a plum tree with a magpie, and beneath it an immortal lady. Title: "Baochun Flower Historian, Immortal Lin." Liu Wenze's short fu followed:

"On plum branches birds announce spring;
beneath plum shade stands a jade person.
Du Lanxiang's marriage cannot be seen;
fortunate to meet true Green Sepal Blossom.
Green sleeves fluttering, white robe self-beautified.
Charm born in bones, elegance from nature.
Removing pearl hairpins makes her more beautiful;
washing powder leaves her yet more fair.
Slender as new moon amid clouds,
light as clear haze outside flowers.
Autumn water fills the ford,
dawn glow adorns the sky.
How cultivated to be thus -
is she not human and yet truly immortal?
Orchid is graceful, chrysanthemum fragrant;
how can I forget this beauty?
Penglai is too remote to reach,
I would follow riding a phoenix.
Heavenly wind blows sleeves in haste,
jade dew chills the robe.
Biting brush tip to write this out,
I linger bewildered before a jade face."

Qinxian praised it as pine-wind and bamboo-rain, fairy dew and bright pearls, capturing all of Chunxi's clear full grace. Chunxi said Wenzhou wrote too seriously and forgot the assigned tone. Qinxian said even so it fit him. Then they viewed the tenth panel: under a cassia tree stood an immortal lady with romantic bearing and bright expressive eyes. Title:

"Toad-Palace Flower Historian, Immortal Wang." They knew it was Guibao. Wang Xun's pentasyllabic ancient poem read:

"Green, green hangs in the moon;
its flowers have reached autumn.
Bright, bright maiden of toad palace,
before mirror always self-sorrowing.
Since she stole elixir and fled,
she has no tie with the world.
Twenty thousand households in Guanghan,
twelve pearl towers.
Round then lacking,
its cycle turns once daily.
People merely gaze upward,
not seeing those cultivated moth-brows.
Penglai waters are shallow now,
perhaps a divine boat may be rowed.
Silver River looks across banks,
on Qixi she tells separation grief.
Only this one wheel of moon,
rainbow ladder still impossible to seek.
Would that Zhang Lihua might come
in plain white to wander in delight."

Qinxian praised it warmly, saying one's mouth turned fragrant reading it, and Ruizhen truly resembled Chang'e. Guibao said: "Not me - this is Toad-Palace Flower Historian." Others said these poems and praises were each competing in marvel; they were written to type, not to the specific person - except Zhujun's poem and Jingyi/Yuxiang's two praises, which were tightly on theme. Qinxian answered that all were close; swap the names and they'd no longer fit. Baozhu said only Jingfang's piece could never be reassigned. Qinxian viewed the eleventh panel: an apricot blossom with an immortal lady beneath, pearl girdle and jade sash, highly alluring. Title: "Jidi Flower Historian." Knowing it was Qiguan, he read Yan Zhongqing's preface:

"Qin Immortal, Jidi Flower Historian, playing in the human world, of whom people say: 'On red-apricot branches spring tumult stirs.'
Bright eyes adept at glancing, smile dimples often opening.
Vivid powder coils emotion, broken red reflects flesh.
Hairpin-birds transform, mirror-phoenixes fly.
Why fear to house her in a golden chamber, with jade screens matching hue?
Yet her heart long ungranted, fierce nature still preserved.
As weaving maiden at her loom, she repeatedly throws the shuttle;
as water goddess rising, she has not yet met one who can untie her girdle.
Cloud robe moves in wind, birthing golden lotuses step by step;
mist gauze and fragrance drift, startling jade trees each dawn.
Who would not call her unmatched in the human world, nor deny her peerless among immortals?
If judging six-palace beauties, she should yield first place;
if naming yearly flower-officers, she is truly phoenix-tail among them."

Qinxian praised it at length. Huifang said the pieces each had their own style and meter, forms never mixed, all from skilled writers, hard to rank. Qinxian said these were small writings, yet like auspicious feathers scattered through the world, better than five-color peacock screens; with such a praise they had not suffered those hard years in vain. All were delighted.

Qinxian dined at Nine-Fragrance Tower and sat chatting. Suddenly Baozhu said: "Today all brothers are gathered. The ten of us sank together in the theater mire in the capital; now each has climbed out. Some suffered, some had ease, but if from now on each keeps his place and conduct, there should be no new storms. Still, our sort is not rare in the world. What is rare is that this circle of famous scholars raised us to such standing. Are you not grateful?" Everyone said of course. Baozhu said: "If grateful, how shall we repay?" None answered. Baozhu said: "I have a method. Since they carved our likenesses as flower gods, why not carve theirs and enshrine upstairs? They call us flower historians; we call them literary stars. Following Sikong's *Poetic Categories*, each of us writes one four-character eulogy, carved with the portrait. Is this repayment good?" Sulan said: "Excellent, though our poems won't match theirs. And who will paint?" Huifang said: "Yaoqing and Little Mei can divide the painting. No need for full costume - no shirts, no shoes is most elegant. Tonight we'll draft all eulogies first and show them tomorrow, then paint. We each return one courtesy gift; one short eulogy is not hard."

Qinxian was delighted, took leave, and at night began drafting but did not mention this to Ziyu. By night's end each person's eulogy was done.

Next day Qinxian went first to Nine-Fragrance Tower to show the drafts. They reviewed and revised repeatedly, then collectively polished them to near perfection; Baozhu copied a clean master. Soon the scholars arrived in numbers; Lord Hua and Jin Jifu came too. They indeed intended to worship the flower gods, but Baozhu and others stopped them; still an incense table was set, and the scholars poured libation and burned incense without prostration. Then four banquet tables were set below Nine-Fragrance Tower, seated by age. This gathering found everyone fully satisfied and cheerful, nothing lacking. Delicacies were arranged in ranks; fragrance of flowers and people swirled through the hall.

When wine reached pleasant heat, Baozhu stepped out and addressed them: "We ten entered the deluded ford together and now have reached awakening shore. Hereafter we shall strive to amend old faults and cultivate better fruits. If we now may stand among proper people, it is all due to your patrons' lifting hands. Though grateful in heart, we lack power to repay; only one stick of pure incense each day can we offer, wishing all patrons long life, enduring honor, and white-haired prosperity. Yesterday we ten resolved to install your literary-star spirit seats upstairs in Nine-Fragrance Tower and carve them in stone likewise, bowing morning and evening in devotion, with short lines appended for recitation. We dare ask whether you will not disdain this humble place and common brush, and allow us to fulfill this wish." The scholars were overjoyed and all consented, while modestly declining at first. Baozhu added: "Master Duxiang who leads all elegance should be in a separate rank above all these literary stars, titled 'Leader of the Immortal Circle.' Do you approve?" All said yes. Ziyun protested humility. Baozhu then respectfully produced the fair-copy manuscript. Besides 'Leader of the Immortal Circle, Literary Star Xu,' the rest were ordered by age: second, 'Immortal Circle - Escaped Grade, Literary Star Xiao'; third, 'Witty Grade, Literary Star Gao'; fourth, 'Wild Grade, Literary Star Shi'; fifth, 'Lofty Grade, Literary Star Yan'; sixth, 'Harmonious Grade, Literary Star Liu'; seventh, 'Joyous Grade, Literary Star Wang'; eighth, 'Splendid Grade, Literary Star Tian'; ninth, 'Bold Grade, Literary Star Hua'; tenth, 'Upper Grade, Literary Star Jin'; eleventh, 'Upright Grade, Literary Star Mei.' The scholars protested these ranks were overpraise. They then read the first piece, jointly written by the ten, titled *Leader of the Immortal Circle*:

"Leader among immortals, able to unite all.
Neither clinging nor sticking, neither too near nor far.
Great freedom attained, vast means at hand.
No private desire, no private self.
Plain moon flowing the sky, illuminating without omission.
Clear vault without cloud, celestial dew descends by itself.
Great bell hollow within - even an inch of rod can make it sound."

Second was Jin Shoufang's *Escaped Grade Among Immortals*:

"Because of ease, therefore pale; because of ease, therefore serene.
Crane cries in forest, cloud sleeps in mountain.
Autumn flowers graceful, clear wind coming and going.
Looking to that bamboo grove, a guest with smiling face.
Washing feet in clear stream, holding qin by Zen gate.
Plum by riverbank, chrysanthemum by hedge.
At small window dividing tea, pine blossoms ripen by themselves."

Third was Yulin's *Witty Grade Among Immortals*:

"Disordered hair and rough clothes are no worse than lovely makeup.
Laughter, scolding, anger, abuse - all become literature.
Dongfang's humor, Chunyu's hidden art.
Turning all four seats upside down, ranging full hall.
Speech not abusive, conduct not out of square.
So it is, so it is - just this and no more.
One smile of Maitreya, and all rejoice."

Fourth was Wang Lanbao's *Wild Grade Among Immortals*:

"Calling dragons to plow mist, whetting blade to cut clouds.
Mad gusts rise on all sides, fallen flowers in confusion.
Hand seizes bright moon, belly dries slanting sun.
Vast blue heaven, sparse human crowd.
Drunk to death, drunk to life - I hear none of it.
Riding whales on blue sea, hair loose in jade capital.
Robes and hats are fine enough, yet this immortal bone cannot be bound."

Fifth was Qin Qiguan's *Lofty Grade Among Immortals*:

"Lone crane pierces smoke, returning wild goose flies far.
At distant sky's edge, green dim among clouds.
Standing alone on thousand cliffs, good wind in robes.
In autumn court looking up, moon bright and stars sparse.
Ancient pine stands by itself, green vines hard to cling.
Taihua enters clouds, Penglai across waters.
Who can climb that peak? One can only look in awe."

Sixth was Lin Chunxi's *Harmonious Grade Among Immortals*:

"Five flavors blended, five tones at peace.
Warm qi enters measure, spring wind moves of itself.
Rising sun soft and broad, clear light competing bright.
Cloud luster gathers like brocade, moon fills stream to brim.
One tune of *Nishang*, nine completions of *Xiaoshao*.
Neither showy nor stern, now resting now at ease.
Kind and pleasing to us - this is Liuxia."

Seventh was Wang Guibao's *Joyous Grade Among Immortals*:

"Pure middle harmony, joy abundant.
Ridges and furrows all erased, frankness as bond.
Drunk moon on autumn eve, embracing flowers in spring dawn.
Fragrant warmth in inner chamber, high music in golden hall.
Heart-fragrance buds, meaning-stamens enclosed.
Called wealth and health, as guest and friend.
Husband and wife in concord, parents with long brows."

Eighth was Su Huifang's *Splendid Grade Among Immortals*:

"In brocade by day, jade face with flowers in hair.
Jade moon full at night, bright pearl吐华.
Morning sun brilliant, streaming clouds radiant.
Golden tray receives dew, powder walls veil gauze.
Majestic wondrous form, heavenly maiden's ornaments.
Jade pendants sound by themselves, sable robes as adornment.
Clouds near Penglai, seen in five colors."

Ninth was Lu Sulan's *Bold Grade Among Immortals*:

"Wearing sword with halberds arrayed, casting tallies and splitting seals.
By my own will, I shatter your coral.
Purple-silk screens, red-brocade sable robes.
Vast song unconstrained, spitting jade and spraying pearls.
Taibai self-admiring, striking cracked spittoon.
North wind crosses sky, snowflakes like palms.
Horn sounds at Luntai, long without answering echo."

Tenth was Yuan Baozhu's *Upper Grade Among Immortals*:

"Supreme upper grade - this lord first.
Stillness breeds many subtleties, drifting beyond the crowd.
Great wisdom possessed, broad learning well heard.
Gentle, good, and plain, versed in Nine Hills and Three Tombs.
Open and upright, holding uncut jade with fragrance inside.
Lofty talk and strong debate, discussing swords and prose.
Though out of season, still free and cloud-borne."

Eleventh was Qu Qinxian's *Upright Grade Among Immortals*:

"Vermilion is true color, elegance true sound.
Archery observes virtue; the body itself is rule.
Sorrow and joy are true nature, yet never excessive.
Pearl light and moon sheen, crystal stored within.
Yu-string and Xia-dance, auspicious age in brightness.
Sweet spring is no mere water, lucky fungus no common grass.
Auspicious stars and celebratory clouds - all say these are ever rare."

After reading, the scholars' faces lit up and praise poured out: "This is truly peach thrown and jade returned in answer." They drank and cheered until dispersing.

Sulan and Chunxi painted for several days, traced onto stone, and wrote inscriptions in vermilion. After more than twenty days all was finished. They chose the third day of third month to install the long-life spirit tablets upstairs in Nine-Fragrance Tower. Qinxian consulted Baozhu that a formal invocation text was required to show sincerity; Baozhu and others agreed deeply. So all ten drafted one text together, revising repeatedly until no seams of patchwork showed. Baozhu said: "At tomorrow's public rite we must invite all scholars. Also, now that we have left the opera world, all old belongings are useless. Evil roots must be cleansed, the realm of lust removed. Let's melt all remaining hairpins and ornaments before the scholars; sleeves and song skirts should be burned in one fire - would that not be exhilarating?" Everyone agreed. "Only Qinxian has none left; we'll gather ours together for tomorrow's burning." On the third day, Nine-Fragrance Tower was crowded with incense and flowers, fruit and vegetables laid out. Flower sisters in rank, literary brothers in order. Baozhu arranged everything with utmost reverence. The scholars arrived together and came upstairs to find paired red candles and cloudlike incense smoke. The ten flower historians seated them, then bowed in unison; the scholars would not accept and quickly raised them. Baozhu said: "Yesterday Yulang proposed an invocation; we patched one together - please correct it."

She handed over the text. Gao Pin said: "Fine, I'll read it aloud." Gao Pin read in a clear voice while all scholars listened closely. He read:

"On this day of such year and month, disciples of Nine-Fragrance Tower, flower historians Yuan Baozhu and others, respectfully burn hundred-blend incense and offer hundred-flower wine before the seats of all literary stars, and pray thus: Those literary stars are spirits of rivers and peaks, with Kui on left and Bi on right, weaving histories and classics. In heaven they are constellations; in the world, inheritors of transmission. Their brightness is like fire; their warmth like spring. Rooted in nature, they uphold norms and human bonds; manifested in diction, they create transformations and marvels. Their speech comes from the center, their feeling naturally wondrous. One note of heavenly music and ten thousand pipes respond. Threads touched become profuse; silk twines and circles. Facing the mirror one sees oneself; turning to one's shadow one smiles alone. Seeking substance within emptiness, distinguishing bad from good. Spring wind and autumn moon - what else could there be? Bright eyes and white teeth - what can be done with such? Powder-white and dark-green brows, iron horse and golden spear. Clear song winding, subtle dance whirling. Swift as four-horse team, trailing like passing wave. Grieving one same track through past and present, while sun and moon forever wear away. Reflecting on creation, they make literature. Things divided by kind, gathered by type. Responding with jade cup to Taibai, turning praise and blame upside down. In tune with zithers and se, clear as sheng and huang. Lyrical and refined in lingering style, unbridled in Han and Zhuang modes. Neither bizarre nor chaotic, taking brilliance and fragrance. Intention upright, diction bright. Crimson clouds color the sky; golden blades flash in sunlight. Spring pours through gullies in urgency; flowers dance wild in wind. Autumn withers one courtyard, lingering fragrance a few stars. Ghosts weep by night, spirits startled by day. Casting tripods to image things, exhausting every form. Demon maidens stand nearby, dragon girls greet in front. Golden branches and kingfisher plumes, lightning and thunder sounding. Huo Xiaoyu brushes paper; Dong Shuangcheng presents inkstone. The divine maiden grants the brush and makes this text. We pray: The brush has five colors, its blossoms half-open; stones crash and boom like heaven's war drums; blue luan sings and purple phoenix dances; small words chanted and chanted suffice for a thousand ages."

When finished, all flower historians bowed together. Then they burned the dance robes, song fans, kingfisher plumes, and gold ornaments by Taihu rocks in the garden. The scholars watched five-colored firelight flashing gold and green. As the burning neared completion, suddenly a fragrant gust blew the ashes into midair, fluttering in specks against a red sun like countless flowers and butterflies dancing. Gold-drunk and paper-bewildered, fragrance filled the nose. The swirl climbed higher and higher until halfway up the sky, becoming ten thousand points of gold light, then vanished in one flash.

In the garden all blossoms seemed to smile, trembling as if about to speak. Truly:

"To meet in person the heavenly maiden scattering flowers,
then receive by hand one blossom-bearing brush.
Sea of sorrow in blue ocean still unfilled,
road to Penglai so far with no fixed term.
Dust-stained face and wheel-rut tracks,
Xu and Yu prose, Wen and Li verse.
I have my own feelings - ask me not;
gain and loss in this, only my inch-heart knows."
